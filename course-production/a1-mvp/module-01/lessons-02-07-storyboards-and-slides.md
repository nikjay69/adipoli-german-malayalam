## Lesson 02 — Why A1 Matters for Malayalis

1. **A1 is the first survival level**
   - Visual: Kerala learner opening app; one `Goethe A1` card.

   - Talking head: Boss gives short Manglish framing.

2. **Four exam skills**
   - Low-text table:
     - `Hören` — hear
     - `Sprechen` — speak
     - `Lesen` — read
     - `Schreiben` — write
   - Use icons: ear, mouth, eye, pen.

3. **Adipoli path**
   - Text only:
     ```text
     Watch → Listen → Say → Check → Fix
     ```
   - No full roadmap.

4. **First identity line**
   - Visual: notebook/phone input.
   - Text: `Ich lerne Deutsch.` / `I am learning German.`

   - Visual: five simple chips.
   - Text: `Goethe A1`, `Germany plan`, `Family`, `Work/Ausbildung`, `Confidence`.
   - Learner says one reason in English/Manglish.

6. **Must-do vs booster**
   - Two-card comparison.
   - Must-do: `answer 5 greetings`.

7. **Closed check**
   - Text: `No notes. Find the weak spot.`
   - Keep calm, not exam-scary.

## Lesson 03 — German Sounds for Malayalam Speakers

### Slide/scene sequence

1. **Clear, not native-perfect**
   - Text: `Clear A1 German. Not perfect accent.`
   - Visual: audio waveform around `Ich lerne Deutsch.`

2. **Soft `ch`**
   - Text: `ich ≠ isch`.
   - Visual: simple mouth cue; avoid medical diagram.
   - Practice: `ich`, `Ich lerne Deutsch.`

3. **`sch` only where written**
   - Table: `ich` vs `schön` vs `Tschüss`.
   - Audio prompt; learner contrasts.

4. **German `w`**
   - Text: `German w ≈ English v`.
   - Examples: `Frau Fischer`, `Wasser`, `Auf Wiedersehen`.

5. **German `v` chunk learning**
   - Table: `viel`, `Vater`, `Visum`, `Video`.
   - Note: learn common words as chunks.

6. **Umlaut cards**
   - Three cards: `ä`, `ö`, `ü`.
   - Examples: `spät`, `schön`, `Tschüss`.
   - No dense phonetics.

7. **Ten-line shadow set**
   - Use audio card + phrase card.
   - Show one line at a time.

8. **Closed pronunciation check**
   - Text: `Say or choose quickly.`

## Lesson 05 — Politeness Survival

### Slide/scene sequence

1. **Three-word survival hook**
   - Text only: `bitte`, `danke`, `Entschuldigung`.
   - Visual: learner at reception/exam desk.

2. **`danke` card**
   - Dialogue: `Frau Fischer: Sehr gut.` → `You: Danke.`

3. **`bitte` flexible card**
   - Three tiny rows: please / here you are / you’re welcome.
   - Example: `Noch einmal, bitte.`

4. **`Entschuldigung` chunk card**
   - Text: `Ent-schul-di-gung`.
   - Practice: slow repeat.

5. **Formal safer defaults**
   - Repair table:
     - `Hey!` → `Guten Tag.`
     - `Was?` → `Noch einmal, bitte.`
     - `Bye!` → `Auf Wiedersehen.`

6. **Goethe speaking mini-scene**
   - Greet, thank, leave.
   - One line at a time.

7. **Polite repair practice**
   - Situation → learner phrase.

8. **Closed check**
   - Five translations/uses.

## Lesson 06 — Goodbye and Repair Phrases

### Slide/scene sequence

1. **Fast German problem**
   - Frau Fischer line appears blurred/fast visually.
   - Learner uses `Noch einmal, bitte.`

2. **Formal goodbye**
   - Text: `Auf Wiedersehen.`
   - Chunk: `Auf Wie-der-se-hen`.

3. **Casual goodbye**
   - Table: teacher/office = `Auf Wiedersehen`; friend = `Tschüss`.

4. **Repeat request**
   - Text: `Noch einmal, bitte.`
   - Dialogue repeat scene.

5. **Slow down request**
   - Text: `Langsam, bitte.`
   - Audio first fast, then slow.

6. **Repair choice table**
   - Missed line → `Noch einmal, bitte.`
   - Too fast → `Langsam, bitte.`
   - Interrupt → `Entschuldigung.`

7. **Closed check**
   - Five prompt responses.

