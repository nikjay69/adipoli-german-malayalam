# Module 1 · Lesson 01 Video Script — Start Listening: `Guten Morgen`

Updated: 2026-06-10 (quality pass: honest length, Nivin thread, recording specs)

**Purpose:** This is the first owned MVP video. It must make the learner hear German, answer aloud, and feel: “I can actually do this.”

**Presenter language:** Boss records the main explanation later in Malayalam/Manglish. This script uses English stage directions plus exact German/on-screen text.

**Target length:** 15 minutes. Dense, no filler — every block earns its minutes.

**Recording spec (applies to every drill/pause in this script):**

- Every practice pause: model audio → `[PAUSE 3s]` silence → model audio again. Writing pauses: `[PAUSE 15s]`.
- All Frau Fischer / model German lines are pre-rendered German audio (never browser SpeechSynthesis). Boss reacts to playback; he does not voice the German models.
- Frau Fischer appears in a video-call frame or at the Goethe Kochi desk. Nivin and the learner are in the Kerala practice setting.

**Learner output by end:** Say and recognise: `Guten Morgen, Frau Fischer.` + `Ich lerne Deutsch.`

## 0:45–2:30 — What just happened?

**Teaching point:** `Guten Morgen` = Good morning. `Frau Fischer` is a polite way to address a woman by surname.

**Presenter:**

[Boss explains in Malayalam/Manglish: In Germany, first safe mode is respect. If you do not know someone, formal/polite is safer. This is useful in Goethe exam, office, visa office, language school, doctor, train counter.]

**On-screen micro-table:**

| German | Use | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Guten Morgen | morning | Good morning |
| Frau Fischer | polite address | Ms/Mrs Fischer |
| Hallo | casual/neutral | Hello |

**Say-aloud drill:**

1. **Guten Morgen.**
2. **Guten Morgen, Frau Fischer.**
3. **Hallo.**

## 4:30–7:30 — Greeting by time

**Teaching point:** German has common greetings by time. For A1, learn the useful set, not every regional variation.

**On-screen table:**

| Time/context | German | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Morning | Guten Morgen | Good morning |
| Day/formal | Guten Tag | Good day/hello |
| Evening | Guten Abend | Good evening |
| Casual | Hallo | Hello |
| Leaving | Auf Wiedersehen | Goodbye |

**Presenter:**

[Boss explains in Malayalam/Manglish: `Gute Nacht` is when someone is going to sleep. Do not use it as a normal evening hello.]

**Practice pause:**

Listen/read → say the reply:

1. Teacher at 9 AM: **Guten Morgen.** → learner: **Guten Morgen, Frau Fischer.**
2. Office at 2 PM: **Guten Tag.** → learner: **Guten Tag.**
3. Leaving class: **Auf Wiedersehen.** → learner: **Auf Wiedersehen.**

---

## 7:30–9:30 — The first sentence: `Ich lerne Deutsch.`

**Teaching point:** The learner needs one identity sentence from day one.

**On-screen:**

```text
Ich lerne Deutsch.
I am learning German.
```

**Presenter:**

[Boss explains in Malayalam/Manglish: `Ich` = I. `lerne` = learn/am learning. `Deutsch` = German language. Do not confuse `Deutsch` with `Deutschland` — Germany the country.]

**Pronunciation guide:**

- **Ich** — soft `ch`, not “ish”.
- **lerne** — two syllables: `ler-ne`.
- **Deutsch** — roughly `doych`.

**Practice pause:**

Learner repeats three times:

> **Ich lerne Deutsch.**

---

## 9:30–11:30 — Common beginner mistakes and repair

**Mistake 1:** using the wrong time greeting.

Wrong:

> **Gute Nacht** at 6 PM when meeting someone.

Better:

> **Guten Abend.**

**Mistake 2:** answering too casually in a formal setting.

Risky:

> **Hey!**

Safer:

> **Guten Tag.**

**Mistake 3:** pronouncing `Ich` as `Ish` every time.

Repair:

> Try a softer sound: **Ich**.

**Presenter:**

[Boss explains in Malayalam/Manglish: We are not aiming for perfect accent today. We are aiming for understandable and confident.]

---

## 11:30–13:30 — Guided first conversation

**Visual:** Nivin's first live exchange: Frau Fischer in the video-call frame, Nivin at his Kochi desk sitting up straight like it is a real class. The learner performs Nivin's lines.

**Dialogue on screen:**

```text
Frau Fischer: Guten Morgen.
Nivin: Guten Morgen, Frau Fischer.
Frau Fischer: Lernen Sie Deutsch?
Nivin: Ja. Ich lerne Deutsch.
Frau Fischer: Sehr gut.
```

**A1 note:** `Lernen Sie Deutsch?` means “Are you learning German?” We do not need grammar depth yet. Just recognise the question.

**Practice:**

Presenter plays/reads Frau Fischer lines. Learner answers aloud:

1. **Guten Morgen.** → **Guten Morgen, Frau Fischer.**
2. **Lernen Sie Deutsch?** → **Ja. Ich lerne Deutsch.**
3. **Auf Wiedersehen.** → **Auf Wiedersehen.**

---

## 13:30–14:30 — Closed mini-test

**Instruction on screen:**

```text
Closed check: no Google, no notes.
This is only to know what to fix.
```

**Test items:**

2. Choose: 9 AM greeting — `Guten Morgen` / `Gute Nacht`.
3. Say: **Ich lerne Deutsch.**
4. Match: `Deutsch` = language or country?
5. What do you say when leaving? **Auf Wiedersehen.**

**Presenter:**

[Boss explains in Malayalam/Manglish: If you miss it, good. The app will show the exact recovery task. Weakness is data, not shame.]
