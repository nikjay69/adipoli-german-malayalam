# Module 1 Checkpoint Rubric — First German Moment

Updated: 2026-06-02 11:05 CEST

## Checkpoint purpose

This is a closed-book diagnostic, not a punishment. It tells the app/teacher which exact recovery task the learner needs before Module 2.

Instruction shown to learner:

```text
Closed check: no Google, no YouTube, no ChatGPT, no dictionary, no notes.
This is only to find what to fix.
```

## Pass rule

Minimum pass:

- Total: 70%+
- Must not fail both Hören and Sprechen.
- Must complete at least one spoken reply aloud/self-marked.

Weak pass:

- 60–69%, or
- one required tag is weak but not failed.

Fail/recovery gate:

- below 60%, or
- fails `hoeren:greetings`, or
- cannot produce any spoken greeting reply.

## Test sections

### 1. Hören — greetings and response choice

**Format:** play/read 5 lines. Learner chooses or says the correct response.

Items:

1. `Guten Morgen.` → expected safe reply: `Guten Morgen.` / `Guten Morgen, Frau Weber.`
2. `Guten Tag.` → `Guten Tag.`
3. `Guten Abend.` → `Guten Abend.`
4. `Auf Wiedersehen.` → `Auf Wiedersehen.`
5. `Lernen Sie Deutsch?` → `Ja. Ich lerne Deutsch.`

Scoring: 10 points.

Weakness tags:

- `hoeren:greetings`
- `hoeren:question_recognition`
- `vocab:greeting_set`

### 2. Sprechen — first reply

**Task:** Say aloud:

```text
Guten Morgen, Frau Weber.
Ich lerne Deutsch.
Auf Wiedersehen.
```

Manual/self scoring, 10 points:

- 4 = says all three chunks without freezing.
- 2 = mostly understandable pronunciation.
- 2 = uses formal/polite tone where needed.
- 2 = can repeat after correction.

Weakness tags:

- `sprechen:greeting_reply`
- `sprechen:formality`
- `pronunciation:ch_sch`
- `pronunciation:w_v`

### 3. Lesen — recognise simple lines

**Task:** Match German to meaning.

| German | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Guten Morgen | Good morning |
| Guten Tag | Good day/hello |
| Auf Wiedersehen | Goodbye |
| Ich lerne Deutsch | I am learning German |
| Deutsch | German language |

Scoring: 10 points.

Weakness tags:

- `lesen:greeting_recognition`
- `vocab:greeting_set`

### 4. Schreiben — first sentence

**Task:** Type/write from memory:

```text
Ich lerne Deutsch.
```

Scoring: 5 points:

- 2 = `Ich` present and capitalised.
- 1 = `lerne` correct enough.
- 1 = `Deutsch` correct and capitalised.
- 1 = sentence understandable as a whole.

Weakness tags:

- `schreiben:first_sentence`
- `grammar:capitalisation_basics`

### 5. Grammar/vocab micro-check

**Items:**

1. `Deutsch` is the language; `Deutschland` is the country. True/false.
2. Which is safer with a teacher/examiner: `Hallo` or `Guten Tag`?
3. When do you use `Gute Nacht`? Going to sleep, not normal evening hello.
4. What does `Ich` mean?
5. What does `lernen` mean?

Scoring: 10 points.

Weakness tags:

- `vocab:deutsch_vs_deutschland`
- `grammar:formal_context`
- `vocab:first_sentence_chunks`

## Recovery prescriptions

### `hoeren:greetings`

Must-do recovery, 12m:

1. Replay 10 greeting audio lines.
2. Choose correct response without subtitles.
3. Retest 5 lines; pass 4/5 to continue.

Score booster, 15m:

- Slow greeting audio shadowing set, 20 lines.

### `sprechen:greeting_reply`

Must-do recovery, 10m:

1. Repeat `Guten Morgen, Frau Weber.` 5 times.
2. Repeat `Ja. Ich lerne Deutsch.` 5 times.
3. Self-record or say aloud once without looking.
4. Retest with one teacher prompt.

Score booster, 10m:

- Goethe-style examiner greeting card: answer formally three ways.

### `sprechen:formality`

Must-do recovery, 8m:

1. Watch/review the formal vs casual micro-card.
2. Sort 8 contexts into formal/casual.
3. Retest 4 contexts.

### `pronunciation:ch_sch`

Must-do recovery, 15m:

1. Shadow: `ich`, `Deutsch`, `Tschüss`, `schön`.
2. Identify `ich` vs `isch` in 8 lines.
3. Retest 5 words.

Score booster, 12m:

- One curated public pronunciation video for `ch/sch`; output is 8 recorded words.

### `pronunciation:w_v`

Must-do recovery, 10m:

1. Compare `Wasser`, `Weber`, `Visum`, `viel`.
2. Say German `w` as English `v`.
3. Retest 6 words.

### `schreiben:first_sentence`

Must-do recovery, 8m:

1. Copy `Ich lerne Deutsch.` twice.
2. Hide it and type from memory.
3. Retest one dictation.

### `vocab:greeting_set`

Must-do recovery, 10m:

1. Match 8 greeting/politeness words.
2. Use 4 in mini-dialogue.
3. Retest matching 6 words.

## Manual scoring notes for MVP

- Speaking can be self-marked first, then teacher-reviewed for paid/cohort versions.
- Writing can be manually coded by tag; AI can suggest tags later but is not required.
- The result page should say one next action, not show a complex analytics dashboard.

Example result:

```text
Weak spot: Hören — greetings.
Do: replay 10 greeting lines, then retest 5.
Time: 12m.
```
