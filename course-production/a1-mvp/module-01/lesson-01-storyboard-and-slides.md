## Slide/scene sequence

### Scene 1 — First sound, not first lecture

- Visual: Kerala learner at desk, phone/laptop open.
- Audio: Frau Weber says `Guten Morgen.`
- On-screen text: `Frau Weber: Guten Morgen.`
- Boss: no long intro; just invite repeat.

### Scene 2 — Learner reply

- Visual: same learner, highlighted reply bubble.
- On-screen text: `You: Guten Morgen, Frau Weber.`
- Action: pause for learner to say it aloud.

### Scene 3 — Meaning micro-table

| German | Meaning | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Guten Morgen | Good morning | morning |
| Frau Weber | Ms/Mrs Weber | polite address |
| Hallo | Hello | casual/neutral |

- Boss explains in Malayalam/Manglish.
- Keep table visible for max 30–45 seconds.

### Scene 4 — The Adipoli learning loop

On-screen:

```text
Watch → Listen → Say → Check → Fix
```

- Visual: five clean icons, not game badges.
- Voice: “No resource dumping. We guide exactly what to do.”

### Scene 5 — Greeting by time

- Visual: simple day timeline: morning/day/evening/leaving.
- Text:
  - Morning: `Guten Morgen`
  - Day/formal: `Guten Tag`
  - Evening: `Guten Abend`
  - Leaving: `Auf Wiedersehen`

### Scene 6 — Wrong vs better

- Visual: split card.
- Left: `Gute Nacht` at a doorway ❌
- Right: `Guten Abend` ✅
- Boss explains: `Gute Nacht` is for going to sleep.

### Scene 7 — First sentence

- Visual: learner notebook with one line.
- On-screen:

```text
Ich lerne Deutsch.
I am learning German.
```

- Boss explains word chunks.

### Scene 8 — Pronunciation close-up

- Visual: simple mouth/phonetic cue, not medical diagram.
- Text:
  - `Ich` ≠ `Ish`
  - `Deutsch` ≈ `doych`
- Action: repeat three times.

### Scene 9 — First tiny dialogue

Dialogue appears one line at a time:

```text
Frau Weber: Guten Morgen.
Learner: Guten Morgen, Frau Weber.
Frau Weber: Lernen Sie Deutsch?
Learner: Ja. Ich lerne Deutsch.
```

- Use audio turns if possible.
- Highlight the current speaker.

### Scene 10 — Closed check

On-screen:

```text
Closed check.
No notes. No Google.
Find the weak spot, then fix it.
```

- Keep this calm, not exam-scary.

### Scene 11 — Recap card

Text:

```text
You can say:
Guten Morgen, Frau Weber.
Ich lerne Deutsch.
Auf Wiedersehen.
```

- Boss: short encouragement.

### Scene 12 — Next action

Button text:

```text
Practice: answer Frau Weber
```

## Phone-friendly editing notes

- Keep every practice scene to one visible German line when possible.
- Tables should appear briefly and collapse back to the current phrase.
- Use large subtitle text for Frau Weber / learner turns.
- Do not show the full dialogue and the word table at the same time on mobile.
- Maximum one German dialogue line visible at a time during practice.
- Tables are allowed only for comparison, not for dense grammar.
- No feature grids, badges, game tiles, or big course roadmap in Lesson 1.
- Use Malayalam/Manglish in Boss narration, not in dense on-screen paragraphs.
- On-screen German must be exact and proofread; never trust AI-image text.
