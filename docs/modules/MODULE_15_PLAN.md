# Module 15: German Culture & Integration (Kultur)

## Teaching Goal
After this module, students should be able to **recognize key German social norms** (punctuality, recycling, quiet hours), **understand major festivals**, **compare German and Kerala customs**, and **identify common colloquial expressions**. This is the cultural integration module — helping students avoid social friction and understand context.

## A1 Exam Relevance

| Exam Section | Relevance |
|---|---|
| **Lesen/Hören** | Cultural context improves interpretation of everyday German texts and situations. Indirect but real. |
| **Sprechen** | Helps students respond more naturally and appropriately in conversation tasks. |
| **Indirect** | Supports real-world success more than direct exam mechanics. Lowest exam priority among core modules. |

## Essential Outcomes
Students must be able to **produce** (not just recognize):
1. Explain 3-4 key German social norms (Pünktlichkeit, Mülltrennung, Ruhezeit, Sonntagsruhe)
2. Name major German festivals and their approximate timing
3. Compare at least 3 differences between Kerala and German culture
4. Recognize (but not necessarily produce) 5-6 common slang expressions
5. Interpret everyday behavior with less cultural shock

## Future Script Direction

### Suggested Narrative Approach
- Warm, observational, sometimes funny: "The first time you hear 'Ruhezeit' you'll think they're joking. They're NOT."
- Cultural contrast should be helpful, never mocking — celebrate both cultures
- Focus on the things that ACTUALLY surprise newcomers from Kerala
- Slang should be treated as "understand it when you hear it, don't use it at the office"

### Malayalam Bridge Points
- **Pünktlichkeit vs IST** — "In Kerala, 'I'll be there at 5' means 5:30-6:00. In Germany, 5:00 means 4:55."
- **Mülltrennung** — "Four different bins! Kerala is still figuring out one bin, and Germans have FOUR."
- **Sonntagsruhe** — "No washing machine on Sunday? No drilling? No lawn mowing? Mind = blown for Malayalis."
- **Directness** — "Germans say what they mean. No 'Ippo vaaam... pinne paraayaam' style hints."
- **Festival parallels** — Weihnachten preparation ≈ Onam preparation (both involve food, decoration, family)
- **Food shock** — "German food is... different. Don't worry, Indian stores exist in every city!"

### Key Example Sentences/Dialogues to Feature

```
Cultural Rules:

Slang (comprehension only):

"Krass!" = "Wow! / Crazy!"

```
