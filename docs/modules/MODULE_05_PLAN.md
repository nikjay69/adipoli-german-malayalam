# Module 05: Daily Routine (Mein Tag)

## Teaching Goal
After this module, students should be able to **conjugate common regular verbs in present tense**, **use separable verbs correctly**, and **describe their daily routine and weekly schedule** in connected sentences. This is the grammar turning point where students go from phrase-memorizers to sentence-builders.

## A1 Exam Relevance

| Exam Section | Relevance |
|---|---|
| **Sprechen** | Daily routine is one of the most standard A1 conversation topics. "Beschreiben Sie Ihren Tag." |
| **Schreiben** | Short messages about daily activities and schedules. "Ich stehe um 7 Uhr auf." |
| **Hören** | Understanding routines and schedules in dialogues and messages. |
| **Grammar** | Present tense verb conjugation is the backbone of ALL A1 communication — this module makes or breaks fluency. |

## Essential Outcomes
Students must be able to **produce** (not just recognize):
1. Conjugate any regular verb correctly for all persons (ich mache, du machst, er macht...)
2. Use separable verbs in correct position: "Ich stehe um 7 Uhr auf."
3. Describe their morning routine in 4-5 connected sentences with correct verb forms
4. Narrate a full day from morning to night using time expressions
5. Talk about weekly habits using frequency words (immer, oft, manchmal)
6. Build original sentences with learned verbs (not just repeat memorized phrases)

## Future Script Direction

### Suggested Narrative Approach
- Frame this as "the grammar turning point" — "After this module, you can BUILD sentences, not just repeat them"
- Use a relatable "day in my life" narrative format
- Heavy pattern drilling: show the logic, then repeat until automatic
- Separable verbs need a memorable visual metaphor: "The prefix is like a boomerang — it always comes back at the end!"

### Malayalam Bridge Points
- **Verb conjugation concept** — Malayalam verbs change form too (ഞാൻ ചെയ്യുന്നു, നീ ചെയ്യുന്നു); the concept isn't alien, just the pattern is different
- **Routine comparisons** — Kerala morning routine (chai, newspaper, temple) vs German routine (Kaffee, Zeitung, joggen)
- **Time discipline** — "In Germany, 'Ich stehe um 6 Uhr auf' means EXACTLY 6:00, not 6:30-ish"
- **Separable verbs have no Malayalam parallel** — acknowledge this is genuinely new and needs extra practice
- **Weekly rhythm** — compare Kerala work/worship week with German Arbeitswoche/Wochenende

### Key Example Sentences/Dialogues to Feature
```
Morning Routine:
"Ich stehe um sechs Uhr auf.
Dann dusche ich und frühstücke.
Um halb acht fahre ich zur Arbeit.
Ich arbeite von acht bis fünf Uhr."

Full Day:
"Morgens stehe ich früh auf.
Mittags esse ich in der Kantine.
Nachmittags lerne ich Deutsch.
Abends koche ich und sehe fern.
Um elf Uhr gehe ich ins Bett."

Weekly Habits:
"Am Montag gehe ich zum Deutschkurs.
Am Mittwoch spiele ich Fußball.
Am Wochenende kaufe ich ein und koche.
Ich lerne jeden Tag ein bisschen Deutsch."
```
