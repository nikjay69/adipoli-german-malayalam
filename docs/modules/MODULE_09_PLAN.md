# Module 09: Travel & Directions (Reisen und Wege)

## Teaching Goal
After this module, students should be able to **navigate public transport**, **buy tickets**, **ask for and understand directions**, **handle airport and train station interactions**, and **use modal verbs** (können, müssen, dürfen, wollen, sollen, mögen) in travel contexts. Essential survival module for anyone arriving in Germany.

## A1 Exam Relevance

| Exam Section | Relevance |
|---|---|
| **Hören** | Station announcements, route information, and travel exchanges are high-frequency listening content. |
| **Lesen** | Timetables, signs, transport information, ticket machines, and platform displays. |
| **Sprechen** | Asking directions, buying tickets, and asking travel questions are practical interaction tasks. |
| **Grammar** | Modal verbs + infinitive structure is a major A1 grammar milestone. |

## Essential Outcomes
Students must be able to **produce** (not just recognize):
1. Name common transport types and say how they travel: "Ich fahre mit der U-Bahn"
2. Buy a ticket: "Eine Fahrkarte nach München, bitte. Hin und zurück."
3. Ask for directions: "Entschuldigung, wie komme ich zum Bahnhof?"
4. Understand simple directions: "Gehen Sie geradeaus, dann links."
5. Navigate basic airport/station vocabulary (Gleis, Abflug, Gate)
6. Use modal verbs in sentences: "Ich kann Deutsch sprechen." / "Du musst umsteigen."

## Future Script Direction

### Suggested Narrative Approach
- Scene-based and practical: "Your first day in Germany — you just landed at Frankfurt Airport"
- Build confidence step by step: airport → station → train → city → destination
- Modal verbs should feel like power-up abilities: "Now you can say what you CAN, MUST, and WANT to do!"
- Include real-life tips: Deutschland-Ticket, DB Navigator app, Sparpreise

### Malayalam Bridge Points
- **KSRTC experience → Deutsche Bahn** — "If you survived Kerala buses, German trains are luxury!"
- **Auto-rickshaw directions → street directions** — same concept, different vocabulary
- **"Evideyaanu?" → "Wo ist...?"** — exact same function
- **Train culture** — Germany's trains run on time (mostly); the schedule is sacred
- **Bargaining vs fixed prices** — "Tickets have ONE price. No negotiation at the Schalter!"
