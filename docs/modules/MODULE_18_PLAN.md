# Module 18: Goethe A1 Exam — Schreiben & Sprechen

## Teaching Goal
After this module, students should be able to **fill out key form fields quickly and accurately**, **write short A1 messages hitting all required content points**, **deliver a stable self-introduction**, **ask and answer simple topic-card questions**, **make polite requests**, and **complete a full A1-style productive mock test with less anxiety**. This is the final conversion layer from “I know German” to “I can pass the exam.”

## A1 Exam Relevance

## Essential Outcomes
Students must be able to **produce**:
1. Fill key form fields accurately and quickly under time pressure
2. Write a short A1 message that hits all required content points without overwriting
3. Deliver a stable 5-7 sentence self-introduction confidently
4. Ask and answer basic topic-card questions in simple German
5. Make polite requests appropriately in everyday scenarios
6. Complete a full productive mock with reduced anxiety and fewer avoidable mistakes

## Gap Analysis
- **Still too little true free production in the wider course** — this module carries too much of the burden alone
- **No speaking exercise type in code** despite the module being Sprechen-heavy
- **Needs the richest correction/scoring layer in the whole course** — rubrics, acceptable answer bands, error feedback
- **Should integrate tightly with `/practice/write` and `/practice/intro`** so exam prep becomes a repeatable habit, not one-off exposure
- **Needs randomization for topic cards and request scenarios** to prevent memorization-only prep
- **Fill-blank still dominates writing prep** — more free-text production is needed

## Future Script Direction

### Suggested Narrative Approach
- Laser-focused, confidence-building, exam-day practical
- Reassure students constantly: short, correct, safe German beats fancy German
- Use templates, routines, and controlled sentence banks
- Every lesson should answer: “What exactly do I do in the exam?”

### Malayalam Bridge Points
- Address common Malayali learner tendencies directly:
  - trying to say too much
  - translating from English under pressure
  - forgetting one required content point in writing
  - freezing when asked an unexpected question
- Emphasize exam survival wisdom: clarity > complexity, calm > perfection
- Connect back to familiar modules so students feel they are reusing known material, not learning something new at the last minute
