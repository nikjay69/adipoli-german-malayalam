# Module 08: My Home (Meine Wohnung)

## Teaching Goal
After this module, students should be able to **name rooms and furniture**, **describe where objects are using prepositions**, **understand apartment ads**, and **write a simple housing-related message**. Covers practical housing vocabulary essential for any new arrival in Germany.

## A1 Exam Relevance

| Exam Section | Relevance |
|---|---|
| **Lesen** | Apartment ads and housing notices are CLASSIC A1 reading material. Abbreviations (Zi, EG, NK, KM, WM) appear frequently. |
| **Schreiben** | Simple notes or messages about housing situations (e.g., responding to an ad, writing to a landlord). |
| **Sprechen** | Describing where you live and your room/home is a common conversation topic. |
| **Grammar** | Location prepositions (in, auf, unter, neben, vor, hinter) build broader spatial communication. |

## Essential Outcomes
Students must be able to **produce** (not just recognize):
1. Name the main rooms in a home with correct articles
2. Name at least 10 common furniture items
3. Describe where objects are: "Der Tisch ist in der Küche." / "Die Lampe steht auf dem Tisch."
4. Read and understand a basic apartment ad (including abbreviations)
5. Describe their home or room in 3-4 sentences
6. Write a short message responding to a housing ad or to a landlord/flatmate

## Future Script Direction

### Suggested Narrative Approach
- Use labeled room visuals and "take a virtual tour" format
- Make apartment hunting feel like an adventure, not bureaucracy: "Your first WG in Germany — exciting!"
- Prepositions need spatial demonstration, not just text explanation
- Message writing should use real templates students can immediately adapt

### Malayalam Bridge Points
- **Kerala independent houses vs German flats/WGs** — "No more separate kitchen outhouse — everything is inside!"
- **Joint living** — WG (Wohngemeinschaft) concept maps loosely to Kerala hostel/PG culture
- **Kayyalikkunna vs Miete** — different rental concepts but same stress level!
- **Balkon culture** — Germans love their balcony like Malayalis love their veranda/thinna
- **Anmeldung surprise** — "You MUST register your address within 2 weeks of moving in — no exceptions!"
