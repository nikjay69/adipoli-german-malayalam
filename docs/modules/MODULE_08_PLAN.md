## Wohnungsanzeigen Abbreviation System

This is THE high-leverage exam content in Module 8:

| Abbreviation | Full form | Meaning |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **Zi** | Zimmer | rooms |
| **m²** / **qm** | Quadratmeter | square meters |
| **KM** | Kaltmiete | cold rent (without heating) |
| **WM** | Warmmiete | warm rent (with heating) |
| **NK** | Nebenkosten | additional costs |
| **EG** | Erdgeschoss | ground floor |
| **OG** | Obergeschoss | upper floor |
| **DG** | Dachgeschoss | attic floor |
| **Blk** | Balkon | balcony |
| **EBK** | Einbauküche | fitted kitchen |
| **TG** | Tiefgarage | underground garage |
| **WG** | Wohngemeinschaft | shared flat |
| **AB** | Altbau | old building |
| **NB** | Neubau | new building |
| **KT** | Kaution | deposit |
| **provisionsfrei** | — | no agent fee |

### Sample Wohnungsanzeige (Exam-style)
```
Schöne 2-Zi-Wohnung in Kreuzberg
55 m², 3. OG, Blk, EBK, AB
KM: 650 €, NK: 150 €, KT: 2 MM
Ab 01.04. frei. Provisionsfrei.
Kontakt: wohnung@beispiel.de
```

**Students must be able to decode this in under 2 minutes for Lesen.**

## Common Malayali Learner Errors to Script Against

1. **Preposition + article combo confusion:** "in der Küche" (dative) vs "in die Küche" (accusative for movement) — at A1, teach mostly dative (location) and flag the difference without deep explanation
2. **Abbreviation blindness:** Students read "2-Zi-Whg, 3. OG, EBK" and freeze — MUST drill abbreviation decoding
3. **KM vs WM confusion:** Thinking Kaltmiete is the total cost → surprise extra Nebenkosten → budget disaster
4. **Floor numbering:** "3. OG" = 4th floor (American), not 3rd — EG is ground floor, 1. OG is first above
5. **Article confusion with rooms/furniture:** das Zimmer, die Küche, der Schrank — no consistent gender pattern, must memorize
6. **Preposition defaults to "in":** Using "in" for everything — "in dem Tisch" instead of "auf dem Tisch"
7. **Missing Kaution concept:** Deposit of 2-3 months rent is standard — not understanding "KT: 2 MM" (2 Monatsmieten)
8. **Writing too formal or too casual:** Ad response should be formal (Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren) but not stiff

## Malayalam Bridge Points

- **House vs flat:** "In Kerala, most people live in a 'veedu' (house). In Germany, most people live in a 'Wohnung' (flat/apartment). Haus is a whole building."
- **Floor confusion:** "In Kerala, ground floor is ground floor. In Germany, Erdgeschoss (EG) = ground floor. 1. OG = first floor UP. If they say '3. OG', climb 3 flights!"
- **Shared flats (WG):** "WG culture is huge in Germany — sharing a flat with strangers is normal, especially for students. Like a hostel but permanent. WG-Casting (flatmate interviews) is a thing!"
- **Renting culture:** "In Germany, most people rent. Owning is less common than in Kerala. Renting is not 'temporary' — people rent for decades."
- **Abbreviation survival:** "German loves abbreviations. An apartment ad looks like a code: '2-Zi-Whg, 3. OG, EBK, Blk, KM 650€'. By the end of this module, you can crack that code in 30 seconds."
- **Kerala homes vs German flats:** "No central courtyard (nadumuttam), but you get a Balkon. No pooja room, but you might get a Abstellraum (storage room). Adapt the vocabulary, keep the feeling of home."
