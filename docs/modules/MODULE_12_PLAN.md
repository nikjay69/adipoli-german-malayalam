# Module 12: Hobbies & Free Time (Freizeit)

## Teaching Goal
After this module, students should be able to **talk about hobbies and interests**, **describe weather and seasons**, **make plans with friends**, and **write and respond to invitations**. This is the social survival module — building connections and having a life beyond work/study in Germany.

## A1 Exam Relevance

| Exam Section | Relevance |
|---|---|
| **Sprechen** | Free-time interests are common personal conversation topics in Sprechen Teil 1 and 2. |
| **Schreiben** | Invitations and responses are CLASSIC A1 message-writing territory (Schreiben Teil 2). |
| **Hören** | Plans, weather, and social arrangement dialogues are common listening topics. |
| **Lesen** | Event notices, activity descriptions, and invitation messages in reading tasks. |

## Essential Outcomes
Students must be able to **produce** (not just recognize):
1. Say what they like doing: "Ich koche gern" / "Ich spiele gern Fußball"
2. Describe basic weather: "Heute regnet es" / "Es ist warm und sonnig"
3. Invite someone: "Hast du am Samstag Zeit? Wollen wir ins Kino gehen?"
4. Accept an invitation: "Ja, gern! Um wie viel Uhr?"
5. Decline with a reason: "Leider kann ich nicht. Ich muss arbeiten."
6. Write a short invitation or excuse message (Schreiben Teil 2 format)

## Future Script Direction

### Suggested Narrative Approach
- Lighter, socially warm tone — "This is the fun module! Making friends in Germany!"
- Invitation writing should feel like WhatsApp messaging, not essay writing
- Weather is background context, not the main event — keep it brief
- Focus on the social interaction loop: invite → accept/decline → make plan → confirm

### Malayalam Bridge Points
- **Kerala hobbies** — Cricket, football (Malabar football culture!), cooking, film watching map directly
- **"Nammuku pokaaam!" → "Wollen wir gehen?"** — same social impulse
- **Weather shock** — "Kerala: 30°C and humid. Germany: -5°C and snowing. Es ist KALT!"
- **Festival invitations** — Onam/Vishu invitation culture parallels German party invitations
- **weil reasoning** — Malayalam also gives reasons naturally; the structure just moves the verb
