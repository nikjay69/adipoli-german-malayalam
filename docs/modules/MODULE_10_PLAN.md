# Module 10: Health & Body (Gesundheit)

## Teaching Goal
After this module, students should be able to **name major body parts**, **describe symptoms and how they feel**, **handle a basic doctor visit**, **ask for medicine at a pharmacy**, and **respond to simple emergency questions**. Especially relevant for the large number of Malayali nurses and healthcare workers heading to Germany.

## A1 Exam Relevance

| Exam Section | Relevance |
|---|---|
| **Hören** | Symptom dialogues, doctor instructions, and pharmacy exchanges appear in listening tasks. |
| **Lesen** | Health forms, pharmacy signs, simple medical notices. |
| **Sprechen** | Describing how you feel is a common practical A1 task and conversation topic. |
| **Schreiben** | Messages canceling appointments or explaining absence due to illness. |

## Essential Outcomes
Students must be able to **produce** (not just recognize):
1. Name at least 15 major body parts with correct articles
2. Say how they feel: "Mir geht es nicht gut. Ich habe Kopfschmerzen."
3. Describe symptoms to a doctor: "Ich habe seit zwei Tagen Fieber und Husten."
4. Ask for medicine: "Haben Sie etwas gegen Halsschmerzen?"
5. Know and say emergency numbers: "Rufen Sie die 112!"
6. Write a simple sick note message: "Ich bin krank und kann heute nicht kommen."

## Future Script Direction

### Suggested Narrative Approach
- Strong empathy and realism: health situations can be stressful in a foreign language
- Build confidence: "You don't need perfect German to see a doctor — these 20 phrases are enough"
- Include nursing/healthcare worker relevance as a motivation hook
- Emergency lesson should be brief but memorable — "Memorize 112 like you memorize your phone number"

### Malayalam Bridge Points
- **Healthcare workers** — "Thousands of Malayali nurses work in Germany — this module is YOUR professional vocabulary too!"
- **Ayurvedic vs Western medicine** — brief cultural comparison to keep engagement
- **"Doctor vilichaal pokanam" → "Ich muss zum Arzt gehen"** — universal experience
- **Pharmacy culture** — "In Germany, the Apotheke is the ONLY place for medicine — no medical shops on every corner like Kerala"
- **Health insurance** — "Krankenversicherung is mandatory in Germany — you literally can't live here without it"
