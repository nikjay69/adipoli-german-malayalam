# Module 03: Numbers & Time (Zahlen und Zeit)

## Teaching Goal
After this module, students should be able to **count confidently 0-100**, **tell and understand clock times**, **say days/months/dates/birthdays**, **understand prices and phone numbers**, and **make simple appointments**. Numbers and time are high-frequency A1 survival skills that appear across all four exam sections.

## A1 Exam Relevance

| Exam Section | Relevance |
|---|---|
| **Hören** | CRITICAL — phone numbers, prices, times, dates, and appointment details are core Hören material. This is where most A1 listening errors happen. |
| **Lesen** | Schedules, opening hours, calendars, ads with prices, event notices all require number/time literacy. |
| **Schreiben** | Date fields on forms (Geburtsdatum), appointment information, time references in messages. |
| **Sprechen** | Answering age, birthday, time, schedule, and appointment questions. |

## Essential Outcomes
Students must be able to **produce** (not just recognize):
1. Count from 0 to 100 without hesitation
2. Say any two-digit number correctly (including the reversed structure)
3. Tell the current time using both formal and informal patterns
4. Correctly interpret "halb drei" as 2:30 (not 3:30)
5. Say their birthday in German date format
6. Understand and say prices in Euro and Cent
7. Make a simple appointment: "Am Montag um 10 Uhr? Ja, das passt."
8. Write today's date in German format

## Future Script Direction

### Suggested Narrative Approach
- Use call-and-response rhythm heavily: "Ich sage die Zahl, du wiederholst!"
- Make the reversed number system feel logical, not scary: "Think of it as 'four-and-twenty' — like old English!"
- Treat time-telling as a survival skill: "Miss your train and you miss your appointment at the Ausländerbehörde!"
- Build speed gradually: slow recognition → fast recognition → production

### Malayalam Bridge Points
- **Number reversal** — Malayalam already has complex number words; German reversal is just a different pattern to memorize
- **12-hour vs 24-hour** — Indian Railways uses 24-hour format too, so formal German time isn't totally foreign
- **Date format** — Indian format (DD/MM/YYYY) matches German format (TT.MM.JJJJ) — advantage over Americans!
- **"Sugham aano?" scheduling** — appointment culture may be less formal in Kerala but the concept is identical
- **Price literacy** — comparing Euro prices to Rupees gives students real-world anchoring
