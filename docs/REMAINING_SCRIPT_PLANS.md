### v2-1-2 — What's Your Name? Part 2 (Module 2, Wave 1)

**Gap:** v2-1-1 covers the core name exchange. v2-1-2 extends to spelling your name, handling unusual names, and form-filling with names.

**Teaching goal:** After this video, learners can spell their name using the German alphabet, handle "Wie schreibt man das?" confidently, and fill name fields on official forms.

**Duration:** ~10 min

**Key narration lines to write:**

1. **Hook (0:15–1:00):**
   "Ivide Germany-il ninne aadhyam chodikunna kaaryam entha? Ninte peru! But just SAYING your name isn't enough — they'll ask you to SPELL it. 'Buchstabieren Sie bitte!' Ini nammal athum padikkaam."

2. **The spelling request (1:00–2:30):**
   - "Wie heißen Sie?" → "Ich heiße Nikhil Jayanth."
   - "Wie schreibt man das?" / "Können Sie das buchstabieren?"
   - "N-I-K-H-I-L. J-A-Y-A-N-T-H."
   - Practice with 4 common Malayali names: Arun, Sreelakshmi, Vishnu, Deepa

3. **Problem names (2:30–4:00):**
   - "German people WILL struggle with 'Sreelakshmi' — that's okay!"
   - Strategy: "Spell slowly, repeat if asked, and DON'T get frustrated"
   - Teach: "Noch einmal, bitte" (once more, please)
   - Cultural note: many immigrants use a shortened name at work

4. **Name on forms (4:00–6:00):**
   - Vorname = first name (literally "pre-name")
   - Nachname / Familienname = last name
   - Geburtsname = birth name (maiden name)
   - "In Kerala, naming conventions differ — father's name, house name, etc. German forms want VORNAME + NACHNAME. Keep it simple."

5. **Form-filling drill (6:00–8:00):**
   - Show a registration form: Vorname, Nachname, Geburtsdatum, Adresse
   - Fill it live with a Malayali example name
   - Highlight date format: DD.MM.YYYY (NOT MM/DD)

6. **Listening + writing (8:00–9:30):**
   - Audio plays someone spelling their name → student writes it
   - 3 practice names at increasing speed

7. **Closing challenge (9:30–10:00):**
   "Spell your full name in German 5 times today. Make it automatic. In the exam, in the Amt, on the phone — this is a skill you'll use EVERY WEEK in Germany."

**Malayalam bridges:**
- Compare Kerala naming complexity (house name + father's name + personal name) with German simplicity
- "Nammude perukal spelling cheyyan budhimuttanu — but practice cheythal easy aakum!"

**Exam tip to embed:**
"In Goethe Sprechen Teil 1, the examiner may ask 'Buchstabieren Sie Ihren Namen, bitte.' Have your spelling ready — don't fumble!"

**Dependencies:** Builds on v2-1-1 (name exchange), connects to v14-1-1 (Anmeldung form filling), references Module 3 for dates.

### v5-1-2 — Practice with Common Verbs (Module 5, Wave 2)

**Gap:** v5-1-1 covers conjugation rules. v5-1-2 is the drill/practice companion — applied conjugation with the 20 most useful A1 verbs.

**Teaching goal:** After this video, learners can confidently conjugate the 20 most common A1 verbs without thinking, in all 6 person forms.

**Duration:** ~10 min

**Key narration lines to write:**

1. **Hook (0:15–1:00):**
   "Nee verb conjugation rules padichchu. Ippo practice cheyyaam! I'm going to throw 20 verbs at you — by the end, conjugation should feel AUTOMATIC."

2. **The Big 20 verbs (1:00–6:00):**
   Organized in functional groups:

   **Daily life (1:00–2:30):**
   - machen (do/make), gehen (go), kommen (come), essen (eat), trinken (drink)
   - Full conjugation table for each, spoken rapidly
   - "Say it WITH me — ich mache, du machst, er macht..."
   - ⏸️ per verb

   **Communication (2:30–3:30):**
   - sprechen (speak — IRREGULAR: du sprichst!), lesen (read — du liest!), schreiben (write), hören (hear), fragen (ask)
   - Flag the stem-changers clearly

   **Movement + routine (3:30–4:30):**
   - fahren (drive — du fährst!), laufen (walk/run — du läufst!), schlafen (sleep — du schläfst!), arbeiten (work — du arbeitest! extra -e-!)
   - "-eit-/-eit- verbs need that extra e: arbeiten → du arbeitest, finden → du findest"

   **Useful verbs (4:30–6:00):**
   - brauchen (need), kaufen (buy), wohnen (live), lernen (learn), spielen (play), kochen (cook)
   - These are all regular — confidence builders after the irregulars

3. **Speed drill (6:00–7:30):**
   - Verb + person flashes → student says the conjugated form
   - "gehen + wir → ?" ... "wir gehen!"
   - 15 rapid-fire items

4. **Sentence building (7:30–9:00):**
   - Move from isolated conjugation to real sentences
   - "Don't just conjugate — USE it! 'Ich gehe zur Arbeit. Du trinkst Kaffee. Er schläft lange.'"
   - 8 sentences, student fills the verb

5. **Closing pattern (9:00–10:00):**
   "Ini ee 20 verbs diary-il ezhuthuka. Divasavum 5 verb conjugate cheyyuka. One week-il automatic aakum. Promise!"

**Malayalam bridge:**
- "Malayalam-il verb-ine nee/ningal/avan/aval ennu maattunnathu pole — German-ilum same concept, different endings"
- Compare: Malayalam has more endings but learners already understand the pattern concept

**Exam tip:**
"In Schreiben, every sentence needs a correctly conjugated verb. In Sprechen, wrong conjugation = lost points. These 20 verbs cover 80% of what you need."

**Dependencies:** Requires v5-1-1 (rules). Feeds into v5-2-1 (daily activities), v5-3-1 (separable verbs), and every subsequent module.

### v6-1-2 — Kerala Food in German (Module 6, Wave 3)

**Gap:** v6-1-1 covers standard German foods. v6-1-2 is the cultural bridge — how to describe Kerala/Indian food in German, which is a real survival skill (explaining your food preferences, ordering, cooking discussions).

**Teaching goal:** After this video, learners can describe common Indian/Kerala dishes in simple German and navigate food conversations where they need to explain unfamiliar food.

**Duration:** ~10 min

**Key narration lines to write:**

1. **Hook (0:15–1:00):**
   "Your German colleague asks: 'Was ist Biryani?' Can you explain it? Or at a WG dinner: 'Was kochst du?' — Nammal nammude food-ine German-il describe cheyyaan padikkaam!"

2. **The Kerala food vocabulary (1:00–3:00):**
   - der Reis (rice — "Nammude staple food!"), das Curry, die Kokosmilch (coconut milk)
   - "Reis und Curry — that's how most Germans understand Indian food. But we know it's SO much more!"
   - das Gewürz (spice) / die Gewürze (spices) — der Pfeffer, der Kurkuma, der Zimt, der Kardamom
   - "German has words for our spices — Kurkuma, Kardamom — they borrowed them!"

3. **Describing Kerala dishes (3:00–5:30):**
   - Sadya: "Das ist ein traditionelles Festessen aus Kerala. Es hat Reis und viele vegetarische Gerichte."
   - Dosa: "Das ist ein dünner Pfannkuchen aus Reismehl." (thin pancake from rice flour)
   - Biryani: "Das ist Reis mit Gewürzen und Fleisch — sehr lecker!"
   - Chai/Chaya: "Das ist Tee mit Milch und Gewürzen."
   - Parotta: "Das ist ein weiches Fladenbrot aus Kerala."
   - For each: simple 1-sentence German description + ⏸️

4. **Dietary vocabulary (5:30–7:00):**
   - vegetarisch (vegetarian), vegan, scharf (spicy!), mild, süß (sweet)
   - "Ich esse kein Schweinefleisch" / "Ich esse kein Rindfleisch" — religious dietary needs
   - "Ist das scharf?" — "Für dich ja, für mich nein! 😄"
   - Lactose/allergen vocab: die Allergie, die Unverträglichkeit

5. **At a German dinner party (7:00–8:30):**
   - Dialogue: German friend invites you for dinner
   - "Was isst du? Gibt es etwas, was du nicht isst?"
   - How to politely explain food preferences
   - "You bring food? Yes! 'Ich habe etwas mitgebracht — das ist ein indisches Gericht.'"

6. **Cooking together (8:30–9:30):**
   - "schneiden (cut), kochen (cook/boil), braten (fry), mischen (mix), würzen (season)"
   - "Cooking with German friends = BEST German practice. Real conversation over real food!"

7. **Closing (9:30–10:00):**
   "Ini nee German-il ninte food explain cheyyaan ready aanu. Next time someone asks 'Was ist das?' — you'll have an answer!"

**Malayalam bridge:**
- Food is THE emotional anchor for Malayalis abroad — use this
- "Nammude food avarkku describe cheyyunnathu = cultural exchange. They LOVE it."
- Spice words borrowed into German from Sanskrit/Tamil/Malayalam roots

**Exam tip:**
"In Sprechen, they might ask about Essen. 'Was essen Sie gern?' If you can describe your favorite food in 2-3 sentences, that's a perfect A1 answer."

**Dependencies:** Builds on v6-1-1 (German foods). Complements v6-2-1 (ordering) and v6-3-1 (restaurant dialogue).

### v1-2-2 — Tricky Sounds for Malayalis (Module 1, Wave 4)

**Gap:** v1-2-1 covers the core sound system. v1-2-2 goes deeper into the alphabet, spelling your name, phone numbers, and the buchstabieren skill.

**Duration:** ~10 min

**Script writing notes:**

**Key content blocks:**
1. **Full alphabet sung/chanted** — not just listed, but with a rhythm
2. **Tricky letter names:** J = "yot" (not "jay"), W = "vay" (not "double-u"), V = "fow", Y = "üpsilon", Z = "tset"
3. **Special characters:** ä, ö, ü (as letter NAMES, not sounds — that was v1-2-1), ß = "Eszett"
4. **Spell common words:** H-A-U-S, S-C-H-U-L-E, M-Ü-N-C-H-E-N
5. **Spell YOUR name drill:** Use Nikhil, Anjali, Sreelakshmi, Vishnu as examples
6. **Buchstabiertafel (spelling alphabet):** A wie Anton, B wie Berta — for phone conversations
7. **Phone number saying:** single digits or pairs, with country code

**Malayalam bridges:**
- "Malayalam alphabet-il 51 letters und — German-il just 30! Already easier 😄"
- Compare how we spell names in English at call centers — same skill, different alphabet

**Exam tip:**
"In Sprechen and at every Amt visit, you'll need to buchstabieren. This is NOT optional — it's a real-world survival skill."

**Specific lines to include:**
- "J is NOT 'jay' — it's 'yot'. This will trip you up at the Amt!"
- "W is 'vay', V is 'fow' — the OPPOSITE of what you expect from English!"
- "Practice your name until you can spell it in your sleep. N-I-K-H-I-L. Perfect."

**Dependencies:** Builds on v1-2-1 (sounds). Connects to v2-1-2 (name on forms).

### v12-2-1 — Weather (Module 12, Wave 4)

**Gap:** Weather vocabulary and seasons for daily small talk and travel planning.

**Duration:** ~10 min

**Teaching goal:** Learners can discuss weather simply, understand weather forecasts, and use weather as small-talk filler.

**Script plan:**

1. **Hook (0:15–1:00):**
   "Germans LOVE talking about weather. 'Schönes Wetter heute!' is the German version of 'How's the weather?' — ee small talk padikaam!"

2. **Core weather vocab (1:00–3:00):**
   - die Sonne (sun) → sonnig (sunny), Es ist sonnig.
   - der Regen (rain) → regnerisch/Es regnet
   - der Schnee (snow) → Es schneit ("Kerala-yil snow illa — but Germany-il thayyaaraakkanam!")
   - der Wind → windig, Es ist windig
   - die Wolke (cloud) → bewölkt (cloudy)
   - der Nebel (fog) → neblig
   - das Gewitter (thunderstorm)

3. **Temperature (3:00–4:30):**
   - "Wie ist das Wetter?" → "Es ist warm/kalt/heiß/kühl."
   - "Es sind 25 Grad." (Celsius always — "Germany uses Celsius, not Fahrenheit!")
   - "Minus 5 Grad" — "Yes, it goes BELOW zero. Buy a Winterjacke!"
   - Kerala → Germany temperature shock: 35°C → -5°C

4. **Seasons (4:30–6:00):**
   - der Frühling (spring), der Sommer (summer), der Herbst (autumn), der Winter (winter)
   - "Im Sommer ist es warm. Im Winter schneit es oft."
   - Kerala comparison: "Nammukku rainy season, summer, winter enna concept und — but Germany-il FOUR distinct seasons!"

5. **Weather small talk patterns (6:00–7:30):**
   - "Schönes Wetter heute!" / "Schreckliches Wetter!" / "Es regnet schon wieder!"
   - "Morgen soll es regnen." (Tomorrow it's supposed to rain)
   - "Brauche ich einen Regenschirm?" (Do I need an umbrella?)
   - This is THE German small talk starter

6. **Weather forecast reading (7:30–9:00):**
   - Show a simplified Wetterbericht
   - Key symbols: ☀️ sonnig, 🌧️ Regen, ❄️ Schnee, ⛈️ Gewitter
   - "Heute sonnig, 18 Grad. Morgen bewölkt mit Regen, 12 Grad."
   - Practice: hear a forecast → dress appropriately

7. **Closing (9:00–10:00):**
   "Ini nee German weather-ne kurichchu samsaarikaan ready aanu. The safest small talk in Germany = weather talk!"

**Malayalam bridge:**
- "Kerala = always warm. Germany = ALWAYS changing. Weather talk is survival!"
- Compare monsoon (Monsunregen) with German Regen

**Exam tip:**
"Weather might appear in Hören or as a Sprechen topic. 'Was machen Sie bei schönem Wetter?' — have an answer ready!"

**Dependencies:** Connects to Module 12 hobbies (outdoor activities depend on weather).

### v12-3-1 — Making Plans & Invitations (Module 12, Wave 4)

**Gap:** How to make plans with friends, suggest activities, agree/decline, and set time/place. Core social German + Schreiben prep.

**Duration:** ~10 min

**Teaching goal:** Learners can propose a plan, agree or decline with a reason, and coordinate time/place for activities.

**Script plan:**

1. **Hook (0:15–1:00):**
   "Someone says 'Hast du am Samstag Zeit?' — How do you answer? Let's learn to make plans like a German!"

2. **Proposing activities (1:00–3:00):**
   - "Hast du Lust, ins Kino zu gehen?" (Do you feel like going to the cinema?)
   - "Wollen wir zusammen essen gehen?" (Shall we go eat together?)
   - "Wie wäre es mit einem Spaziergang?" (How about a walk?)
   - Pattern: Hast du Lust + zu + infinitive
   - Simpler: "Wollen wir...?" / "Sollen wir...?"

3. **Accepting (3:00–4:00):**
   - "Ja, gern!" / "Super, das klingt gut!" / "Ja, ich komme!" / "Tolle Idee!"
   - "Germans appreciate enthusiasm but keep it natural — 'Ja, gern!' is perfect"

4. **Declining with reason (4:00–5:30):**
   - "Leider kann ich nicht." (Unfortunately I can't)
   - "Tut mir leid, ich habe keine Zeit." (Sorry, I don't have time)
   - "Am Samstag kann ich leider nicht. Aber am Sonntag?" (Counter-offer!)
   - "Ich muss arbeiten / lernen / zum Arzt." (I have to work/study/go to doctor)
   - Light weil: "Ich kann nicht kommen, weil ich arbeiten muss." (preview)

5. **Coordinating details (5:30–7:00):**
   - "Wann treffen wir uns?" (When do we meet?)
   - "Um wie viel Uhr?" (At what time?)
   - "Wo treffen wir uns?" (Where do we meet?)
   - "Am Samstag um 15 Uhr am Bahnhof?" (Saturday at 3 PM at the station?)
   - "Passt dir das?" (Does that work for you?)

6. **Full plan-making dialogue (7:00–9:00):**
   - Complete exchange from proposal → discussion → agreement → confirmation
   - A: "Hey, hast du am Wochenende Zeit? Wollen wir ins Kino gehen?"
   - B: "Am Samstag kann ich nicht, aber am Sonntag passt es!"
   - A: "Super! Um 17 Uhr? Der Film fängt um 17:30 an."
   - B: "Perfekt! Wo treffen wir uns?"
   - A: "Am Kino? Um 17 Uhr."
   - B: "Alles klar, bis Sonntag! Tschüss!"
   - ⏸️ role-play both sides

7. **WhatsApp/message version (9:00–9:30):**
   - Same plan but as text messages — more informal
   - This bridges to v12-4-1 (invitation writing for Schreiben)

8. **Closing (9:30–10:00):**
   "Plan-making is REAL German. Practice with a friend — even by text! The patterns are always the same: propose → agree/decline → set time and place."

**Malayalam bridge:**
- "Kerala-il plan undaakkaan phone vilikkanam. Germany-il message aanu — and they KEEP to the plan!"
- German punctuality in social plans: "If you say 17:00, be there at 16:55"

**Exam tip:**
"In Sprechen Teil 2, they might give you a card about planning something. These exact patterns are what you need."

**Dependencies:** Uses Module 3 time expressions, connects to v12-4-1 (written invitations), bridges into Module 13 (past tense for reporting what you did).
