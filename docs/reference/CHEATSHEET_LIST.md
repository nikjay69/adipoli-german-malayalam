## The Complete Set (20 Cheatsheets)

### Category 1: Daily Essentials (stick above your desk)

#### 1. **Der / Die / Das — The Article Cheat Code**
- All 3 genders with color coding (der = blue, die = red, das = green)
- Noun ending patterns that predict gender:
  - der: -er, -ling, -ismus, -or, -ig (der Computer, der Schmetterling)
  - die: -ung, -heit, -keit, -tion, -ie, -schaft, -e (die Zeitung, die Freiheit)
  - das: -chen, -lein, -um, -ment, -nis (das Mädchen, das Dokument)
- Exceptions that WILL appear on the exam
- Malayalam memory trick: "die = like 'dee' = lady = feminine words often"
- Cases row: der→den→dem, die→die→der, das→das→dem, die(pl)→die→den

#### 2. **Verb Conjugation Master Table**
- Present tense endings: -e, -st, -t, -en, -t, -en
- Full conjugation of the 6 most important verbs:
  - sein (am/is/are), haben (have), machen (do/make)
  - können (can), müssen (must), möchten (would like)
- Irregular stems highlighted
- Separable verb rule with arrow showing prefix jumping to end
- Example: "Ich stehe um 7 Uhr **auf**" ← "aufstehen"

#### 3. **The German Case System — One Page**
- 4 cases in a clean 4×4 grid (Nominativ, Akkusativ, Dativ, Genitiv)
- Article changes: der/die/das/die → den/die/das/die → dem/der/dem/den
- When to use each case:
  - Nominativ = subject (who is doing it?)
  - Akkusativ = direct object (what is being done to?)
  - Dativ = indirect object (to/for whom?)
- Common prepositions grouped by case they demand
- Akkusativ: durch, für, gegen, ohne, um
- Dativ: aus, bei, mit, nach, seit, von, zu
- Two-way (Akk for movement, Dat for location): in, auf, an, über, unter, neben, vor, hinter, zwischen
- Color-coded to match Sheet #1

#### 4. **Numbers, Time & Date — Quick Reference**
- Numbers 0-20 (spelled out), then pattern for 21-100
- German number reversal: 24 = vierundzwanzig (four-and-twenty)
- Clock time: "Es ist halb drei" = 2:30 (NOT 3:30!) ← biggest trap
- Ordinal numbers: erste, zweite, dritte... (for dates)
- Days of the week + months (with pronunciation)
- Date format: Tag.Monat.Jahr (12.03.2026)
- Phone number reading pattern (two digits at a time)

### Category 4: Grammar Reference (stick near your study desk)

#### 14. **Perfekt Tense — How to Talk About the Past**
- Formula: haben/sein + ... + Partizip II (at the end!)
- Regular pattern: ge- + stem + -t (machen → gemacht, lernen → gelernt)
- Top 30 irregular past participles that WILL appear on exam:
  - essen→gegessen, trinken→getrunken, schreiben→geschrieben
  - fahren→gefahren, gehen→gegangen, kommen→gekommen
  - lesen→gelesen, sehen→gesehen, sprechen→gesprochen
- haben vs. sein rule:
  - sein = movement (gegangen, gefahren, gekommen, geflogen) + sein/werden/bleiben
  - haben = everything else
- 5 example sentences

#### 15. **W-Fragen — Question Words**
- All question words with examples:
  - Wer? (who), Was? (what), Wo? (where), Wohin? (where to)
  - Wann? (when), Warum? (why), Wie? (how)
  - Wie viel? (how much), Wie viele? (how many)
  - Welch-? (which), Woher? (where from)
- Word order after W-word: W-Wort + Verb + Subjekt + ...
- Ja/Nein question word order: Verb + Subjekt + ...?
- 10 most common exam questions using these

#### 16. **Word Order Rules — Where Does the Verb Go?**
- Rule 1: Verb is ALWAYS position 2 in statements
- Rule 2: In yes/no questions, verb is position 1
- Rule 3: In W-questions, W-word is position 1, verb is position 2
- Rule 4: Second verb (infinitive/participle) goes to the END
- Rule 5: Time-Manner-Place order (wann-wie-wo)
- Visual: color-coded sentence diagrams
- 5 correct vs. incorrect examples

### Category 5: Exam Day (carry with you / stick on bathroom door)

#### 17. **Goethe A1 Exam Format — At a Glance**
- 4 sections, time per section, points per section
- Hören: 20 min, 3 parts, 25 points — LISTEN carefully, 2 plays max
- Lesen: 25 min, 3 parts, 25 points — read signs, ads, emails
- Schreiben: 20 min, 2 parts, 25 points — fill form + write message
- Sprechen: 15 min, 3 parts, 25 points — intro, Q&A, requests
- Pass: 60/100 total, minimum 45% per section (so 11.25 per section minimum)
- Tips: read questions BEFORE listening, manage time, don't leave blanks

#### 18. **Schreiben Teil 2 — Email/Message Templates**
- 5 email templates for common exam topics:
  - Cancelling an appointment / sick leave
  - Inviting someone to a party/event
  - Thanking someone for help
  - Asking about a product/course/apartment
  - Responding to a classified ad
- Each template: greeting → 3 content points → closing
- Key phrases: Liebe/Lieber, Sehr geehrte/r, Mit freundlichen Grüßen, Viele Grüße
- Word count target: ~30 words (don't overthink it)

#### 19. **Form-Filling Cheat — Schreiben Teil 1**
- Every field that can appear on a German form:
  - Vorname (first name), Nachname/Familienname (last name)
  - Geburtsdatum (date of birth), Geburtsort (place of birth)
  - Adresse/Straße (street), PLZ (postcode), Ort/Stadt (city)
  - Telefonnummer, E-Mail-Adresse
  - Staatsangehörigkeit (nationality), Familienstand (marital status)
  - Beruf (profession), Unterschrift (signature), Datum (date)
- Example filled form (Kuttan's details)
- Common traps: Familienstand ≠ Familienname, PLZ = numbers only

### Category 6: Motivation & Culture (stick on your wall for daily reminder)

#### 20. **German Culture Survival Guide — Dos & Don'ts**
- Punctuality: 5 min early = on time. On time = late.
- Trash sorting: Gelber Sack, Biomüll, Restmüll, Papier, Glas (by color)
- Ruhezeit: No noise on Sundays and after 10 PM (seriously)
- Greetings: Handshake + eye contact. "Hallo" is fine for informal.
- At the store: Bring your own bags. You pay for bags.
- Apartment: Anmeldung within 2 weeks of moving in (mandatory!)
- Bread: Germany has 3000+ types. You will love it.
- Kerala parallels: hospitality culture, respect for elders, food = love (same same!)
