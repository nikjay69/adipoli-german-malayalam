## How to Read This Document

Each lesson blueprint covers:
- **Teaching Sequence** — what order to present content in the lesson flow
- **Video → Exercise Bridge** — how video content connects to practice
- **Exercise Progression** — from recognition → guided production → free production
- **Production Targets** — what the learner should be able to PRODUCE (not just recognize) after this lesson
- **Exam Checkpoint** — a mini-test that proves readiness for the specific exam task this lesson supports
- **SRS Feed** — which vocabulary items should enter spaced repetition from this lesson
- **Forward Links** — which later lessons depend on this one

# MODULE 2: Who Are You? (Wer bist du?)

**Exam role:** Sprechen Teil 1 (self-intro), Schreiben Teil 1 (form fields), Hören (personal info dialogues)

---

## Lesson 2-1: What's Your Name? (Wie heißt du?)

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Motivational hook: "Your name is your first word in any language — let's make it work in German"
2. Three name patterns: `Ich heiße...`, `Mein Name ist...`, `Ich bin...`
3. Formal vs informal asking: `Wie heißt du?` vs `Wie heißen Sie?`
4. Meeting response: `Freut mich!` → `Gleichfalls!`
5. Name spelling: German alphabet for Malayali names
6. Form connection: Vorname, Nachname fields

**Video → Exercise Bridge:**
- v2-1-1 teaches name exchange → exercises 2-1-1 through 2-1-5 test recognition
- v2-1-2 teaches spelling → exercises 2-1-6 through 2-1-8 test alphabet knowledge
- **Gap to fill:** No production exercise bridges the two videos

**Exercise Progression:**
1. MC: formal vs informal choice (ex2-1-1) — recognition
2. Fill-blank: `___ heiße Priya` (ex2-1-2) — guided recognition
3. MC: meaning of `Freut mich` (ex2-1-3) — vocabulary
4. Matching: phrase-meaning pairs (ex2-1-4) — recognition
5. Ordering: conversation sequence (ex2-1-5) — structural understanding
6. Fill-blank: `buchstabieren` (ex2-1-6) — vocabulary
7. MC: response to `Freut mich` (ex2-1-7) — social pattern
8. MC: alphabet knowledge (ex2-1-8) — recognition
- **Missing:** Free-text "Write your self-introduction", speaking drill, dictation

**Production Targets (what learner must be able to DO):**
- Say: "Hallo! Ich heiße [Name]. Freut mich!"
- Spell their name using German letter names
- Ask someone's name formally and informally
- Fill Vorname/Nachname fields correctly

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> Mini-test: "Introduce yourself in 3 sentences. Include your name, a greeting, and spell your last name."
> Pass criteria: correct greeting, one name pattern used, name spelled without errors

**SRS Feed:** 10 vocab items (vocab2-1-1 through vocab2-1-10)
- Priority items for early review: `Ich heiße`, `Wie heißt du?`, `Freut mich!`, `buchstabieren`

**Forward Links:**
- Lesson 2-2 (origin) builds directly on this
- Module 14 Lesson 14-1 (forms) reuses Vorname/Nachname
- Module 18 Lesson 18-3 (Sprechen Teil 1) expects fluent self-intro

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Free-text:** "Stell dich vor! Write 3 sentences: greeting, your name, and 'nice to meet you'."
2. **Speaking:** Record yourself saying "Hallo! Ich heiße [your name]. Freut mich!" — compare with model
3. **Dictation:** Hear a name spelled in German alphabet → write it down
4. **Form drill:** Fill Vorname and Nachname fields from spoken personal info

## Lesson 2-2: Where Are You From? (Woher kommst du?)

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Core question patterns: `Woher kommst du?` / `Woher kommen Sie?`
2. Answer pattern: `Ich komme aus [Country/City]`
3. Country-nationality-language triangle
4. Special: Inder ≠ Indianer!
5. German cities every migrant should know
6. Grammar preview: `aus` + article exceptions (Schweiz, USA, Iran)

**Video → Exercise Bridge:**
- v2-2-1 teaches countries/nationalities → exercises test matching and recall
- v2-2-2 teaches continent/grammar details → deeper exercises
- **Gap:** No production tasks for saying where you're from

**Exercise Progression:**
1. MC/matching: country-nationality pairs — recognition
2. Fill-blank: `Ich komme aus ___` — guided completion
3. MC: Inder vs Indianer trap — common error prevention
4. Matching: continent names — vocabulary
5. Grammar: `aus der Schweiz`, `aus den USA` — article exceptions
- **Missing:** Free-text origin statement, speaking drill for full origin sentence

**Production Targets:**
- Say: "Ich komme aus Kerala in Indien. Ich bin Inder/Inderin."
- Name 5+ countries with correct nationality form
- Handle article exceptions for `aus` (Schweiz, USA)
- Say city + country together: "Ich wohne in Kochi. Ich komme aus Indien."

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> Mini-test: "Say where you're from, your nationality, and one city you know in Germany."
> Pass criteria: correct `aus` usage, nationality form correct, one German city named

**SRS Feed:** 10 vocab items — priority: `Ich komme aus`, `Indien`, `indisch`, `Deutschland`

**Forward Links:**
- Lesson 2-3 (profession) adds another self-intro layer
- Module 18 Lesson 18-3 (Sprechen) expects all origin patterns fluent

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Free-text:** "Woher kommst du? Write 3 sentences about your origin."
2. **Speaking:** Record "Ich komme aus [city] in [country]. Ich bin [nationality]."
3. **Listening + writing:** Hear someone say where they're from → fill a form with Land, Stadt, Staatsangehörigkeit

## Lesson 2-3: What Do You Do? (Was machst du?)

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Profession question: `Was bist du von Beruf?` / `Was machen Sie beruflich?`
2. Answer patterns: `Ich bin [Beruf]`, `Ich arbeite als [Beruf]`
3. Masculine/feminine profession forms: Lehrer/Lehrerin, Arzt/Ärztin
4. Student pattern: `Ich studiere [Fach] an der Universität`
5. Common professions relevant to Malayali migration: Krankenpfleger, Informatiker, Ingenieur

**Exercise Progression:**
- MC: profession-translation pairs
- Matching: masculine-feminine profession forms
- Fill-blank: `Ich bin ___ von Beruf`
- **Missing:** Free-text "describe your job", speaking drill

**Production Targets:**
- Say: "Ich bin [profession] von Beruf" or "Ich studiere [subject]"
- Use correct masculine/feminine profession form
- Ask someone about their profession formally and informally

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> "What's your profession or what do you study? Answer in 2 sentences."
> Pass: correct profession pattern, correct gender form

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Free-text:** Write 2 sentences about your profession/studies
2. **Speaking:** Record your profession sentence + what you study/do

---

## Lesson 2-4: Languages I Speak (Sprachen)

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Language question: `Welche Sprachen sprichst du?`
2. Answer: `Ich spreche Malayalam, Englisch und ein bisschen Deutsch`
3. Language skill levels: `gut`, `ein bisschen`, `fließend`
4. Pattern: language is always capitalized when it's a noun
5. `sprechen` conjugation: irregular stem change (du sprichst, er spricht)

**Production Targets:**
- Say: "Ich spreche [3 languages]. Ich lerne Deutsch."
- Rate skill levels for each language
- Handle `sprechen` irregular conjugation

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> "What languages do you speak? Say at least 2 languages with skill level."
> Pass: correct `sprechen` form, at least 2 languages named, one with level indicator

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Free-text:** "Welche Sprachen sprichst du? Describe your language skills."
2. **Speaking:** Record your language list with skill levels

---

## Lesson 2-5: My First Full Conversation

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Combine all 4 previous lessons into one flowing conversation
2. Model dialogue: greeting → name → origin → profession → languages → goodbye
3. Role-play both sides
4. Practice with different formality levels (friend vs office)

**Production Targets:**
- Sustain a 5-7 sentence self-introduction covering all topics
- Handle both formal and informal register
- Respond to questions, not just deliver a monologue

**Exam Checkpoint (Module-Level):**
> Full self-introduction: "Introduce yourself. Cover your name, where you're from, your profession, languages, and one hobby. 2 minutes."
> This is a direct Goethe Sprechen Teil 1 simulation.

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Full self-intro recording:** 2-minute timer, all 5 topic cards
2. **Paired role-play script:** Student plays both asker and answerer
3. **Form drill:** Complete a full registration form from spoken self-intro audio

# MODULE 3: Numbers & Time (Zahlen und Zeit)

**Exam role:** Hören (numbers, times, prices, dates in audio), Lesen (schedules, prices), Schreiben (date fields), Sprechen (age, birthday, appointments)

---

## Lesson 3-1: Numbers 0-20

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Count 0-10 with pronunciation focus (v3-1-1)
2. Count 11-20 with pattern recognition (v3-1-2)
3. Speed drills: counting up, counting down, random order
4. Application: age, price, quantity contexts

**Exercise Progression:**
- MC: number recognition
- Fill-blank: number-word matching
- **Missing:** Audio dictation (hear number → write), speaking (say number aloud)

**Production Targets:**
- Count 0-20 at conversational speed
- Hear a number and identify it without seeing text
- Say their age: "Ich bin [number] Jahre alt"

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> Audio test: hear 5 numbers, write them down. 4/5 = pass.

**SRS Feed:** Numbers 0-20 as vocab items, plus `Jahre alt`

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Dictation:** Hear 8 numbers → write each
2. **Speaking:** Say your age, your parents' ages
3. **Speed drill:** Random number flash → say in German within 3 seconds

---

## Lesson 3-2: Numbers 21-100

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. The German number reversal: `vierundzwanzig` (four-and-twenty, not twenty-four!)
2. Pattern: ones + und + tens
3. Round tens: zwanzig, dreißig, vierzig... (note: dreißig, not dreizig!)
4. Practice: ages, prices, addresses, phone numbers

**Production Targets:**
- Say any 2-digit number correctly (with reversal)
- Hear a 2-digit number and write it
- Handle prices: "Das kostet siebenunddreißig Euro"

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> Dictation: hear 5 two-digit numbers, write them. Also: hear a price and write it.

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Dictation:** Hear numbers in price/age/address context → write
2. **Speaking:** Read a price aloud, say your phone number
3. **Number reversal drill:** See "47" → say "siebenundvierzig" (timed)

---

## Lesson 3-3: What Time Is It? (Wie spät ist es?)

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Official time (24h): `Es ist vierzehn Uhr dreißig`
2. Conversational time: `halb drei` = 2:30 (NOT 3:30!)
3. `Viertel vor/nach`: quarter to/past
4. Common time phrases: `um [time]`, `von ... bis ...`
5. **Critical trap:** `halb drei` = 2:30, the biggest time confusion for non-native speakers

**Production Targets:**
- Tell time in both official and conversational German
- Understand `halb` correctly (refers to the NEXT hour)
- Say when something happens: "Der Kurs beginnt um neun Uhr"

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> Hear 5 time expressions → write the digital time. Must get `halb` correct.

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Listening dictation:** Hear times → write in HH:MM format
2. **Speaking:** Say what time you wake up, eat lunch, go to bed
3. **Trap drill:** 10 `halb/Viertel` items → select correct digital time

---

## Lesson 3-4: Days & Months (Tage und Monate)

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Days of the week: Montag through Sonntag
2. Months: Januar through Dezember
3. Pattern: `am Montag`, `im Januar`
4. Birthday: `Mein Geburtstag ist am [ordinal] [month]`
5. Date format: TT.MM.JJJJ

**Production Targets:**
- Name all days and months from memory
- Say their birthday: "Mein Geburtstag ist am dritten Mai"
- Write a date in German format

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> "When is your birthday? Write it in German format AND say it aloud."

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Free-text:** Write today's date, your birthday, and a friend's birthday
2. **Speaking:** Say your birthday with ordinal number
3. **Dictation:** Hear dates → write in DD.MM.YYYY

---

## Lesson 3-5: Dates & Birthdays

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Ordinal numbers: erste, zweite, dritte... (for dates)
2. Full date construction: `am dritten März zweitausendein`
3. Asking: `Wann hast du Geburtstag?`
4. Form connection: Geburtsdatum field
5. Seasons connection: `im Frühling`, `im Sommer`

**Production Targets:**
- Say any date with ordinal numbers
- Fill Geburtsdatum field from spoken information
- Handle year expression correctly

---

## Lesson 3-6: Making Appointments (Termine machen)

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Asking for an appointment: `Ich möchte einen Termin machen`
2. Offering times: `Geht es am Mittwoch um zehn Uhr?`
3. Confirming: `Ja, das passt` / `Nein, leider nicht`
4. Canceling: `Ich muss leider absagen`
5. Doctor/office/school appointment scenarios

**Production Targets:**
- Make a simple appointment by phone/in person
- Suggest and respond to time proposals
- Cancel with a simple reason

**Exam Checkpoint (Module-Level):**
> Listening test: hear an appointment arrangement → note day, time, place.
> Speaking test: role-play making a doctor's appointment.

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Listening + form fill:** Hear appointment details → write day, time, place
2. **Speaking role-play:** Make an appointment at the doctor's
3. **Free-text:** Write a message confirming or canceling an appointment

# MODULE 5: Daily Routine (Mein Tag)

**Exam role:** Sprechen (daily routine is a standard topic), Schreiben (describing activities), Grammar foundation for all production

---

## Lesson 5-1: Regular Verbs Present Tense

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. The infinitive → stem + ending logic (v5-1-1)
2. All 6 person endings: -e, -st, -t, -en, -t, -en
3. Drill the Big 20 verbs (v5-1-2)
4. Special stems: `-eit-` verbs need extra `-e-` (arbeiten → du arbeitest)
5. Sentence building from conjugation

**Exercise Progression:**
- MC: identify correct conjugation
- Fill-blank: complete verb form
- Matching: infinitive → conjugated form
- **Missing:** Free-text sentence building, speaking drills

**Production Targets:**
- Conjugate any regular verb in present tense for all 6 persons
- Build simple sentences: subject + verb + object/complement
- Handle special `-eit-` and `-aten` stems without error

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> Conjugation speed test: 10 verb-person combos → write correct form. 8/10 = pass.

**SRS Feed:** The Big 20 verbs as flashcards (infinitive → ich/du/er forms)

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Free-text:** Write 5 sentences about what you do daily using different verbs
2. **Speaking:** Read aloud a conjugation table for 3 verbs
3. **Transformation:** Given "ich mache" → produce "du ___, er ___, wir ___"

---

## Lesson 5-2: Morning Routine

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Routine verbs: aufstehen, duschen, frühstücken, Zähne putzen
2. Time connectors: zuerst, dann, danach, zum Schluss
3. Model morning: "Zuerst stehe ich um sieben Uhr auf. Dann dusche ich."
4. Time link to Module 3

**Production Targets:**
- Describe morning routine in 4-5 sequential sentences
- Use time connectors correctly
- Handle separable verbs in main clause (aufstehen → ich stehe ... auf)

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Free-text:** "Beschreib deinen Morgen. Schreib 4-5 Sätze."
2. **Speaking:** Record 1-minute morning routine description
3. **Ordering:** Put morning activities in logical sequence

---

## Lesson 5-3: Separable Verbs

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. What separable verbs are: aufstehen, anfangen, einkaufen, fernsehen
2. The split rule: prefix goes to the end in main clause
3. Visual demonstration of the split
4. Common separable prefixes: auf-, an-, ein-, aus-, mit-, zu-, ab-
5. Non-separable prefixes to contrast: be-, er-, ver-, ent-

**Production Targets:**
- Use common separable verbs in correct word order
- Distinguish separable from non-separable
- Build sentences: "Ich stehe um 7 Uhr auf" (not "Ich aufstehe")

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> Write 5 sentences using separable verbs. The prefix must be at the end.

---

## Lesson 5-4: My Day — From Morning to Night

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Full day narrative combining all routine verbs
2. Time expressions: morgens, mittags, nachmittags, abends, nachts
3. Model narrative: teacher describes a full day
4. Student personalizes with their own routine

**Production Targets:**
- Describe a full day from waking to sleeping in 8-10 sentences
- Use variety of time expressions
- Mix regular verbs, separable verbs, and time connectors

**Exam Checkpoint (Module-Level):**
> Speaking: "Beschreib deinen normalen Tag. 2 Minuten."
> Writing: "Schreib über deinen Tag. 5-6 Sätze."

---

## Lesson 5-5: Telling About Your Week

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Day-specific activities: "Am Montag arbeite ich. Am Dienstag lerne ich Deutsch."
2. Frequency: immer, oft, manchmal, selten, nie
3. Weekend vs weekday patterns
4. Combine with hobbies preview

**Production Targets:**
- Describe weekly pattern with day-specific activities
- Use frequency adverbs naturally
- Talk about weekend vs weekday difference

# MODULE 14: Formal Life in Germany (Offizielles Leben)

**Exam role:** Schreiben Teil 1 (form filling — direct), Lesen (official letters/notices), Sprechen (office interactions)

---

## Lesson 14-1: Filling Out Forms (Formulare ausfüllen)

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Why forms matter: Anmeldung as first task in Germany (v14-1-1)
2. Every form field explained with examples (v14-1-2)
3. Date format drill: DD.MM.YYYY
4. Live form walkthrough with Malayali example data
5. Common mistakes: date format, name order, missing documents

**Exercise Progression:**
- MC: field name recognition
- Matching: field → meaning
- Fill-blank: complete form fields from text
- **Missing:** Production form-filling from audio, full blank form drill

**Production Targets:**
- Fill any standard Anmeldeformular without errors
- Handle date, address, and name fields in German format
- Understand Familienstand, Staatsangehörigkeit, Religionszugehörigkeit fields

**Exam Checkpoint:**
> Given blank form fields + audio with personal info → fill the form. All fields correct = pass.
> This is a direct Goethe Schreiben Teil 1 simulation.

**SRS Feed:** All form field names (Vorname, Nachname, Geburtsdatum, etc.)

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Form drill:** Complete blank Anmeldeformular with your own data
2. **Dictation + form:** Hear personal info for a fictional person → fill their form
3. **Error correction:** Given a filled form with 3 mistakes → find and fix them
4. **Speaking:** Spell your address aloud in German

---

## Lesson 14-2: At the Office (Im Amt)

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Office vocabulary: Termin, Nummer, Wartezimmer, Schalter
2. Arrival dialogue: checking in, taking a number, waiting
3. At the counter: presenting documents, answering questions
4. Politeness: `Ich hätte gern...`, `Können Sie mir helfen?`
5. Post-visit: receiving documents, next steps

**Production Targets:**
- Navigate a basic office visit
- Ask for help and present documents
- Understand and respond to simple official questions

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Role-play:** Office visit from door to counter
2. **Listening:** Understand announcements in waiting room
3. **Speaking:** Ask for a new Termin

---

## Lesson 14-3: Opening a Bank Account (Konto eröffnen)

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Why you need a Girokonto in Germany
2. Bank vocabulary: Konto, Überweisung, Geldautomat, PIN, IBAN
3. Bank account opening dialogue
4. Online banking basics

**Production Targets:**
- Request a bank account opening in simple German
- Understand basic banking terms
- Fill a bank application form

---

## Lesson 14-4: Writing Formal Letters & Emails

**Teaching Sequence:**
1. Formal letter structure: Sehr geehrte/r → body → Mit freundlichen Grüßen
2. Informal message structure: Liebe/r → body → Viele Grüße
3. Common message types: appointment request, information request, cancellation
4. Email etiquette in Germany

**Production Targets:**
- Write a formal email requesting an appointment
- Write an informal message to a friend
- Use correct greeting/closing for each formality level

**Exam Checkpoint (Module-Level):**
> Schreiben Teil 1 simulation: fill a registration form from audio input
> Schreiben Teil 2 prep: write a short formal message requesting an appointment

**Production Exercises to Add:**
1. **Free-text:** Write a formal email to a Sprachschule requesting course information
2. **Free-text:** Write an informal message canceling plans with a friend
3. **Template matching:** Given a situation → choose correct greeting/closing

# MODULES 17 & 18: Exam Prep (Blueprint Summary)

These modules have the strongest existing exercise coverage. The lesson-level gap is primarily in delivery mechanics, not content structure.

## Module 17 Key Lesson Gaps:
- **17-2 and 17-3 (Hören):** Need real audio. Without audio generation, these lessons simulate but don't train.
- **17-4 and 17-5 (Lesen):** Need timed mode. Students should practice under exam time pressure.
- **17-6 (Mock Test):** Needs scoring and performance feedback, not just exercise completion.

## Module 18 Key Lesson Gaps:
- **18-1 (Form filling):** Needs production forms, not just teaching about forms.
- **18-2 (Short messages):** Needs more varied prompts and model answer banks.
- **18-3 (Self-intro):** Connected to /practice/intro — needs random prompt variation.
- **18-4 (Questions):** Needs random topic-card generator with timer.
- **18-5 (Requests):** Needs situation-card deck with polite request templates.
- **18-6 (Full mock):** Needs integrated scoring across all 4 sections.

# Exam Readiness Checkpoints — Where to Place Them

The course should have checkpoint gates at these specific lesson transitions:

| After Lesson | Checkpoint Tests | Must-Pass Criteria |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 2-5 | Full self-intro (2 min) + form fill | All 5 identity topics covered, form 100% correct |
| 3-6 | Number dictation + time + appointment | 80% numbers correct, `halb` trap passed, appointment made |
| 5-4 | Daily routine speaking (2 min) | 6+ sentences, verb conjugation correct, time connectors used |
| 6-5 | Restaurant ordering role-play | Polite order placed, accusative mostly correct |
| 14-4 | Form fill + formal email | Form 100%, email has greeting/body/close |
| 17-6 | Mock Hören + Lesen (timed) | 60% each section (matching real exam pass threshold) |
| 18-6 | Mock Schreiben + Sprechen | Form correct, message hits 3 points, intro is 2 min, request is polite |

**Implementation note:** Checkpoints should block progression only if student scores below 50%. Between 50-69%, show warning and allow retry. Above 70%, proceed with encouragement. Above 90%, skip ahead option.
