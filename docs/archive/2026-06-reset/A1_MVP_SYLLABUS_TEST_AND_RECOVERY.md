# Adipoli German A1 MVP — Syllabus, Test, and Recovery System

Updated: 2026-06-02 10:43 CEST

This is the active production artifact for the guided A1 video-course spine. It converts the existing 18-module course material into an 8-module MVP path with owned video lessons, must-do practice, closed diagnostics, weakness tags, and exact recovery prescriptions.

## 1. Target course size

### Owned video target

Target: about 35 hours of Adipoli-owned guided video lessons.

Recommended MVP allocation:

- 56 owned video lessons.
- 2,105 video minutes total = 35h 05m.
- Phone-first lesson blocks: usually 25–45 minutes of video split into watchable chapters, followed by required app practice.
- Public resources are reinforcement only. They never replace the Adipoli teaching spine.

### Why not bloat to 40h now

Do not expand to 40h unless checkpoint data proves a real A1 gap.

40h would add about 5 extra owned video hours. For this audience, that is likely to hurt completion more than it helps pass rate if the extra time becomes more explanation, more dashboards, or more resource browsing.

A1 readiness needs:

1. clear sequence;
2. repeated speaking/listening/writing production;
3. closed diagnostics;
4. exact recovery tasks;
5. a final mock.

It does not need 5 more hours of generic grammar explanation. If a module needs more help, add targeted recovery videos or booster drills, not more must-do video by default.

Rule for adding video beyond 35h:

- Add only when a checkpoint weakness repeats across learners.
- The added video must map to one weakness tag, e.g. `hoeren:numbers` or `schreiben:three_points`.
- The added video must have a retest task.
- Prefer replacing a weak lesson over increasing total hours.

### What happens to current Module 13, 15, and 16

- Current Module 13 past tense: keep only A1-safe chunks needed for short personal talk, e.g. `Ich war krank`, `Am Wochenende habe ich gelernt`, as score-booster or Module 8 exam expression support. Do not make Perfekt depth a must-do MVP burden.
- Current Module 15 culture: use as short context inserts inside relevant modules. Do not make culture a standalone must-do module unless it directly supports life/exam comprehension.
- Current Module 16 A1+ grammar bridge: score-booster only. Dative deep dives, two-way prepositions, reflexive verbs, and subordinate clauses are not A1 MVP must-do unless a Goethe task truly needs the pattern.

## 3. Module-by-module must-do and score-booster tracks

Every module follows the same product rhythm:

`scene hook → video lesson → app practice → closed mini-check → module diagnostic → recovery if weak → next module or score booster`

A learner should never choose from a library. The app shows the next required block.

### Module 1 — First German moment

Purpose: give the learner a real German win before theory. Existing Module 1 already has the right raw ingredients: Kerala/Goethe Kochi setting, greetings, pronunciation, first conversation, and a first sentence/dictation such as `Ich lerne Deutsch.` The MVP must cut motivation bloat and lead quickly to hear/say/repair.

Owned video lessons, 240m:

1. Start listening: `Guten Morgen` / `Guten Morgen, Frau Weber`.
2. Why A1 matters for Malayalis, kept short and outcome-led.
3. German sounds for Malayalam speakers: `ch`, `sch`, `w`, `v`, umlauts.
4. Formal greetings: `Guten Morgen`, `Guten Tag`, `Guten Abend`.
5. Politeness survival: `bitte`, `danke`, `Entschuldigung`.
6. Goodbye and repair phrases: `Auf Wiedersehen`, `Noch einmal, bitte`.
7. First mini-conversation: greet, reply, thank, leave.

Must-do sequence:

1. Hear Frau Weber greet the learner.
2. Say the reply aloud.
3. Watch the greeting/pronunciation video.
4. Shadow 10 short audio lines.
5. Do a pronunciation discrimination check: `ich` vs `isch`, `w` vs English w.
6. Do one greeting repair task.
7. Type or dictate `Ich lerne Deutsch.`
8. Mini-check: listen to 5 greetings and choose the correct response.
9. Speaking check: record or self-mark one greeting exchange.
10. Module diagnostic: pass Hören/greeting + Sprechen/formality basics.

Score-booster track:

- One 8–12m curated public pronunciation video for `ch/sch/ü`, assigned only after the learner fails or wants extra accent confidence.
- One slow audio shadowing set: 20 greetings/politeness lines.
- One Goethe-style micro-card: examiner greets; learner answers formally.

### Module 2 — Identity, numbers, time

Purpose: build Goethe speaking Teil 1 foundation and the most common listening details: name, spelling, age, origin, address basics, phone numbers, dates, times.

Owned video lessons, 250m:

1. Name and spelling: `Ich heiße...`, alphabet, spelling cards.
2. Origin and nationality: `Ich komme aus Indien`, `Ich bin Inder/Inderin`.
3. Job/study/languages: `Ich arbeite`, `Ich studiere`, `Ich spreche Malayalam`.
4. Numbers 0–100 and phone numbers.
5. Time, days, months.
6. Dates, birthdays, appointment language.
7. Full self-introduction script.

Must-do sequence:

1. Watch name/spelling video.
2. Spell own name aloud using German alphabet.
3. Fill personal mini-form: name, country, language, phone.
4. Watch numbers/time video.
5. Dictate 10 phone numbers and 8 appointment times.
6. Build a 45-second self-introduction.
7. Answer 10 examiner-style personal questions.
8. Mini-checks after each video.
9. Module diagnostic: self-intro, spelling, numbers/time listening, personal form.
10. If pass, unlock Module 3; if weak, recovery task is skill-tagged.

Score-booster track:

- One selected German alphabet song/video only if spelling is weak; output is a recorded spelling of own name.
- One public slow-number audio drill; output is 15 correctly written numbers/times.
- One extra Goethe speaking Teil 1 card set; output is a 90-second self-introduction.

### Module 3 — People, home, daily life

3. Articles: `der/die/das` as survival patterns, not a theory lecture.
4. Possessives: `mein/meine`, `dein/deine`.

8. Mini-checks: family vocab, articles, routine verbs.
9. Module diagnostic: read a profile, describe one person, write a tiny routine.

Score-booster track:

- One worksheet-type article drill, limited to 20 high-frequency A1 nouns.
- One public daily-routine listening clip; output is a completed routine timeline.
- One speaking prompt: describe family for 60 seconds.

### Module 8 — Goethe A1 bootcamp

Purpose: turn course skill into exam readiness. This is not new broad teaching; it is exam format, timed execution, weak-tag recovery, and final mock confidence.

Owned video lessons, 310m:

1. Goethe A1 format and closed-test rules.
2. Hören Teil 1–3 strategy and practice.
3. Lesen Teil 1–3 strategy and practice.
4. Schreiben Teil 1 forms + Teil 2 short message.
5. Sprechen Teil 1 self-intro and spelling.
6. Sprechen Teil 2/3 question cards and polite requests.
7. Final mock debrief and next 7-day plan.

Must-do sequence:

1. Watch Goethe format video.
2. Complete Hören section practice closed-book.
3. Complete Lesen section practice timed.
4. Complete Schreiben form + short message.
5. Complete Sprechen self-intro + question/request cards.
6. Receive manual or rubric-based score by section and tag.
7. Do required recovery for any section under threshold.
8. Retest weak section only.
9. Complete final full mock.
10. Receive pass/weak-pass/fail result and exact final 7-day plan.

Score-booster track:

- One extra Goethe sample paper, assigned only after the first mock or for strong learners.
- One speaking partner/teacher practice slot; output is a scored speaking sheet.
- One writing model-answer comparison; output is a revised 30-word message.

## 4. Checkpoint schedule

### Lesson mini-checks

After every owned video lesson:

- Closed-book.
- 5–8 tasks, 5–8 minutes.
- At least one production action where relevant: say, type, fill, or dictate.
- Tags attached to every missed item.
- Pass target: 70% or all required production tasks complete.
- If failed: prescribe one micro-recovery task before the next lesson block.

Mini-check examples:

- Module 1 greeting lesson: hear 5 greetings, choose/form the correct reply, say one reply aloud.
- Module 2 numbers lesson: hear 8 numbers/times, write them.
- Module 6 writing lesson: write a 30-word SMS with 3 required points.

### Module diagnostics

At the end of each module:

- Closed-book.
- 20–35 minutes depending on module.
- Mixed skills, but weighted toward that module’s exam-relevant output.
- Result states:
  - `pass`: continue.
  - `weak_pass`: continue allowed, but show one optional booster and schedule review.
  - `recovery_required`: next module locked until the weak tag retest passes.

Module pass thresholds:

- Hören/Lesen recognition: 70%.
- Grammar/vocab controlled tasks: 70%.
- Writing/speaking production: rubric score at least 3/5 and no missing required content point.
- Critical exam readiness modules 7–8: 60% minimum per Goethe section, 70% recommended.

### Goethe-style gates

| Gate | After module | Sections tested | Purpose | Unlock rule |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- | --- |
| Gate 1: survival oral | 1 | Hören + Sprechen | Can hear and answer basic formal lines | Required speaking/greeting tasks complete |
| Gate 2: personal-info foundation | 2 | Sprechen Teil 1 + numbers/time Hören + form basics | Can introduce self and capture personal data | No critical weakness in spelling/numbers/self-intro |
| Gate 3: daily-life interaction | 4 | Hören details + Sprechen requests + reading signs/prices | Can buy/order/read everyday info | Pass Hören details and request rubric |
| Gate 4: writing and topic cards | 6 | Schreiben Teil 2 + Sprechen Teil 2 + reading messages | Can write/ask/answer simple topics | Message has all 3 content points |
| Gate 5: exam bridge | 7 | Goethe Hören/Lesen + Schreiben Teil 1 | Can handle paper-style input | 60% per tested section, form fields complete |
| Final mock | 8 | Full Goethe A1 | Final exam readiness | Overall 60%, no section below 45%; recovery if weak |

### Final mock schedule

Final mock happens in Module 8 after all section strategies.

Mock structure:

1. Hören: 20m, 25 points.
2. Lesen: 25m, 25 points.
3. Schreiben: 20m, 25 points.
4. Sprechen: 15m equivalent, 25 points, manually scored or self-recorded for human review.

Result interpretation:

- `ready`: 70+ overall and no section below 60.
- `exam-pass likely`: 60–69 overall and no section below 45.
- `risky`: 60+ overall but one section below 45, or 50–59 overall.
- `not ready`: below 50 overall or missing writing/speaking production.

## 6. Manual scoring rubric

Manual scoring is acceptable for MVP. The product should be honest: closed tests diagnose, rubrics score, recovery prescribes. AI can help later but is not required.

### Recognition tasks: Hören, Lesen, grammar/vocab checks

- Score objectively by correct answer.
- Every wrong item carries 1–3 weakness tags.
- If the learner guesses but cannot explain, still score by answer for MVP; use retest consistency to catch shallow passes.

### Writing rubric, 5 points per task

Use for forms and short messages.

| Score | Meaning | Criteria |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 5 | Strong A1 | All required info present, understandable, A1-safe, minor errors only |
| 4 | Pass | All required info present, understandable, some grammar/article errors |
| 3 | Weak pass | Main message understandable, one small content/detail weakness, errors do not block meaning |
| 2 | Recovery required | Missing one required point or repeated word-order/form errors |
| 1 | Not pass | Meaning unclear, multiple required points missing, copied/open-book feel |
| 0 | No valid attempt | Blank, unrelated, or not German enough to assess |

Required fail conditions for Goethe-style writing:

- Missing one of the three content points in Schreiben Teil 2.
- Form field copied into wrong field when meaning matters, e.g. phone/email/address.
- Message is far above A1 and appears copied from AI/public source.

### Speaking rubric, 5 points per task

Use for self-introduction, question cards, and request cards.

| Score | Meaning | Criteria |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 5 | Strong A1 | Complete answer, understandable pronunciation, correct formality, natural enough |
| 4 | Pass | Complete answer, minor pronunciation/grammar issues, no meaning blocker |
| 3 | Weak pass | Understandable but hesitant or incomplete in one detail |
| 2 | Recovery required | Missing required info, wrong question/request pattern, formality confusion |
| 1 | Not pass | Examiner cannot understand the answer or request |
| 0 | No valid attempt | No spoken attempt |

Speaking evidence options for MVP:

- learner self-check with model audio and required aloud response;
- recorded clip for teacher/manual review;
- live teacher/partner review;
- later AI transcription as support only.

### Module result logic

- `pass`: all required tasks pass thresholds.
- `weak_pass`: one non-critical tag below target, no production fail.
- `recovery_required`: any critical production task fails, any Goethe section below threshold, or same tag fails twice.
- `manual_review_needed`: writing/speaking attempted but cannot be confidently scored by rule.

## 7. Recovery prescriptions

Recovery must be exact and short. Never say only `revise`, `practice more`, or `watch YouTube`.

### Hören recovery

Use when tags include `hoeren:*`.

Default prescription:

1. Rewatch the matching recovery clip, 6–10m.
2. Do one focused audio drill, 10–15 items.
3. Retest only the failed listening tag.

Examples:

- `hoeren:numbers`: replay Numbers 0–100 recovery, 8m → write 15 heard numbers → retest 8 phone numbers.
- `hoeren:time_dates`: replay time/date recovery, 7m → catch 10 appointment times → retest 5 appointments.
- `hoeren:announcements`: listen to 6 slow station/office announcements → write place/time/action → retest 3 new announcements.
- `hoeren:audio_to_form`: listen to 5 personal-info clips → fill form fields → retest 2 new forms.

### Lesen recovery

Use when tags include `lesen:*`.

Default prescription:

1. Watch scanning strategy clip, 5–8m.
2. Do one timed reading drill of the same type.
3. Retest same reading type with new text.

Examples:

- `lesen:signs`: match 15 signs to situations → retest 5 signs in 5 minutes.
- `lesen:ads`: underline need words first, then match 8 ads → retest 4 ad matches.
- `lesen:emails`: answer who/when/where/action for 5 short messages → retest 3 messages.
- `lesen:forms`: identify field labels in 4 forms → retest 1 complete form.

### Schreiben recovery

Use when tags include `schreiben:*`.

Default prescription:

1. Rebuild one model answer from chunks.
2. Write one new answer using a strict checklist.
3. Manual/rubric score before retest.

Examples:

- `schreiben:three_points`: watch 3-point message recovery, 6m → mark the three required points → write a 30-word SMS → retest with a new prompt.
- `schreiben:greeting_closing`: copy 4 correct openings/closings → repair 6 wrong messages → retest one message.
- `schreiben:form_fields`: fill 3 forms from data cards → fill 1 audio form → retest form.
- `schreiben:word_order`: repair 10 sentences with verb position → write 5 A1 sentences → retest message sentence order.

### Sprechen recovery

Use when tags include `sprechen:*`.

Default prescription:

1. Listen to model audio.
2. Shadow 5–10 lines.
3. Record/say the target answer again.
4. Retest with a new card.

Examples:

- `sprechen:self_intro`: shadow model intro, 5m → record own 60-second intro → retest prompt cards.
- `sprechen:spelling`: alphabet micro-drill → spell own name + email → retest with one unfamiliar name.
- `sprechen:question_answer`: drill 10 W-question cards → answer aloud → retest 5 random cards.
- `sprechen:request_phrase`: practice `Können Sie...?`, `Ich hätte gern...`, `Bitte...` → retest 5 situation cards.
- `sprechen:formality`: repair `du/Sie` choices in 8 scenes → retest formal office/Goethe scene.

### Grammar recovery

Use grammar as support, not a separate textbook detour.

Default prescription:

1. Watch one micro-lesson for the exact grammar tag, max 8m.
2. Repair 10 sentences.
3. Use the pattern in one speaking or writing output.
4. Retest inside a real A1 task.

Examples:

- `grammar:verb_position`: repair `Ich Deutsch lerne` → `Ich lerne Deutsch` → write 5 personal sentences.
- `grammar:articles`: sort 20 high-frequency nouns by article → use 8 in sentences.
- `grammar:modal_word_order`: repair `Ich kann einen Termin machen` patterns → role-play appointment request.
- `grammar:accusative_survival`: repair `ein/eine/einen` only for common ordering/shopping phrases.

### Vocab recovery

Vocab recovery must force recall and production, not passive re-reading.

Default prescription:

1. Review 15–25 tagged words with audio.
2. Do active recall without looking.
3. Use 8 words in spoken or written sentences.
4. Retest with mixed old/new items.

Examples:

- `vocab:numbers_time`: hear → write → say 20 numbers/times.
- `vocab:food_shopping`: choose items from a shopping list, then say an order.
- `vocab:travel_health`: match picture/situation to phrase, then say a request.
- `vocab:official_exam`: fill forms/notices using exact labels.

## 8. Public-resource curation rules

Public resources are allowed but must be assigned like medicine, not dumped like a library.

### Allowed resource types

- One selected public YouTube lesson for a specific weakness.
- One public Goethe-style sample/worksheet for a specific exam task.
- One slow German audio clip or playlist segment for a specific Hören tag.
- One printable/form worksheet for forms, signs, ads, or writing.
- One speaking prompt/card set for partner or teacher practice.

### Assignment rules

1. Assign at most one external resource at a time.
2. Every resource must have a target tag, expected output, and time box.
3. The app must say exactly what to do after the resource.
4. Never show more than three booster options on a page, and default to one.
5. Do not send learners to a broad channel, playlist, or search query without a bounded task.
6. Public resources must not contradict Adipoli wording, A1 level, or Goethe format.
7. If a public resource is longer than 20 minutes, assign only the exact timestamp range.
8. If a resource is in English/Hindi/German, the app must still frame the task in Manglish-friendly Adipoli language.

### Good assignments

- `Watch 06:10–13:45 of the selected number-listening video. Then write 15 heard numbers. 15m.`
- `Do Goethe-style signs worksheet 1, questions 1–5 only. Time limit: 6m. Mark wrong answers with lesen:signs.`
- `Listen to this slow appointment audio twice. Fill time/date/place. Then retest 5 new appointments.`

### Bad assignments

- `Watch these 12 YouTube videos.`
