## The "Golden Restaurant Dialogue" — Module 6 Centerpiece

This dialogue (taught in v6-3-1) is the module's signature output. Students must be able to reproduce this pattern:

```
Kellner: Guten Tag! Was möchten Sie trinken?
Gast: Einen Kaffee, bitte.
Kellner: Und zum Essen?
Gast: Ich nehme eine Suppe, bitte.
Kellner: Gern. Noch etwas?
Gast: Nein, danke. Was kostet das?
Kellner: Acht Euro fünfzig.
Gast: Hier, bitte. Danke schön!
```

### Key Ordering Patterns to Drill
- `Ich nehme + ACCUSATIVE` (einen Kaffee, eine Suppe, ein Wasser)
- `Ich hätte gern + ACCUSATIVE` (formal/polite)
- `Ich möchte + ACCUSATIVE` (common but slightly less polite)
- `Was kostet...?` / `Was macht das?`
- Polite `bitte` placement: after the item or at end of sentence

### Accusative Article Quick-Reference (Food Context)
```
NOMINATIV → AKKUSATIV (only masculine changes!)
der Kaffee → Ich nehme einen Kaffee.
die Suppe → Ich nehme eine Suppe.
das Wasser → Ich nehme ein Wasser.

RULE: Only DER changes (der → den/einen). Die and das stay the same.
"Nur der Mann ändert sich!" — a memory trick that works. 😄
```

## Common Malayali Learner Errors to Script Against

1. **Dropping accusative change:** "Ich nehme ein Kaffee" instead of "einen Kaffee" — THE #1 error in this module
2. **"gern" placement:** "Ich gern esse" instead of "Ich esse gern" — gern goes AFTER the verb
3. **English food words leaking in:** "Ich möchte rice" instead of "Reis" — drill German food words with pictures
4. **Price confusion:** "drei fünfzig" — is it €3.50 or €350? Always "drei Euro fünfzig" for clarity
5. **Too polite (Indian style):** Overly long phrases when "Einen Kaffee, bitte" is perfectly sufficient and natural
6. **Forgetting articles entirely:** German food ALWAYS has an article; memorize der/die/das WITH the word
7. **"scharf" confusion:** In German, "scharf" means spicy AND hot (temperature) AND sharp — context matters
8. **Vegetarian vocabulary gaps:** "Ich bin vegetarisch" (wrong — that describes the food) vs "Ich bin Vegetarier/Vegetarierin" (correct — that describes you)
9. **Mixing möchten/mögen:** "Ich möge Kaffee" — at A1, stick with "Ich möchte" (I would like) and "Ich trinke gern" (I like to drink)

## Malayalam Bridge Points

- **Food as emotional anchor:** "Nammude food nammude identity aanu! German-il food describe cheyyaan padikkumbol, nee ninte culture share cheyyananu. Germans will LOVE hearing about Kerala food."
- **Spice vocabulary is borrowed:** "Kurkuma, Kardamom, Ingwer, Zimt — German-il ee words und! They came from OUR languages into theirs."
- **Rice culture:** "Reis ist in Kerala staple food — and actually, Germans eat quite a lot of Reis too! 'Reis mit Soße' is very common."
- **Chai/Tee bridge:** "Chai ennu paranjaal Germans-ku manassilaakum — 'Chai-Tee' is sold everywhere in Germany! But ee 'Chai Latte' nammal kaanung Chai alla 😄"
- **Vegetarian in Germany:** "Good news: Germany has EXCELLENT vegetarian food now. 'Ich bin Vegetarier' — ee vakku Germany-il every restaurant-il manasilaakkum."
- **Tipping culture:** "In Kerala, tipping is optional. In Germany, you round up or add 5-10%. 'Stimmt so!' means 'keep the change.' Very useful phrase!"
- **German meals vs Kerala meals:** "Germans eat a big Frühstück (breakfast), a warm Mittagessen (lunch), and often a cold Abendessen (dinner — just bread + cheese). Kerala-il nammude pattern different aanu!"
