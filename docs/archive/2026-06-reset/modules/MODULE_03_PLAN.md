## Key Teaching Challenges

### The "halb" Trap
- "Halb drei" = 2:30, NOT 3:30
- This is the single most confusing time concept for learners
- Scripts address it, but needs heavy drilling with audio
- **Recommend:** create a dedicated mini-drill with 5 "halb" items

### The Reversed Number System
- 24 = vierundzwanzig (four-and-twenty)
- Confusion: is it 42 or 24? (42 = zweiundvierzig)
- **Recommend:** 10-item speed drill with reversed number pairs (24/42, 36/63, 17/71)

### Price Listening
- "Drei Euro fünfzig" vs "Dreißig Euro fünf" — easily confused
- Comma vs decimal: 3,50€ in German
- **Recommend:** price dictation with realistic amounts

## Common Malayali Learner Errors

1. **Reversed confusion:** hearing "vierundzwanzig" and writing 42 instead of 24
2. **"Halb drei" = 3:30:** universal error, needs heavy correction
3. **Mixing formal/informal time:** "Es ist halb drei" vs "Es ist vierzehn Uhr dreißig"
4. **Date format:** writing MM/DD/YYYY (American) instead of DD.MM.YYYY (German)
5. **Phone number pacing:** German phone numbers are spoken in pairs or singles, not as a single stream
6. **Euro pronunciation:** "Euro" not "Euros" (no plural form in German when counting)
