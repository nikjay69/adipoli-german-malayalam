## Module 4: My Family & People (Meine Familie)

**Scripts:** ✅ v4-1-1 (Family Members), v4-2-1 (Describing People), v4-3-1 (Possessives) — ALL COMPLETE

### What's Unique About Module 4
- First module where articles become unavoidable (der Vater, die Mutter, das Kind)
- Possessives (mein/meine) are introduced and must stick
- Describing people = adjective + noun agreement awareness begins

### Key Production Exercises Needed
1. **4-P1: Family Description** — "Describe your family in 4–5 sentences" (free-text)
2. **4-P2: Possessive Transformation** — Complete with mein/meine/dein/deine (guided)
3. **4-P3: Family Presentation** — 1-min speaking about one family member

### Specific Teaching Risk
- If articles don't start becoming habitual here, every subsequent module suffers
- **Recommend:** Each family word ALWAYS appears with its article in exercises (der Bruder, never just "Bruder")

### Priority: MEDIUM (supports Sprechen but not directly exam-tested as a topic)

## Module 7: Shopping & Money (Einkaufen)

**Scripts:** ✅ v7-1-1 (Supermarket), v7-2-1 (Prices & Currency), v7-3-1 (Colors & Shopping) — ALL COMPLETE

### What's Unique About Module 7
- Reinforces numbers (M3) and accusative (M6) in a new context
- Price listening is HEAVILY tested in Hören
- Shopping dialogues require productive politeness
- Pfand system, Sunday closing, and checkout culture = German life

### Key Production Exercises Needed
1. **7-P1: Shopping Dialogue** — Write a dialogue buying a shirt (free-text)
2. **7-P2: Price Dictation** — 10 prices from audio (dictation)
3. **7-P3: Shopping List** — Write 8 items with articles (free-text)

### Specific Teaching Risk
- Price listening confusion: "drei fünfzig" (3.50) vs "dreißig fünf" (35)
- **Recommend:** Price dictation is the FIRST audio exercise to build for this module

### Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH (price listening is exam-critical)

## Module 8: My Home (Meine Wohnung)

**Scripts:** ✅ v8-1-1 (Rooms & Furniture), v8-2-1 (Describing Home), v8-3-1 (Wohnungsanzeigen) — ALL COMPLETE

### What's Unique About Module 8
- Apartment ads (Wohnungsanzeigen) are a CLASSIC Goethe A1 Lesen item
- Prepositions of location (auf, unter, neben, in, vor, hinter) introduced
- Short message writing (to landlord/flatmate) supports Schreiben

### Key Production Exercises Needed
1. **8-P1: Room Description** — Describe your room in 4–5 sentences (free-text)
2. **8-P2: Housing Ad Response** — Write a short message to a landlord (free-text)
3. **8-P3: Object Location Dictation** — Place objects based on audio instructions

### Specific Teaching Risk
- Wohnungsanzeige abbreviations (Zi. = Zimmer, NK = Nebenkosten, KM = Kaltmiete) must be explicitly taught
- **Recommend:** Create 3 realistic housing ads with comprehension questions

### Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH (Lesen exam relevance through apartment ads)

## Module 10: Health & Body (Gesundheit)

**Scripts:** ✅ v10-1-1 (Body Parts), v10-2-1 (Symptoms), v10-3-1 (Doctor Dialogue) — ALL COMPLETE

### What's Unique About Module 10
- Doctor dialogue is a standard A1 practical scenario
- "Tut weh" / "Ich habe ... Schmerzen" / "mir ist schlecht" — critical patterns
- Absence message (Schreiben) is a natural output
- Nursing/care worker relevance is HIGH for Malayali audience

### Key Production Exercises Needed
1. **10-P1: Doctor Dialogue** — Write a dialogue explaining 2 symptoms (free-text)
2. **10-P2: Sick Note Message** — Write to teacher/boss saying you're sick (free-text)
3. **10-P3: Symptom Dictation** — Write symptoms from audio

### Specific Teaching Risk
- Students overcomplicate symptom descriptions beyond A1
- **Recommend:** Model answers should be SHORT: "Ich habe Kopfschmerzen. Mein Bauch tut weh." — not medical German

### Priority: MEDIUM (practical relevance high, exam relevance medium)

## Module 12: Hobbies & Free Time (Freizeit)

**Scripts:** ✅ v12-1-1 (Hobbies), v12-2-1 (Weather), v12-3-1 (Plans), v12-4-1 (Invitations), v12-4-2 (Weil-Clauses) — ALL 5 COMPLETE

### What's Unique About Module 12
- Invitation writing is THE classic Goethe A1 Schreiben Teil 2 task
- "gern" placement is tested throughout Sprechen
- Weather small talk is German social currency
- Plan-making combines time (M3) + activity (M5) + preferences (M6)

### Key Production Exercises Needed
1. **12-P1: Invitation Message** — Write a birthday party invitation (free-text) — **EXAM CRITICAL**
2. **12-P2: Decline with Reason** — Decline politely and give a reason (free-text)
3. **12-P3: Hobby Presentation Speaking** — 1-min speaking about hobbies

### Specific Teaching Risk
- Invitation writing seems casual but has strict content requirements in the exam
- **Recommend:** Drill the 3-content-point formula hard: "Your message MUST include: (1) what, (2) when, (3) where/request"

### Priority: HIGH (invitation writing = direct Schreiben Teil 2 exam prep)

## Module 13: Talking About the Past (Was hast du gemacht?)

**Scripts:** ✅ v13-1-1 (Perfekt + haben), v13-2-1 (Perfekt + sein), v13-3-1 (Irregular Participles), v13-4-1 (Weekend Stories) — ALL 4 COMPLETE

### What's Unique About Module 13
- Perfekt tense is the biggest grammar milestone after present tense
- haben vs sein auxiliary choice is the main error source
- Irregular past participles need memorization (no shortcut)
- Weekend storytelling is a Sprechen topic classic

### Key Production Exercises Needed
1. **13-P1: Weekend Story** — Write 5 sentences about last weekend in Perfekt (free-text)
2. **13-P2: Present → Perfekt Transformation** — Rewrite 4 sentences (guided)
3. **13-P3: Weekend Dictation** — Write a short weekend narration from audio

### Specific Teaching Risk
- Students forget Perfekt within 2 weeks if not recycled
- **Recommend:** Module 17/18 exercises should include past-tense items to force recall

### Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH (Perfekt appears in Sprechen, Hören, and Schreiben)
