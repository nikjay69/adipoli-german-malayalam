## 1. Course goal

Adipoli German should take a Malayali beginner from zero German to confident Goethe A1 readiness through a complete online app experience that feels alive, useful, engaging, and worth paying for.

The promise is not “learn random German words” or “read some lessons.” The promise is:

> Start in Kerala, follow Kuttan’s journey inside a self-sufficient web app, learn usable A1 German through Manglish-friendly teaching, recorded/video lessons, guided practice, review, checkpoints, and become ready to handle Goethe A1 listening, reading, writing, and speaking.

The pass target from the planning docs is ambitious: a student using only this course should have a strong chance of passing Goethe A1. Therefore every module must move from passive exposure toward real performance, and the app must guide the learner without Boss manually explaining what to do next.

## 2. Current MVP lock — 2026-06-02

Boss reset the direction: **MVP course first, games later.** The product should not be sold as a games app or an AI gimmick. It should be a guided A1 course that teaches, tests, diagnoses, and prescribes exactly what to do next.

### MVP promise

> A spoon-fed A1 German course for Malayali learners: ~35h of our own guided video lessons, phone-first practice, closed checkpoints, exact recovery tasks, and Goethe A1 exam readiness.

### Product structure

- **Core video course:** target ~35h of Adipoli-owned lessons. 30h is possible but tight; 35h is the recommended sweet spot. Avoid bloating to 40h unless a module genuinely needs it.
- **Must-do path:** the exact minimum sequence to pass A1 if completed seriously: video → vocab → speaking/listening/writing practice → closed test → recovery task if weak.
- **Score-booster path:** optional but still spoon-fed: selected external YouTube/audio/worksheets, harder mocks, extra speaking/writing variants.
- **External resources:** allowed, even competitor/public YouTube content, but only as assigned reinforcement. Never dump a resource list and ask learners to figure it out.
- **Games:** postponed. Use later as retention/remediation, not as the MVP backbone.
- **Mobile + desktop:** both must work; phone is the default learner device.

### Intelligent checkpoint spine

Use tests as the adaptive engine:

1. Small lesson checks after lessons.
2. Module diagnostic tests.
3. Goethe-style checkpoints every few modules.
4. Final A1 mock/bootcamp.

Tests must be closed-book: tell learners not to use Google, YouTube, ChatGPT, dictionaries, or notes. The point is diagnosis, not cheating a score.

Diagnostics should classify weaknesses by tag:

- `hoeren` — numbers, slow audio, question words, detail extraction.
- `lesen` — forms, notices, short messages, scanning.
- `schreiben` — SMS/email structure, verb position, missing required info.
- `sprechen` — self-intro, spelling, question response, incomplete answers.
- `grammar` — verb position, articles, pronouns, negation, modal basics.
- `vocab` — module-specific recall gaps.

After a test, prescribe exact work:

- **Must-do recovery:** the smallest required remedial set before continuing.
- **Score booster:** extra guided material for stronger marks.

Manual/human answer coding is acceptable for MVP, especially for writing/speaking. The system can feel intelligent with rubrics + tags + recovery prescriptions. AI may later assist with writing checks, transcription, tag suggestions, and feedback, but AI is optional infrastructure — not the learner-facing promise.

## 3. Course soul

This is the core identity to protect — but not blindly. The course goal wins over any mascot, story device, or prior idea.

- **Kerala-rooted:** the learner’s world is the bridge into German.
- **Story-driven:** Kuttan is useful only if he makes adult learners feel accompanied, not patronized.
- **Manglish-friendly:** explanations should feel like a Malayali teacher / older cousin, not a textbook.
- **Exam-serious:** warmth and humor are allowed, but the final output must prepare for Goethe A1.
- **Production-first:** students must speak, write, listen, and recall — not just choose options.
- **Immersion over chore-clicking:** sentence builders, chip placement, undo/clear loops, 0-of-N counters, and tap-heavy mini-games are scaffolds only — not the main product feel. The core path should feel like being inside a short German moment: listen, understand, respond, repair, win.
- **Phone-first production:** do not make early learners do long typing or fussy builders on mobile. Prefer speaking aloud, listening/shadowing, short tap-to-complete repairs, tiny optional text entry, and exam-relevant writing only when it clearly earns the effort.
- **Dialogue-scene-first:** if a learner must read paragraphs to understand what is happening, the UX has failed. Prefer two-person visual scenes with speech/audio turns (`Frau Weber` says one line → learner replies) over pages, cards, dashboards, or written explanations. Text should mostly be captions/subtitles for a lived moment.
- **Pedagogically intentional:** every tap must have a learning reason: hear meaning, notice form, say it, repair a mistake, or prove a Goethe A1 skill. Do not add pages, games, or interactions because they look polished.
- **App-first:** this is a full web product, not a pile of scripts; routes, videos, exercises, games, review, progress, pricing, and visual UX all matter.
- **Self-sufficient:** the app must tell learners what to do next through onboarding, daily plan, lesson flow, practice, checkpoints, and remediation.
- **Engagement-first:** no boring dead moments; every lesson and screen must earn attention and make learners want to continue.
- **Retention-first:** the course should create “one more lesson” pull through tiny wins, emotional story beats, review loops, and visible progress.
- **Premium-feeling at low price:** keep the product affordable for scale, but make the experience feel trustworthy, polished, and worth more than it costs.
- **A1-safe:** short, correct, high-probability German beats fancy German.

If a lesson could be dropped into any generic app by replacing German with Spanish, it is not Adipoli enough. If a lesson is correct but boring, it is still not good enough.

### Challenge rule

Do not assume every existing idea is correct just because it is documented. Challenge course decisions against the real goal: adult Malayali learners passing Goethe A1 and feeling confident about Germany.

Especially audit:

- **Kuttan tone:** peer/older-cousin learner is good; childish mascot/protagonist is bad.
- **Story density:** story should create memory and motivation, not slow down serious learners.
- **Humor:** light Manglish warmth is good; cringe comedy is bad.
- **Germany scenes:** learner imagination and future-goal rehearsal are useful; breaking the A1 Kerala arc is bad.
- **Exam seriousness:** any story or game that does not lead to Hören/Lesen/Schreiben/Sprechen output is decoration.

The right standard is not “preserve Kuttan at all costs.” The right standard is: make the course feel distinctive, adult-safe, culturally rooted, and exam-effective.

## 4. Narrative canon

Canonical source: `docs/A1_STORY_BIBLE.md`

Rules:

- A1 happens physically in Kerala.
- Kuttan is in Kerala until the final airport departure.
- Germany can appear as dream, goal, cousin video call, imagined learner scenario, or exam/life rehearsal.
- Kuttan must not casually be placed in Berlin, Frankfurt, Munich, German WGs, German supermarkets, or German universities during A1.
- Appu is a silent UI mascot, not a speaking story character.

Important nuance:

- “Imagine you are at a bakery in Berlin…” is fine as a learner exercise.
- “Kuttan walks through Berlin Hauptbahnhof…” is not fine in A1.

## 6. Quality target

The course must evolve from:

> expose → explain → quiz

into:

> story encounter → hear → respond aloud → light check/repair → tiny write only when valuable → review → checkpoint → exam confidence

Main exam-readiness gaps:

1. Real Hören/audio-first practice
2. Production exercises: speaking, free-text, dictation
3. Goethe checkpoints and mock-test gates
4. SRS/review loop
5. Script/app wiring consistency
6. German/script QC: no wrong labels, no canon drift, no generic filler
