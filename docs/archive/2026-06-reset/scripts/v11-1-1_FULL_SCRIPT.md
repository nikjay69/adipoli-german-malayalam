## Section 2: The One Big Rule — Feminine Usually Adds `-in` (0:45–2:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Masculine → Feminine pairs building one by one, `-in` highlighted]
### Narration: [ENERGY: simple, structured]
"The best news first: German profession words follow one very useful pattern.

### Core rule
- **der Lehrer** → **die Lehrerin**
- **der Student** → **die Studentin**
- **der Ingenieur** → **die Ingenieurin**
- **der Verkäufer** → **die Verkäuferin**

So the beginner rule is:
**Learn the base form. Add `-in` for many feminine forms.**

### Two common umlaut changes
- **der Arzt** → **die Ärztin**
- **der Koch** → **die Köchin**

Don't memorize 50 exceptions today. Learn the main pattern first.

*Malayalam-il adhyaapakan / adhyaapika pole oru feeling undu. Same idea: profession word changes for gender.*

⏸️ Feminine of **der Student**?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**die Studentin.**

⏸️ Feminine of **der Arzt**?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**die Ärztin.**"

## Section 4: The 3 Best Ways to Say Your Profession (3:45–5:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: 3 speaking patterns with one model under each]
### Narration: [ENERGY: teachable, exam-focused]
"Now the real output. Three patterns are enough.

### Pattern 1 — Fastest
**Ich bin Krankenpflegerin.**

### Pattern 2 — Slightly more formal
**Ich bin Ingenieur von Beruf.**

### Pattern 3 — Good for work context
**Ich arbeite als Programmiererin.**

### Important A1 note
With professions, German often drops the article in these statements.
- ✅ **Ich bin Arzt.**
- ✅ **Ich bin Studentin.**
- Avoid teaching yourself heavy article habits here.

[EXAM-TIP] For **Sprechen Teil 1**, Pattern 1 and Pattern 2 are safest.

### Drill — predict before reveal
You are a female student. Say it with Pattern 1.
⏸️

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**Ich bin Studentin.**

You are a male engineer. Say it with Pattern 2.
⏸️

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**Ich bin Ingenieur von Beruf.**

You work as a programmer. Pattern 3.
⏸️

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**Ich arbeite als Programmierer / Programmiererin.**"

## Section 6: Forms vs Speaking — One Word, Two Uses (6:45–7:45)

### Visual: [FORM: field `Beruf` highlighted on left; speaking prompt on right]
### Narration: [ENERGY: practical, exam-useful]
"This word appears in two places.

### On forms
You see: **Beruf:** ______
Write only the profession.
- **Studentin**
- **Krankenpfleger**
- **Ingenieurin**

No full sentence needed.

### In speaking
The examiner asks, and YOU make the sentence.
- **Ich bin Studentin.**
- **Ich arbeite als Altenpfleger.**

[EXAM-TIP] In **Schreiben Teil 1**, if the form says `Beruf`, keep it short. One correct word is enough.

⏸️ Form mode: if the field says `Beruf`, what do you write?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

Just the word. Not a sentence."

## Section 7: Common Mistakes Malayali Learners Make (7:45–8:45)

### Visual: [SLIDE: 4 mistakes with ❌ → ✅]
### Narration: [ENERGY: preventive, sharp]
"Four mistakes to kill today.

### Mistake 1
❌ Using the wrong gender form
Female student? Not **Student**. **Studentin.**

### Mistake 2
❌ Forgetting `-in`
**Ingenieur** → **Ingenieurin**
**Lehrer** → **Lehrerin**

### Mistake 3
❌ Panicking at umlaut forms
Yes, **Arzt → Ärztin** and **Koch → Köchin** change slightly. Learn these as special pairs.

### Mistake 4
❌ Giving a long life story instead of a profession
If asked **Was sind Sie von Beruf?**, don't start with your whole migration journey. Give the job title first.

*Question small aanenkil answer-um first small aakanam. Pinne detail add cheyyaam.*"
