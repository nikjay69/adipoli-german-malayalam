## Section 2: Building the Sentence — Statement Rule (0:45–2:30)

### Visual: [FORMULA: Person + [conjugated verb] + ... + [prefix]. Examples: Ich kaufe ... ein. Ich rufe ... an.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: steady, instructional]
"In a normal statement, the main part of the verb stays in its usual home (Position 2), and the prefix goes to the absolute **END**.

[Examples appear]
1. 🔊 **einkaufen** (to shop):
   **'Ich kaufe am Samstag ein.'**
2. 🔊 **anrufen** (to call):
   **'Ich rufe meine Mutter an.'**
3. 🔊 **aufräumen** (to tidy up):
   **'Ich räume mein Zimmer auf.'**

*Suffix-ne end-il e-dhirunnu focus cheyyanam. Ithu nannaayi focus cheyyuka.*

⏸ **Try: I shop!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Ich kaufe ein.** ✅

⏸ **I call!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Ich rufe an.** ✅"

---

## Section 3: The 'Not' Rule — Negation (2:30–4:30)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: "nicht" placement. Example: "Ich sehe heute NICHT fern." Arrows showing prefix as the absolute LAST word.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"What if you're *not* doing something? Where does **'nicht'** go?

Rule: The prefix is the absolute LAST thing in the sentence. Everything else, including 'nicht', comes before it.

[Example]
🔊 **'Ich sehe heute nicht fern.'**
🔊 **'Ich kaufe am Sonntag nicht ein.'**

*Rule: Prefix-ine end-il thanne vekkanam. 'Nicht' sentence-il center-il varum.*

⏸️ **Try: I don't tidy up today.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Ich räume heute nicht auf.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: The Question Curve — Asking Others (4:30–6:30)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Verb + [Subject] + ... + [prefix]. Example: "Kaufst du heute ein?". Word order swap animation.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: attentive, instructional]
"Asking a question? The verb takes the lead, but the prefix stays loyal to the end!

[Logic cards]
🔊 **'Kaufst du heute ein?'**
🔊 **'Siehst du abends fern?'**
🔊 **'Räumst du dein Zimmer auf?'**

*Question-il verb first-il varum, pakshe prefix end-il thanne nilkkum!*

⏸️ **Ask: Do you call? (anrufen)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Rufst du an?** ✅"

---

## Section 5: Malayalam Logic — Verb Clusters (6:30–8:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Malayalam 'Njan shopping cheyyunnu' vs German 'Ich kaufe ein'.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, structural]
"In Malayalam, we often use clusters like 'shopping cheyyunnu' or 'clean akkunnu'.

German separable verbs (trennbare Verben) do the same. They combine two meanings (like 'fern' = far, 'sehen' = see) into one new word. The only difference is that German physically splits them in the sentence.

*Nammal 'shopping cheyyilla' ennu parayum pole, German-il 'kaufe ... nicht ein' ennu parayaam! Same energy.*

[EXAM-TIP] In the **Sprechen** (speaking) part, use **'mitkommen'** (to come along). It's a gold mine. Ask the examiner: 'Kommen Sie mit?' — it shows you can handle separable verbs in questions!"

## Section 6: Speed Challenge — Boomerang Blitz (8:00–10:00)

### Visual: [FLASHCARDS: Verb + Scenario. Student builds the full sentence.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, rapid-fire]
"Rapid fire. Statement or Question?

⚡ I + watch TV? → **Ich sehe fern!**
⚡ You + shop (No)? → **Du kaufst nicht ein!**
⚡ I + tidy up? → **Ich räume auf!**
⚡ Do you + call? → **Rufst du an?**
⚡ He + comes along? → **Er kommt mit!**
⚡ We + watch TV (No)? → **Wir sehen nicht fern!**

Excellent! If you didn't forget the prefix at the end, you've completed this level."
