## Section 2: Exam Map — 3 Parts, 3 Reading Jobs (0:45–1:25)

### Visual: [TABLE: Lesen overview]
| Teil | What you read | What you do | Main skill |
|------|---------------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | Signs / notices / short public texts | Match a situation to the right text | Spot purpose fast |
| 2 | Ads / short info | Match a person's need to the right offer | Compare needs with details |
| 3 | Email / message / short correspondence | Answer comprehension questions | Find exact information |

### Narration: [ENERGY: structured]
"Teil 1 asks: **Which sign fits this situation?**
Teil 2 asks: **Which offer fits this person?**
Teil 3 asks: **What exactly does this message say?**

Three parts. Three different reading habits.

[EXAM-TIP] Always read the **task** before the text. If you don't know what you're searching for, every sentence feels important."

## Section 3: Teil 1 — Signs and Situations (1:25–3:15)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Teil 1 — Situation first, sign second"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: teaching, crisp]
"Teil 1 gives you short real-life texts: signs, office notices, shop information, station messages.

The trap is obvious: students read the sign first because it's short.

Better strategy: **read the situation first**.
Then scan signs for the matching purpose.

Let's do one.

### Visual: [Situation card]
**Situation:** 'Sie möchten am Samstag Bücher ausleihen.'

### Visual: [Notice]
```
STADTBIBLIOTHEK
Mo–Fr 9:00–18:00
Samstag geschlossen
```

⏸️ **Does this fit the situation?** (3s)

✅ **No.** Saturday is closed.

You did not need every word. You needed only:
- Samstag
- geschlossen

Now one more.

### Visual: [Situation card]
**Situation:** 'Sie brauchen heute Abend ein günstiges Abendessen.'

### Visual: [Notice]
```
BISTRO CITY
Heute ab 18 Uhr
Pasta-Abend
Nur 6,50 €
```

⏸️ **Fit or not fit?** (3s)

✅ **Yes.** Today evening, food, low price.

*Kerala-il board nokki 'open', 'closed', 'offer', 'today' enna key words mathram pidichu decision edukkunna pole thanne.*

Teil 1 is not literature. It's survival reading."

---

## Section 4: Teil 1 Rule — Check the 4 Fastest Clues (3:15–3:50)

### Visual: [SLIDE: 4 clue icons]
### Narration: [ENERGY: brisk]
"In signs and notices, check these four clues first:

- **day / date**
- **time**
- **place**
- **price / restriction**

⏸️ If a sign says 'Max. 2 pro Person' and your situation needs five tickets — fit or not fit? (2s)

✅ **Not fit.** Restriction kills the match.

That's Teil 1 in one line: situation first, then scan for the deal-breaker."

---

## Section 5: Teil 2 — Match People to Ads or Offers (3:50–5:50)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Teil 2 — Need first, offer second"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: practical, methodical]
"Teil 2 usually gives you people with specific needs — and several ads, notices, or short offers.

Your job is not: 'What does this whole ad mean?'
Your job is: 'Which offer solves this person's problem?'

### Visual: [Person need card]
**Person:** 'Asha sucht einen Deutschkurs am Abend für Anfänger.'

Before you read any ad, underline the need words:
- **Deutschkurs**
- **am Abend**
- **für Anfänger**

Now look at an offer.

### Visual: [Ad]
```
Deutsch lernen im Zentrum!
Anfängerkurs A1
Mo und Mi 18:30–20:00
Anmeldung online
```

⏸️ **Good match or bad match?** (3s)

✅ **Good match.** Course? yes. Beginner? yes. Evening? yes.

Now compare with this one:

### Visual: [Ad]
```
Intensivkurs Deutsch B1
Mo–Fr 9:00–12:00
```

⏸️ **Match?** (2s)

✅ **No.** Wrong level and wrong time.

[EXAM-TIP] In Teil 2, three mismatch reasons appear all the time:
- wrong **time**
- wrong **level / topic**
- wrong **location / condition**

Don't ask: 'Is this ad about courses?'  
Ask: 'Does it fit THIS person exactly?'"

---

## Section 6: Teil 2 Drill — Use Elimination, Not Hope (5:50–6:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: 4 ads, some crossed off]
### Narration: [ENERGY: game-like]
"Once an ad clearly fits one person, cross it off.

That shrinks the puzzle and saves time.

⏸️ If one person needs a weekend concert and the ad says 'Samstag, 19 Uhr, Eintritt 8 €' — what 3 clues make it fit? (3s)

✅ **Day, time, event type.**

Teil 2 rewards comparison skills — not perfect vocabulary."

---

## Section 7: Teil 3 — Emails and Messages (6:30–8:20)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Teil 3 — Find exact information in correspondence"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: careful, clear]
"Teil 3 gives you a short email, message, or letter.

Here the danger is different: students read the whole message like a story.

Better method:
1. read question 1
2. scan the text for the relevant sentence
3. answer
4. move to question 2

Let's practice.

### Visual: [Email]
```
Liebe Anna,

ich bin seit zwei Wochen in meiner neuen Wohnung in Hamburg.
Am Samstag mache ich eine kleine Einweihungsparty.
Komm bitte um 18 Uhr.
Kannst du einen Salat mitbringen?

Liebe Grüße
Sara
```

### Narration:
"Question 1: **Wo wohnt Sara jetzt?**

⏸️ (2s)

✅ **In Hamburg.**

Question 2: **Wann beginnt die Party?**

⏸️ (2s)

✅ **Um 18 Uhr.**

Question 3: **Was soll Anna mitbringen?**

⏸️ (2s)

✅ **Einen Salat.**

Notice what we did NOT do:
- we did not translate every sentence
- we did not summarize the whole email
- we just matched each question to one line

*Question-oru, answer-line-oru. Ithu master cheythaal Teil 3 slow aavilla.*"

---

## Section 8: The 4 Most Common Reading Traps (8:20–9:10)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Lesen traps"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: alert]
"Four traps appear again and again:

**Trap 1: same topic, wrong detail**  
It's about a course — but the time is wrong.

**Trap 2: number/date switch**  
18 Uhr in the text, 20 Uhr in the answer option.

**Trap 3: negation**  
'geschlossen', 'nicht', 'kein'.

**Trap 4: half match**  
Two details fit, one important detail does not.

⏸️ If a person needs an **evening** class and the ad is perfect except it runs at **9:00 morgens**, is it a match? (2s)

✅ **No.** Half-correct is still wrong."

---

## Section 9: Active Recall — Rebuild All 3 Strategies (9:10–10:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: three blank prompts]
### Narration: [ENERGY: challenging, encouraging]
"Let's lock it in.

**Teil 1:** what comes first — situation or sign?  
⏸️ (2s)

✅ **Situation first.**

**Teil 2:** what do you compare?  
⏸️ (3s)

✅ **Person's need vs ad details.**

**Teil 3:** how do you read the message?  
⏸️ (3s)

✅ **Question by question, not full translation first.**

Bonus: what's the fastest deal-breaker to check in any reading item?

⏸️ (2s)

✅ **Time, date, place, price, restriction.**"
