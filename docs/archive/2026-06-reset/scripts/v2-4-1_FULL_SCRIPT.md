## Section 3: The Verb 'sprechen' — Watch the Vowel! (2:15–4:30)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: The verb 'sprechen' with its full conjugation table. Red highlights for stem changes.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: warning but steady]
"The verb for 'to speak' is **sprechen**.

It almost follows our normal pattern from Module 5, but there's a small vowel trap. Look at the 'e' in **sprechen**. For **du** and **er/sie/es**, it changes from **e** to **i**.

[Conjugation table appears]
- **ich spreche**
- **du sprichst** 💡 *(e → i change!)*
- **er / sie / es spricht** 💡 *(e → i change!)*
- **wir sprechen**
- **ihr sprecht**
- **sie / Sie sprechen**

*Wait, 'ihr sprecht' stays with 'e'! It's only the small subjects (du, er, sie, es) that change to 'i'.*

⏸️ **Quick Drill: He speaks?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**er spricht.** ✅

⏸️ **I speak?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**ich spreche.** ✅

⏸️ **You speak (formal)?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Sie sprechen.** ✅"

## Section 4: How Well Do You Speak? — Proficiency Levels (4:30–6:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Meter showing percentage of fluency from 10% to 100%. Three key terms popping up.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: practical, precise]
"Just saying 'I speak' is often not enough. You want to say 'how well'.

[Key terms appear]
- **ein bisschen** — a little. (Ich spreche ein bisschen Deutsch.)
- **gut** — well. (Ich spreche gut Englisch.)
- **fließend** — fluently. (Ich spreche fließend Malayalam.)
- **Muttersprache** — mother tongue. *(Malayalam ist meine Muttersprache.)*

*Note: 'ein bisschen' is your best friend when you move to Germany. It's polite, honest, and people will love you for trying!*

⏸️ **Say it: I speak a little German!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Ich spreche ein bisschen Deutsch.** ✅"
