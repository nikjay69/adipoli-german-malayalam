## Section 1: Hook — The Preference Puzzle (0:15–0:50)

### Visual: [SCENE: A menu. Student thinks "I like coffee" (General) vs. "I want a coffee" (Now). Icons: Heart ❤️ (mögen) and Hand pointing ☝️ (möchten).]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, engaging]
"In English, we have 'I like' and 'I want'. In German, we have a few ways to say what we enjoy.

Imagine you're at a party. You like beer (in general) but right now you want a water! You need two different words for that.

Today, we're sorting out **'mögen'**, **'möchten'**, and our old friend **'gern'**. Don't worry, we'll make it as simple as a plate of Appam and Stew! Let's start the comparison!"

---

## Section 2: Method 1 — "Ich mag..." (General Likes) (0:50–2:30)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: mögen (to like/love). Table: ich mag | du magst | er/sie/es mag | wir mögen... Word 'general' highlighted.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: steady, instructional]
"**'mögen'** is for your hobbies, your food loves, and your general feelings.

[Examples]
1. 🔊 **'Ich mag Pizza.'** — *I like pizza (anytime).*
2. 🔊 **'Ich mag Kerala.'** — *I like/love Kerala.*
3. 🔊 **'Magst du Curry?'** — *Do you like curry (in general)?*

*Mögen-nu ich-um er-um oru pole 'mag' aanu. Ithu ororthu vekkanam!*

⏸️ **Try: I like music.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Ich mag Musik.** ✅"

---

## Section 3: Method 2 — "Ich möchte..." (Polite Wish) (2:30–4:30)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: möchten (would like). Icon: Order at a restaurant. Caption: "Right now!"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"Now, you're at a cafe. You don't want to talk about your whole life. You want a drink **right now**.

Use 🔊 **'Ich möchte...'** (I would like).

[Examples]
🔊 **'Ich möchte einen Kaffee, bitte.'** — *I'd like a coffee, please.*
🔊 **'Möchten Sie den Fisch?'** — *Would you like the fish (on the menu)?*

*Difference: 'Ich mag Kaffee' (I am a coffee person) vs 'Ich möchte einen Kaffee' (Give me one coffee, please!).*

⏸️ **Say: I'd like a water.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Ich möchte ein Wasser.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: Method 3 — "gern" (Action Likes) (4:30–6:00)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: gern (gladly). Formula: Verb + gern. Icon: A person eating happily.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"If you want to say you like **doing** an action, use 🔊 **'gern'**.

[Examples]
🔊 **'Ich esse gern Curry.'** — *I like eating curry.*
🔊 **'Ich trinke gern Tee.'** — *I like drinking tea.*

*Note: 'mögen' uses a noun (Ich mag Fleisch). 'gern' uses a verb (Ich esse gern Fleisch). Both mean 'I like it'!*

⏸️ **Try: I like cooking. (Ich koche ...)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Ich koche gern.** ✅"

---

## Section 5: Malayalam Logic — The "Ishtam" Connection (6:00–8:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Malayalam 'Ennikku ... ishtamaanu' vs German 'Ich mag ...' / 'Ich esse gern ...'.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, structural]
"In Malayalam, we use 'Ennikku ishtamaanu' for everything — likes, wants, hobbies.

In German, those small differences matter.
- General like? **mögen**.
- Polite request? **möchten**.
- Enjoying the process? **gern**.

*Nammalkku oru word thanne mathi, pakshe German-il ninte level-up cheyyaan ee moonu words-um important aanu!*

[EXAM-TIP] The examiner might ask: 'Was möchten Sie trinken?' Answer: 'Ich möchte einen Tee, bitte.' — or — 'Ich trinke gern Tee.' Both are correct, but 'Ich möchte' sounds more like an order."

---

## Section 6: Vegetarian/Vegan Check (8:00–9:30)

### Visual: [ICONS: Carrot (Vegetarier) | Leaf (Veganer). Caption: "Ich bin Vegetarier/in".]
### Narration: [ENERGY: focused, helpful]
"Wait, what if you DON'T like something? (Maybe you're a vegetarian!).

[Negation card]
🔊 **'Ich mag KEIN Fleisch.'** (I don't like meat).
🔊 **'Ich möchte KEINEN Fisch.'** (I don't want fish).

🔊 **'Ich bin Vegetarier'** (Male) / **'Ich bin Vegetarierin'** (Female).

*In Germany, 'Vegetarisch' ennu paranjaal ellarkkum ariyam. Nammude Malayali food often vegetarisch aanu, so ithu orkkanam!*

⏸️ **Try: I don't like meat. (Ich mag ...)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Ich mag kein Fleisch.** ✅"

## Section 7: Speed Challenge — Choice Check (9:30–10:30)

### Visual: [FLASHCARDS: Scenario (Order in cafe, talk about hobby, general like). Student picks 'mag', 'möchte', or 'gern'.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, rapid-fire]
"Final challenge. Pick the right word!

⚡ I like pizza (General)? → **Ich mag Pizza!**
⚡ I'd like a coffee (Order)? → **Ich möchte einen Kaffee!**
⚡ I like eating fruit (Action)? → **Ich esse gern Obst!**
⚡ I like Kerala (Place)? → **Ich mag Kerala!**
⚡ I'd like the bill (Order)? → **Ich möchte die Rechnung!**
⚡ I like playing music (Action)? → **Ich spiele gern Musik!**

Excellent! If you got the heart (mag) and the hand (möchte) separate, you've won!."
