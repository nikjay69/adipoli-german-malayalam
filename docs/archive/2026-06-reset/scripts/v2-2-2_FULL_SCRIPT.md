## Section 3: The 'aus' Rule — Most Countries Are Free (2:15–3:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Ich komme aus..." with icons of India, Germany, China, Japan. NO ARTICLES shown.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, logical]
"The word for 'from' is **aus**.
For 90% of countries, it's simple: **aus + Country Name**. No articles needed.

- **aus Indien** (from India)
- **aus Deutschland** (from Germany)
- **aus China**
- **aus Japan**

*Ithra easy aanu! Article venda, direct country name.*

⏸️ **Say it: 'I come from India.'**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Ich komme aus Indien.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: The 4 Exceptions — Enter the Dativ (3:30–6:00)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: Red warning box: "THE EXCEPTIONS". Flags of Switzerland, Turkey, USA, Iran.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: focused, warning but friendly]
"But there are 4 'divas' in the world of countries. They insist on having an article. And when you use **aus**, that article changes because of a rule called **Dativ**.

[Table appears with arrows]
| Country | Original | aus + Dativ |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Switzerland | die Schweiz | **aus der Schweiz** |
| Turkey | die Türkei | **aus der Türkei** |
| USA (plural) | die USA | **aus den USA** |
| Iran | der Iran | **aus dem Iran** |

*Ithu important aanu! 'Aus die Schweiz' parayilla. 'Aus der Schweiz' ennayirikkum.*

[Logic breakdown]
- **die** changes to **der**
- **die (plural)** changes to **den**
- **der** changes to **dem**

⏸️ **Drill: 'I come from Switzerland.' Say it!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Ich komme aus der Schweiz.** ✅

⏸️ **'I come from the USA.' Go!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Ich komme aus den USA.** ✅

⏸️ **'I come from Turkey.'**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Ich komme aus der Türkei.** ✅"

## Section 5: The Kerala Diaspora Flex (6:00–7:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Malayalis Everywhere!". Photos of Malayalis in Gulf, UK, Germany, USA.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: proud, cultural]
"We Malayalis are everywhere! Let's practice saying where our friends and family are.

- My brother is in the USA: **'Mein Bruder ist in den USA.'**
- My friend is from Switzerland: **'Mein Freund kommt aus der Schweiz.'**
- Many Malayalis live in Germany: **'Viele Malayalis wohnen in Deutschland.'**

[EXAM-TIP] If you were born in India but lived in another country, say: 'Ich komme aus Indien, aber ich wohne in den USA.' (I come from India, but I live in the USA). Examiners LOVE this 'aber' (but) connection!"
