## Section 1: Hook — The Self-Action (0:15–0:50)

### Visual: [SCENE: A man looking in a mirror. Arrows pointing from him, back to him. Caption: "Ich wasche MICH" (I wash MYSELF).]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, engaging]
"When you wake up, you don't just 'wash'. You wash *yourself*. You don't just 'dress'. You dress *yourself*.

In German, these are called **Reflexive Verbs**. It sounds fancy, but it just means the action 'reflects' back to you. Like a mirror in the morning!

Today, we're mastering the verbs that help you look and feel good in German. Let's start the routine!"

## Section 2: Thinking in Mirrors — The Reflexive Pronoun (0:50–2:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: Personal Pronoun vs. Reflexive Pronoun. ich → mich | du → dich | er/sie/es → sich | wir → uns | ihr → euch | sie/Sie → sich.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: steady, instructional]
"Before the verb, we need the 'Mirror Word'. These are reflexive pronouns.

[Pronouns appear]
- **ich** → **mich** (myself)
- **du** → **dich** (yourself)
- **er / sie / es** → **sich** (him/her/itself)
- **wir** → **uns** (ourselves)
- **ihr** → **euch** (yourselves)
- **Sie / sie** → **sich** (yourselves/themselves)

*Sich anusharichu pronoun maarum. 'Ich' ennu parayumpol 'mich' aayi mariyirukkanam. Ithu nannaayi ormikkuka.*

⏸️ **Try: I ... myself?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**ich mich.** ✅

⏸️ **You ... yourself?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**du dich.** ✅"

## Section 4: The Double Challenge — Separable + Reflexive (4:30–6:30)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: sich an|ziehen (to get dressed). Formula: Person + [Verb] + reflexive + [Prefix].]
### Narration: [ENERGY: attentive, warning]
"Some verbs are both reflexive AND separable. This is where you become a German pro.

[Logic cards]
**sich anziehen** (to get dressed)
**sich ausziehen** (to get undressed)

🔊 **'Ich ziehe mich an.'**
(I pull myself on = I get dressed).

🔊 **'Ich ziehe mich aus.'**
(I pull myself off = I get undressed).

*Suffix 'an' or 'aus' end-il varum, reflexive 'mich' center-il varum. Ithu kandaal examiner impress aakum!*

⏸️ **Try: I get dressed!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Ich ziehe mich an.** ✅"

## Section 5: Malayalam Logic — "Self" in Kerala (6:30–8:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Malayalam 'Njan kuli-kkunnu' vs German 'Ich wasche mich'.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, structural]
"In Malayalam, the reflexive is often built INTO the verb. When you say 'njan kuli-kkunnu', it already implies you are washing yourself.

In German, you **must** say who you are washing. If you just say 'Ich wasche', the German will ask: 'What are you washing? The car? The dog?'.

*Njan ennethanne aanu washi-cheyyunnathu ennu clear aayi parayanam. Athinu vendiyanu ee 'mich/dich' use cheyyunnathu.*

[EXAM-TIP] In the **Hören** (listening) exam, listen for 'sich'. If you hear it, the person is talking about their personal routine, not a general activity."
