## Section 2 — Strategy 1: Scanning (Don't Read Every Word)
🎬 [Energy: Practical coach — teaching a skill, not grammar]

"First strategy: SCANNING. Nee oru newspaper muthuvum muthuvum vaayikkarullo? Headline first, then important details.

German texts — same approach. Look for **KEY INFORMATION** first:

- **Names** — Who is this about?
- **Numbers** — Dates, prices, times, phone numbers
- **Places** — Cities, addresses, rooms

*[SLIDE: Sample text with names/numbers/places highlighted in color]*

These are your anchors. Find them first, THEN read the sentences around them.

Let's practice. I'll show you a sentence — find the key info BEFORE reading everything."

*[SLIDE: "Das Meeting ist am Montag um 14 Uhr im Konferenzraum 3."]*

⏸️ *[3 seconds — spot the key info]*

"Key info: **Montag** — when. **14 Uhr** — time. **Konferenzraum 3** — where. You understood the important part WITHOUT reading every word!"

## Section 3 — Strategy 2: Context Guessing + Cognates
🎬 [Energy: Encouraging — building confidence]

"Second strategy: you know MORE words than you think!

Look at this sentence:

*[SLIDE: "Das Restaurant hat eine gute Atmosphäre und die Musik ist fantastisch."]*

⏸️ *[3 seconds — how much can you understand?]*

"Restaurant, Atmosphäre, Musik, fantastisch — these are **Cognates**! Words that look like English. German is FULL of them:

*[SLIDE: Cognate pairs appearing one by one]*

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| das Telefon | telephone |
| der Computer | computer |
| das Hotel | hotel |
| die Information | information |
| die Musik | music |
| das Ticket | ticket |

"Ippo nee English ariyaam — so you already know hundreds of German words for free! 😄"

**Third power: Compound Word Breaking!**

"Remember Module 8? Long German words = small words stuck together.

*[SLIDE: Compound word decomposition]*

- **Kranken·schwester** = Kranken (sick) + Schwester (sister) = nurse
- **Kühl·schrank** = kühl (cool) + Schrank (cupboard) = fridge  
- **Haupt·bahnhof** = Haupt (main) + Bahnhof (train station) = central station

⏸️ *[2 seconds]*

"Athayathu — if you see a monster word, BREAK IT. You probably know the pieces!"

## Section 5 — Practice Text 2: The Apartment Ad
🎬 [Energy: Practical urgency — this is a real skill you'll use in Germany]

"Next text — and this one you WILL see in real life AND on the exam. An apartment ad!

*[SLIDE: Apartment ad]*

---

**2-Zi.-Whg., 55 qm, EBK, Balkon**  
Nähe Hauptbahnhof, 3. OG, ruhige Lage  
580 € warm, ab 01.04.  
Kontakt: 030-9876543

---

⏸️ *[8 seconds — decode everything you can]*

"Let's decode — using everything from Module 8!

- **2-Zi.-Whg.** = 2-Zimmer-Wohnung — 2-room apartment
- **55 qm** = 55 Quadratmeter — 55 square meters
- **EBK** = Einbauküche — fitted kitchen (built-in!)
- **Balkon** — cognate! Balcony
- **Nähe Hauptbahnhof** — near the main station. 'Nähe' = near
- **3. OG** = 3. Obergeschoss — 3rd floor
- **ruhige Lage** — quiet location. 'Ruhig' from Module 15 Ruhezeit!
- **580 € warm** — 580 euros INCLUDING heating. Remember Module 8: **warm = everything included, kalt = plus heating!**
- **ab 01.04.** — available from April 1st. Module 3 date format!

Now answer:

**Question:** You need a quiet apartment near public transport, max 600€. Is this apartment good for you?"

⏸️ *[4 seconds]*

"Yes! Quiet location ✅, near Hauptbahnhof ✅, 580€ warm ✅. Perfect match!"

> 📌 **[EXAM-TIP]:** Lesen Teil 1 often gives you a need → you match it to an ad. Train this: read what the person NEEDS, then scan ads for those specific features. Don't read every ad completely!

⏸️ *[12 seconds — read the whole text]*

- **aus Kerala nach Berlin gezogen** — moved from Kerala to Berlin (Module 9: aus + nach!)
- **Am Anfang war alles schwer** — at the beginning everything was hard
- **Ich habe nur Englisch gesprochen** — I only spoke English (Module 13 Perfekt!)
- **einen Deutschkurs gemacht** — did a German course (Akkusativ + Perfekt!)
- **als Krankenpfleger** — as a nurse (Module 11!)
- **jedes Wochenende** — every weekend (Module 13!)
- **ich fühle mich** — I feel myself — that's a **reflexive verb** from THIS module, v16-3-1!

**Question:** Name 3 things that were difficult at the beginning."
