## Section 2: Teil 2 Recap — The Classifieds (0:50–2:15)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: A newspaper page with 5 classified ads. Beside each, a 'Richtig' or 'Falsch' checkbox.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, logical]
"Teil 2 gives you 5 short advertisements. Beside each ad is a statement. Your job? Decide if the statement matches the ad. **Richtig** (True) or **Falsch** (False).

*Ithu nammude newspaper ads pole thanne aanu. Oru ad ezhuthiyirunnu, pinne oru statement undaakum. Athu correct aanonno allaonno parayanam. Ithra ullu!*

🔊 **The Golden Strategy:** Focus on the NUMBERS and the ACTIONS. If the ad says '2 Zimmer' and the statement says '3 Zimmer', it's **Falsch**. Instant point."

## Section 3: Teil 3 Recap — The Emails (2:15–4:30)

### Visual: [SCENE: An email on a screen. 3 options (a/b/c) below it. Highlight on 'Lieber Paul' and 'Viele Grüße'.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high-value, precise]
"Teil 3 moves to short personal or semi-formal emails. You have to answer ONE multiple-choice question for each of the 5 emails.

[Structure of an email]
- **Anrede:** 'Lieber/r Mark' (Informal) or 'Sehr geehrte/r Frau/Herr...' (Formal).
- **Inhalt:** The message (I invite you, I'm sick, where is the meeting?).
- **Schluss:** 'Viele Grüße' (Informal) or 'Mit freundlichen Grüßen' (Formal).

🔊 **The Golden Strategy:** Read the QUESTION first. It tells you exactly whose perspective you need. 'Was soll Mark machen?' (What should Mark do?). Look for the command verbs like **'kommen'**, **'anrufen'**, **'schreiben'**.

⏸️ **Quick Quiz: What is 'Anruf'?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**A CALL.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: The 3 Classic Differentiators (4:30–6:15)

### Visual: [TABLE: 'Not' vs. 'No', 'Saturday' vs. 'Sunday', '13' vs. '30'. Flags showing the traps.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: focused, helpful]
"The exam wants to trick you in 3 ways:

[Trap 1]: **Negation**
Ad: 'Kaffee **nicht** im Angebot.'
Statement: 'Es gibt billigen Kaffee.' → **Falsch!**

[Trap 2]: **Time/Day Swap**
Email: 'Wir treffen uns am **Samstag**.'
Question: Wann treffen sie sich? a) Sonntag b) Samstag c) Montag. → **Samstag!**

[Trap 3]: **Similar Numbers**
Ad: 'Der Mietpreis ist **130** Euro.'
Statement: 'Die Miete kostet 310 Euro.' → **Falsch!**

*Ithu pole numbers-ul and days-ul vachulla trap-ukal kandaal, twice check cheyyuka.*

⏸️ **Try: What is 'Sonntag'?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**SUNDAY.** ✅"

## Section 5: Real Practice — Classifieds (6:15–8:30)

### Visual: [SCENE: Ad: "2-Zi-Wohnung in Berlin, 600€ + NK, frei ab 01.05." Statement: "Die Wohnung ist ab sofort frei."]
### Narration: [ENERGY: interactive, coaching]
"Let's practice Teil 2.

[Ad]: **'2-Zi-Wohnung in Berlin, 600€ + NK, frei ab 01.05.'**
(2-room apartment, 600 euros + costs, free from May 1st.)

[Statement]: **'Die Wohnung ist ab sofort frei.'**
(The apartment is free IMMEDIATELY.)

⏸️ **Richtig or Falsch?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (4 seconds)
**Falsch.** ✅ Because it's only free on May 1st, not right now ('ab sofort').

*Ippol manassilaayi alle? Specific date vs 'immediately'. Difference undu!*

[Email]: **'Liebe Sarah, ich kann leider heute nicht kommen. Ich bin krank. Können wir uns am Freitag treffen? Gruß, Anna.'**

[Question]: **'Wann will Anna Sarah treffen?'**
a) Heute b) Freitag c) Montag

⏸️ **Which one is it?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (4 seconds)
**b) Freitag.** ✅ She says she *can't* come today. She wants to meet on Friday.

*Negation 'nicht' trap-il veenilla, so b) is correct!*"

## Section 6: Malayalam Logic — The 'Specifics' Trap (8:30–9:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: General vs. Specific mapping.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: sharp, warning]
"In Malayalam, if someone says 'Let's meet tomorrow', we often take it as 'anytime tomorrow'. In German exams, if the email says 'Meet at 2:00 PM', and the question asks 'Meet in the afternoon or evening?', the answer is **Afternoon** (Nachmittag). Be precise!

[EXAM-TIP] In **Lesen**, skip the words you don't know. If the sentence is 'The apartment has a *balcony*', and you don't know 'balcony', but it says '2 rooms', and the statement says '2 rooms', that's all you need! Focus on the verbs and nouns you ALREADY know."

## Section 7: Speed Challenge — True/False & MC (9:30–11:00)

"Final speed round.

1. Ad: 'Suche Auto' | Statement: 'Er verkauft ein Auto.' → **Falsch!** (Suche = looking for)
2. Email: 'Bring bitte Pizza mit' | Question: What should you bring? Pizza! → **Richtig!**
3. Ad: 'Mo-Fr 9-18' | Statement: 'Samstag geschlossen.' → **Richtig!**
4. Ad: 'Preis: 15€' | Statement: 'Es kostet 50€.' → **Falsch!**
5. Email: 'Ich bin im Urlaub' | Question: Where is she? a) At home b) In office c) On holiday → **c) On holiday!**

Excellent! You're decoding the facts perfectly."
