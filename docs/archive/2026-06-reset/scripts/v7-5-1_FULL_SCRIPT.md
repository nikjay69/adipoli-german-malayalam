## Section 2: Step 1 — The Comparative (-er + als) (1:00–3:30)

### Visual: [FORMULA: Adjective + "-er" + "als". Examples: billig → billiger als. schnell → schneller als. Counter: "A is ...er than B".]
### Narration: [ENERGY: steady, instructional]
"To say something is 'more' [adjective], just add an **-er** at the end. And to say 'than', use the word **'als'**.

[Examples appear]
- 🔊 **billig** (cheap) → **billiger als** (cheaper than)
  **'Das Brot ist billiger als das Fleisch.'**
- 🔊 **schnell** (fast) → **schneller als** (faster than)
  **'Der Zug ist schneller als der Bus.'**
- 🔊 **klein** (small) → **kleiner als** (smaller than)
  **'Meine Tasche ist kleiner als deine Tasche.'**

*Suffix '-er' add cheythaal 'more' ennu artham varum. 'Als' use cheythaal 'than' ennu artham varum.*

⏸️ **Try: Bigger than. (groß)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**größer als.** ✅ *(Note the umlaut!)*"

---

## Section 3: The Umlaut Trap — A, O, U (3:30–5:30)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: groß → größer | alt → älter | jung → jünger. Ballooning umlauts over the letters.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"Warning! Some short, common adjectives with a, o, or u get an **Umlaut** when they grow bigger.

[The "Dots" Rule]
- **alt** (old) → **älter**
- **groß** (big) → **größer**
- **jung** (young) → **jünger**
- **kalt** (cold) → **kälter**

*Oru syllable ulla words-il 'a, o, u' undenkil mostly umlaut add cheyyanam. Ithu ninte pronunciation clearer aakkum!*

⏸️ **Say: Older than.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**älter als.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: The Irregulars — The "Must-Learns" (5:30–7:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: gut → besser | viel → mehr | gern → lieber. Icons: Good/Better, Many/More, Love/Prefferred.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: focused, instructional]
"Some words don't follow the -er rule. They change completely!

[The Irregulars]
- 🔊 **gut** (good) → **besser** (better)
  **'Der Kaffee ist besser als der Tee.'**
- 🔊 **viel** (much) → **mehr** (more)
  **'Ich habe mehr Reis als Fleisch.'**
- 🔊 **gern** (gladly) → **lieber** (preferably)
  **'Ich esse lieber Curry.'** *(I prefer to eat curry).*

*Ithu nammude dictionary-il direct aayi memory-yil save cheyyanam. Ithu daily useful aanu!*

⏸️ **What is 'better'?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**besser.** ✅"

---

## Section 5: Step 2 — The Superlative (am -sten) (7:30–9:30)

### Visual: [FORMULA: am + [adjective] + "-sten". Examples: am billigsten, am schnellsten, am besten. Icon: Number 1 medal.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, logical]
"Now, let's go for the GOLD! The Number 1. The 'Most' [adjective].

We use: **'am'** + adjective + **'-sten'**.

[The Superlatives]
- 🔊 **billig** → **am billigsten** (the cheapest)
- 🔊 **schnell** → **am schnellsten** (the fastest)
- 🔊 **gut** → **am besten** (the best)
- 🔊 **viel** → **am meisten** (the most)

*Suffix '-sten' end-il cheruk-kunna-thu 'most' ennu artham varum. 'Am' position-nu munne thanne vekkanam.*

⏸️ **Say: The best!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**am besten.** ✅"

## Section 6: Malayalam Logic — Comparatives & High Points (9:30–11:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Malayalam 'Ee uduppu athine-kkaal billig-aanu' vs German 'Das Kleid ist billiger als...'.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, structural]
"In Malayalam, we use '-kaal' for comparisons. 'Athinekkaal nallathu' = Better than that.

In German, the **adjective itself changes** (-er). This is why learning the 'root' word is so important. Once you know 'billig', you automatically know 'billiger' and 'am billigsten'.

*Base word padichal comparative-um superlative-um direct aayi build cheyyaam!*

[EXAM-TIP] The examiner might show you two price tags. Question: 'Welcher ist teurer?' (Which is more expensive?). Answer: 'Der braune Mantel ist teurer als der blaue Mantel.' Use full sentences for better marks!"
