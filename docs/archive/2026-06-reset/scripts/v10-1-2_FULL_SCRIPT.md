## Script

**Narration (Malayalam/Manglish):**
Namskaram machane! Aadyathe lesson-il nammal body parts padichu (Kopf, Hand, Fuß...). Ini, "Ente thala vedanikkunnu" or "Ente kaal vedanikkunnu" ennu engane German-il parayaam enn nokkaam. Ithu doctor-ude aduthu poyal super important aanu.

German-il "To hurt" ennu parayaaan "wehtun" (separable verb) aanu upayogikkunnath. Ithinte use bhayankara simple aanu. Two patterns mathram orma vechaal mathi.

**Topic: Singular Pain (Tut weh)**
Oru body part mathramaano vedanikkunnath? Enkil "tut weh" upayogikkuka.

Structure: [Mein/Meine/Dein/Deine] + [Body Part] + tut weh.

- "Mein Kopf tut weh." (My head hurts / Ente thala vedanikkunnu)
- "Meine Hand tut weh." (My hand hurts)
- "Mein Rücken tut weh." (My back hurts)

"Weh" eppozhum sentence-inte last-il aayirikkum varunnath.

**Topic: Plural Pain (Tun weh)**
Ippo randu kannu or randu kaal vedanikkunnu enkil "tun weh" upayogikkuka. (Singular "tut", Plural "tun").

Structure: [Meine/Deine] + [Body Parts (Plural)] + tun weh.

- "Meine Augen tun weh." (My eyes hurt / Ente kannu vedanikkunnu)
- "Meine Füße tun weh." (My feet hurt)
- "Meine Ohren tun weh." (My ears hurt)

**Topic: Adding Intensity (Adverbs)**
Pain ethrakund ennu parayaanum nammalkku padikkaam.

- "ein bisschen" (a little): "Mein Kopf tut ein bisschen weh."
- "sehr" (very): "Mein Bein tut sehr weh."
- "furchtbar" (terribly): "Mein Rücken tut furchtbar weh."

Kerala-yil nammal "Kore vedana undu" ennu parayunna polle "sehr weh" ennu parayaam.

**Topic: Interaction Drill**
Machane, ippo njane oru body part parayum, ningal "tut/tun weh" sentence parayanam. Ready?

1. (Points to head) → "Mein Kopf tut weh."
2. (Points to eyes) → "Meine Augen tun weh."
3. (Points to stomach) → "Mein Bauch tut weh."

Adholoke!

**Closing:**
Singular aayittu oru part aano "tut weh", plural aano "tun weh" — ithu mathram orma vechaal mathi. 

Adutha lesson-il nammal "Feelings and General Health" (Wie geht es dir?) patti padikkaam. Until then, tschüss!
