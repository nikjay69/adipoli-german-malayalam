## Section 2: The 5-Block Formula (0:50–2:30)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: 5 interlocking blocks color-coded.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, structured]
"A perfect self-introduction has five blocks. We've learned the pieces. Now we assemble them.

[Block 1] **Greeting** (Guten Tag!)
[Block 2] **Name** (Ich heiße...)
[Block 3] **Origin** (Ich komme aus...)
[Block 4] **Profession** (Ich bin...)
[Block 5] **Languages** (Ich spreche...)

*Ithu nammude resume pole aanu. Simple blocks. Safe grammar.*

🔊 **Repeat the order with me:** Greeting → Name → Origin → Profession → Languages.

⏸️ **Quick Quiz: Which block comes after Origin?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Profession.** ✅"

## Section 3: Scenario 1 — The Goethe Speaking Exam (2:30–5:30)

### Visual: [SCENE: Formal setting. Text appearing line by line with audio.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: professional, rehearsed]
"In the exam, use Pattern 2 (Mein Name ist) or Pattern 1 (Ich heiße). Always be formal.

[Full Intro appears in gold]
🔊 **'Guten Tag! Mein Name ist Anita Nair. Ich komme aus Indien, aus Kerala. Ich bin Krankenschwester von Beruf. Ich spreche Malayalam, Englisch und ein bisschen Deutsch. Freut mich!'**

---

### Step-by-Step Breakdown:
1. **Greeting:** 'Guten Tag!'
2. **Name:** 'Mein Name ist [Full Name].'
3. **Origin:** 'Ich komme aus [Country].' — *Note: mentioning Kerala is a great icebreaker!*
4. **Profession:** 'Ich bin [Profession].' — *Note: NO article ('ein') here! Just the word.*
5. **Languages:** 'Ich spreche [List].'

⏸️ **Your Turn: Look at the template. Say YOUR introduction for an examiner. Ready? Go!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (10 seconds)
**'Guten Tag! Ich heiße...'** ✅"

## Section 4: Scenario 2 — The First Day at the Office (5:30–7:30)

### Visual: [SCENE: Modern German office. Coffee machine. Colleagues smiling. Blue 'Sie' label.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: warm, professional]
"Your first day at a German company (or even a university). People will ask you to introduce yourself.

[Office Intro appears]
🔊 **'Hallo! Ich heiße Nikhil. Ich komme aus Indien. Ich bin der neue Ingenieur hier. Ich spreche Englisch und lerne gerade Deutsch. Es freut mich, Sie alle kennenzulernen!'**

*Here, notice we added 'der neue' (the new). It's a bit more conversational. And we used 'Es freut mich, Sie alle kennenzulernen!' which means 'Nice to meet you all!'*

⏸️ **Try: Say 'Nice to meet you all!' in German!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (4 seconds)
**Es freut mich, Sie alle kennenzulernen!** ✅"

---

## Section 5: Scenario 3 — The Casual Party (7:30–9:00)

### Visual: [SCENE: Social gathering. Music. Green 'du' label. Casual clothing.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: relaxed, friendly]
"You meet friends of friends. No need to be stiff! Use 'du'.

[Casual Intro appears]
🔊 **'Hi! Ich bin Akhil. Ich komme aus Kerala. Ich bin Student hier in Berlin. Ich spreche Malayalam und Englisch. Und du?'**

*Short. Sweet. Finished with 'Und du?' to keep the conversation going. In a casual setting, you don't even need to say your full name — just your first name is fine.*

⏸️ **Try: Say 'And you?' casually!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Und du?** ✅"

## Section 6: Malayalam Logic — Respecting the Hierarchy (9:00–10:15)

### Visual: [TABLE: Malayalam introductions showing 'Njan [name]' vs formal introductions.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, relatable]
"In Kerala, we often lead with our job or our family background when the situation is formal. In Germany, the logic is: **GREETING → NAME → ORIGIN**.

The hierarchy is different. They want to know WHO you are and WHERE you are from before WHAT you do.

[EXAM-TIP] If you get stuck, RESTART the current block. Don't panic. The examiners want to see you flow. If you say 'Ich komme... uh... Indien', just stop and say **'Ich komme aus Indien.'** once more clearly. Corrections are appreciated!"
