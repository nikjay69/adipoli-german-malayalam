## Section 2: Breaking Down the Magic Sentence (0:40–2:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Sentence with word-by-word highlights]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, methodical]

"Let's unpack it:

[TEXT highlights word by word]
- **Eine Fahrkarte** — one ticket. 'Fahr' = travel, 'Karte' = card. Travel card = ticket.
- **nach Berlin** — to Berlin. Swap the city, go anywhere.
- **bitte** — please. Always.

🔊 **Drill — swap the city:**
- 'Eine Fahrkarte nach München, bitte.'
- 'Eine Fahrkarte nach Hamburg, bitte.'
- 'Eine Fahrkarte nach Frankfurt, bitte.'

⏸️ **Your turn: pick YOUR dream city in Germany. Say the sentence!**

*'Oru ticket [place]-kku' — same idea, German style. But always say bitte!*"

---

## Section 3: The Counter Questions — einfach, Klasse, Ermäßigung (2:00–4:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Three counter questions stacked]
### Narration: [ENERGY: practical, Q&A rhythm]

"You said your magic sentence. Now the counter person fires 3 questions. Here they are:

**Question 1: 'Einfach oder hin und zurück?'**
- einfach = one way (literally 'simple')
- hin und zurück = round trip (there and back)

*'Oru vazhikku mathiyo, back-um veno?' — exactly this question!*

🔊 **Say it:** 'Hin und zurück, bitte.'

**Question 2: 'Erste oder zweite Klasse?'**
- Erste Klasse = first class (expensive, quiet)
- Zweite Klasse = second class (normal — 99% of people)

🔊 **Say it:** 'Zweite Klasse, bitte.'

**Question 3: This one YOU ask!**
[SLIDE: "Gibt es eine Ermäßigung?" highlighted]
'Gibt es eine Ermäßigung?' = Is there a discount?

ALWAYS ask this. Student discounts, BahnCard discounts, group deals — they exist, but only if you ask.

*India-yilum discount chodikkum alle? Germany-yilum same — but politely. No bargaining though!*

🔊 **Say it:** 'Gibt es eine Ermäßigung?'

⏸️ **Recap: what are the 3 counter exchanges? One-way or round trip... class... and you ask for?... discount!**"

## Section 4: Decode the Departure Board (4:00–5:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Annotated departure board — labels HIDDEN initially]
### Narration: [ENERGY: challenge mode, detective]

"Before you board, you need to READ the departure board. Let's decode it.

[SLIDE: Board appears with labels hidden — just German words visible]

⏸️ **Look at the board. Can you guess what any of these words mean?**

Now let me reveal:
- **Abfahrt** — departure. YELLOW board at the station.
- **Ankunft** — arrival. WHITE board.
- **Gleis** — platform. 'Gleis 7' = Platform 7.
- **Ziel** — destination. Where the train goes.
- **Verspätung** — delay. *Welcome to Deutsche Bahn reality!* 😄

*Kerala-il train delay expected aanu. Germany-yil aalukal VERY upset aakum. 'Die Bahn hat Verspätung' is a national joke!*

🔊 **Repeat the 5 words:** 'Abfahrt... Ankunft... Gleis... Ziel... Verspätung...'

⏸️ **Quick: Platform = ? ... Gleis! Departure = ? ... Abfahrt! Delay = ? ... Verspätung!**"

## Section 5: The Golden Ticket Dialogue (5:30–8:00)

### Visual: [DIALOGUE: Full counter scene, lines appear one by one]
### Narration: [ENERGY: immersive, role-play]

"Now the FULL dialogue. But we're doing this actively — I'll set up each line, you PREDICT what comes next.

**[Scene: train station counter]**

You walk up. What do you say first?

⏸️ **Think... then listen:**

> **Reisender:** 'Guten Tag! Eine Fahrkarte nach Hamburg, bitte.'

The counter asks about one-way or return. What do you expect to hear?

> **Beamter:** 'Einfach oder hin und zurück?'

⏸️ **You want round trip. What do you say?**

> **Reisender:** 'Hin und zurück, bitte.'

Next question — class. You already know it:

> **Beamter:** 'Erste oder zweite Klasse?'

⏸️ **Your answer + your bonus question?**

> **Reisender:** 'Zweite Klasse. Gibt es eine Ermäßigung für Studenten?'

> **Beamter:** 'Ja, mit Studentenausweis bekommen Sie 25 Prozent Rabatt.'

> **Reisender:** 'Super! Kann ich mit Karte zahlen?'

> **Beamter:** 'Natürlich. Das macht fünfundvierzig Euro. Ihr Zug fährt um vierzehn Uhr dreißig auf Gleis sieben.'

> **Reisender:** 'Vielen Dank!'

> **Beamter:** 'Gute Reise!'

[SLIDE: Key phrases highlighted]
**Unpack the hard parts:**
- **'Das macht fünfundvierzig Euro.'** — That comes to 45€. 'Das macht...' = the total is...
- **'vierzehn Uhr dreißig'** — 14:30. 24-hour time!
- **'auf Gleis sieben'** — on platform 7.
- **'Gute Reise!'** — Have a good trip!

🎯 **EXAM TIP:** In Hören, the counter person gives 3 key details: **price**, **time**, **platform**. These are EXACTLY what they test. Listen for numbers!

⏸️ **From the dialogue: price? ... 45€. Time? ... 14:30. Platform? ... 7. If you got all 3, you'd ace the Hören question!**"

## Section 6: Payment + nach Rule (8:00–9:30)

### Visual: [SPLIT SLIDE: Payment vocab left, nach rule right]
### Narration: [ENERGY: brisk, reference-mode]

"Two quick practical things before we wrap.

**Paying:**
| German | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| bar | cash |
| mit Karte | by card |
| kontaktlos | contactless (tap) |

'Kann ich mit Karte zahlen?' — your payment sentence.

*Germany STILL loves cash. Always carry some, especially at smaller stations.*

**The 'nach' rule for destinations:**

Most cities and countries: **nach**.
'Nach Berlin. Nach München. Nach Indien. Nach Deutschland.'

Countries with articles: **in die / in den**.
'In die Schweiz. In die Türkei. In die USA. In den Iran.'

*How to know which? Most countries have NO article. Exceptions: die Schweiz, die Türkei, die USA, der Iran. If unsure, use 'nach' — Germans will understand.*

🔊 **Say:** 'Eine Fahrkarte nach München.' ... 'Ich fahre in die Schweiz.'"

The magic sentence + the Golden Dialogue — memorize these and you travel Germany with confidence.
