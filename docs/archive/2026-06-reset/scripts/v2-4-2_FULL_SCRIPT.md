## Section 1: Hook — The Unexpected Change (0:15–0:50)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Stem of 'sprechen' (spre-) shifting to 'sprich-' with a flashing arrow.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: alert, direct]
"In the last lesson, we saw something strange. **Ich spreche** but **du sprichst**. The 'e' jumped to an 'i'.

Why? Because German has a group of 'stem-changing' verbs. They follow the regular rules for endings, but they change their internal vowel for **du** and **er/sie/es**.

Think of them like 'rebel' verbs. They only change for the two most common people. Let's master the three patterns you need for the A1 exam."

---

## Section 2: Pattern 1 — The e → i Change (0:50–2:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: essen (to eat) and sprechen (to speak) side by side. e → i change in red.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: steady, methodical]
"Pattern number one: **e** changes to **i**. This is very common.

[Verbs appear together]
🔊 **sprechen** (to speak) → **ich spreche** | **du sprichst** | **er spricht**
🔊 **essen** (to eat) → **ich esse** | **du isst** | **er isst**

*Wait, 'du isst' and 'er isst' are the same? Yes! It's actually easier!*

Other common ones: **helfen** (to help) → **du hilfst**, **geben** (to give) → **du gibst**.

⏸️ **Try: You help? (helfen)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**du hilfst.** ✅

⏸️ **He eats? (essen)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**er isst.** ✅"

---

## Section 3: Pattern 2 — The e → ie Change (2:30–4:15)

### Visual: [TABLE: lesen (to read) and sehen (to see). ie change in blue.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: attentive, clear]
"Pattern number two: **e** changes to **ie**. This makes the sound a bit longer.

[Verbs appear]
🔊 **lesen** (to read) → **ich lese** | **du liest** | **er liest**
🔊 **sehen** (to see) → **ich sehe** | **du siehst** | **er sieht**

Watch out: In **lesen**, the 's' is already in the stem, so we don't add another one for 'du'. Just **'du liest'**.

*Note: The plurals (wir, ihr, sie) NEVER change. They stay with the original 'e'.*

⏸️ **Drill: You read? (lesen)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**du liest.** ✅

⏸️ **She sees? (sehen)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**sie sieht.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: Pattern 3 — The a → ä Change (4:15–6:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: fahren (to travel/drive) and schlafen (to sleep). ä change in green (the dots!).]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, warning]
"Pattern number three: **a** changes to **ä** (Umlaut!). This is the most visual change.

[Verbs appear]
🔊 **fahren** (to travel/drive) → **ich fahre** | **du fährst** | **er fährt**
🔊 **schlafen** (to sleep) → **ich schlafe** | **du schläfst** | **er schläft**

*Most verbs starting with 'a' do this. Ithu kandaal article change pole aanu — umlaut add cheyyuka.*

⏸️ **Say it: He travels to Berlin! (fahren)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Er fährt nach Berlin.** ✅"

---

## Section 5: The 'Change Zone' — Only du and er/sie/es (6:00–7:30)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Highlight on the second and third rows of a conjugation table. Others grayed out.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: focused, simplifying]
"If you feel overwhelmed, remember this one golden rule:

**The vowel only changes in the second and third persons singular.**

[The table glow]
- **ich** = NO change
- **du** = **CHANGE!** ✅
- **er/sie/es** = **CHANGE!** ✅
- **wir / ihr / sie / Sie** = NO change

*Plurals eppolum safe aanu. Original vowel thanne mathi.*

⏸️ **Quick check: wir + fahren? Change or no change?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**NO change. Wir fahren.** ✅"

## Section 6: Malayalam Logic — Vowel Nuances (7:30–8:45)

### Visual: [TABLE: Malayalam verbs showing subtle internal changes between formal and informal.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: reassuring, connecting]
"In Malayalam, we change the whole ending based on respect levels. In German, they change the 'heart' of the word (the vowel) for the most basic subjects.

Think of it like our vowel shifts: A vs Ä, E vs I. It changes the 'sound' but not the 'meaning'.

[EXAM-TIP] The #1 stem-change error is **'du fahrst'** instead of **'du fährst'**. Even if you forget the dots on paper, make sure you say the 'air' sound when speaking. Examiners listen for that 'ä'!"

## Section 7: Speed Challenge — Catch the Change (8:45–10:15)

### Visual: [FLASHCARDS: pronoun + verb. Student says the full form.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, timer]
"Final challenge. 10 rapid-fire verbs. Does it change or not?

1. **ich + essen?** → **Ich esse!** (No change)
2. **du + essen?** → **Du isst!** (CHANGE!)
3. **er + fahren?** → **Er fährt!** (CHANGE!)
4. **wir + lesen?** → **Wir lesen!** (No change)
5. **du + sehen?** → **Du siehst!** (CHANGE!)
6. **ihr + schlafen?** → **Ihr schlaft!** (No change)
7. **sie (she) + sprechen?** → **Sie spricht!** (CHANGE!)
8. **Sie (formal) + fahren?** → **Sie fahren!** (No change)
9. **du + schlafen?** → **Du schläfst!** (CHANGE!)
10. **es + essen?** → **Es isst!** (CHANGE!)

Kidu! If you caught the change for 'du' and 'er/sie/es', you've mastered the pattern."
