## Section 1: Hook — The Smallest Task, the Silliest Mistakes (0:15–0:45)

### Visual: [FORM: clean Goethe-style sample form with 5 highlighted fields]
### Narration: [ENERGY: direct, reassuring]
"On exam day, this is one of the most controllable writing tasks you will see: a short form with personal information.

The danger is not difficult German. The danger is simple confusion — surname in the first-name box, birthday where today's date should go, country instead of nationality.

*Task budhimuttalla. Paksha label thettiyaal point poyekkam.*

In this lesson, we'll train three things: the main field labels, the three classic traps, and a fast final check before you move on."

---

## Section 2: The Core Rule — Read the LABEL, Not Just the Empty Box (0:45–1:25)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Golden Rule: Read the label twice"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: calm, precise]
"Before any field, one rule:

**Read the label twice before you write.**

Many learners don't lose points because they don't know their information. They lose points because they rush.

So your exam sequence is:
1. Read the label
2. Decide what type of information it wants
3. Write only that information
4. Check once

⏸️ Say the rule now: what do you read twice?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**The label.** Exactly."

---

## Section 3: The Name Pair — Familienname vs Vorname (1:25–2:30)

### Visual: [FORM zoom: Familienname / Vorname. Then a red swap animation]
### Narration: [ENERGY: alert, teacherly]
"The first big trap is the name pair.

**Familienname** = surname / last name
**Vorname** = first name / given name

*Familienname = veettu peru. Vorname = ninte own first name.*

[SLIDE: ❌ Familienname: Nikhil | Vorname: Jayanth]
[SLIDE: ✅ Familienname: Jayanth | Vorname: Nikhil]

This swap happens because students see the empty box first and write automatically.

If the form says only **Name**, that usually means the surname.
If it says **Name, Vorname**, write surname first, then first name.

⏸️ Quick check: if your name is Nikhil Jayanth, what goes under Familienname?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**Jayanth.** Good.

And what goes under Vorname?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**Nikhil.**"

---

## Section 4: Geburtsdatum, Datum, Geburtsort — Don't Mix the Three (2:30–3:45)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: three boxes — Geburtsdatum / Datum / Geburtsort]
### Narration: [ENERGY: careful, contrast-heavy]
"Now the second trap family: three similar-looking labels.

### 1. Geburtsdatum
Your date of birth.
German format: **TT.MM.JJJJ**
Example: **15.03.1995**

### 2. Datum
Today's date.
Not your birthday.

### 3. Geburtsort
Your birthplace.
A city name, like Kochi or Kozhikode.
Not a date.

[SLIDE: ✅ Geburtsdatum = 15.03.1995 | ✅ Datum = 23.03.2026 | ✅ Geburtsort = Kochi]

*Ivide label-onnum nokkathe ezhuthiyal confusion ഉറപ്പാണ്.*

⏸️ If the form says **Datum** at the bottom next to your signature, what do you write?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**Today's date.** Not your birthday.

And German dates use what — slashes or dots?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**Dots.**"

---

## Section 5: Wohnort, Adresse, Straße, PLZ — How Specific Is the Form? (3:45–4:55)

### Visual: [ADDRESS anatomy card: Wohnort / Straße / Hausnummer / PLZ / Ort]
### Narration: [ENERGY: practical, brisk]
"Now let's separate city from full address.

### Wohnort
Just the town or city where you live.
Example: **Berlin**

### Adresse
The full address.
Usually:
- **Straße + Hausnummer** → Hauptstraße 15
- **PLZ + Ort** → 10115 Berlin

### Straße
Only the street name, sometimes with number depending on the form design.

### PLZ
Postleitzahl — postal code. In Germany, usually 5 digits.

The trick is simple:
**Write only the level of detail the field asks for.**

If it says Wohnort, don't write the whole address.
If it says Adresse, don't answer with only the city.

⏸️ What do you write for Wohnort — city only, or full address?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**City only.** Good."

---

## Section 6: Nationality Trap — Staatsangehörigkeit Is Not the Country Name (4:55–5:45)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Indien ❌ | Inder ❌ | indisch ✅]
### Narration: [ENERGY: emphatic, memorable]
"This is the trap many learners remember too late.

**Staatsangehörigkeit** means nationality.
So you usually write the adjective form.

For an Indian learner, the safe exam answer is:
**indisch**

Not:
- **Indien** — that's the country
- **Inder / Inderin** — that's a person noun

*Country alla. Person noun-um alla. Adjective form aanu.*

⏸️ Staatsangehörigkeit — what do you write?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**Indisch.**"

---

## Section 7: Other Common Fields — Fast Recognition Round (5:45–6:35)

### Visual: [CARD STACK: Telefonnummer / E-Mail / Beruf / Familienstand / Unterschrift / Geschlecht]
### Narration: [ENERGY: quicker, energetic]
"Let's do a fast recognition round.

- **Telefonnummer** → your phone number
- **E-Mail** → your email address exactly as it is
- **Beruf** → your profession, for example Student, Studentin, Krankenpfleger
- **Familienstand** → ledig, verheiratet, and so on
- **Unterschrift** → your signature
- **Geschlecht** → männlich / weiblich / divers, or sometimes m / w / d

You do not need elegant language here. You need correct information in the correct box.

⏸️ Which field means signature?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**Unterschrift.**"

---

## Section 8: Practice Form 1 — Slow and Clean (6:35–7:35)

### Visual: [FORM: library registration form with 6–7 fields]
### Narration: [ENERGY: coaching, active]
"Let's do a full practice form slowly.

Read each label. Then fill it with your own information.
Pause if needed.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (8 seconds)

Now check the logic, not just the spelling:
- Did surname and first name stay in the right boxes?
- Did you use dots in the date?
- Did Wohnort get only a city?
- Did Adresse get the full address?
- Did you sign only in Unterschrift?

[FORM fills in field by field with example answers]

This is how you should check on exam day: **box by box, label by label.**"

---

## Section 9: Practice Form 2 — Speed With Trap Detection (7:35–8:40)

### Visual: [FORM: VHS / course registration form with Name, Vorname, Geburtsdatum, Staatsangehörigkeit, Wohnort, Beruf]
### Narration: [ENERGY: challenge mode, brisk]
"Second form. Faster now.

This time, before writing, identify the trap labels first.
Which ones here are dangerous?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (4 seconds)

Correct — usually:
- **Name / Vorname**
- **Geburtsdatum**
- **Staatsangehörigkeit**

Now fill the whole form.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (8 seconds)

Check with the example:
- Name: **Kumar**
- Vorname: **Arun**
- Geburtsdatum: **05.11.1992**
- Staatsangehörigkeit: **indisch**
- Wohnort: **München**
- Beruf: **Student**

Notice: short, clean, no overthinking."

---

## Section 10: The 20-Second Final Check (8:40–9:35)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Final Check Before You Move On"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: disciplined, exam-focused]
"Before leaving Schreiben Teil 1, give yourself one fast final check.

### The 20-second checklist
1. **Names correct?** Familienname / Vorname not swapped
2. **Dates correct?** Dots, day first, right date in the right place
3. **Field type correct?** City vs full address, country vs nationality
4. **Nothing blank by accident?**

This is not the time for perfectionism. This is damage prevention.

⏸️ Which mistake is worse: one small spelling issue, or writing the wrong type of information in the box?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

Usually the **wrong information in the wrong box**. That's why labels matter so much."

---

## Section 11: Active Recall Summary (9:35–10:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: 3 traps + 1 golden rule hidden, then revealed]
### Narration: [ENERGY: firm, closing]
"Last recall.

What is the golden rule?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**Read the label twice.**

And the three big traps?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (4 seconds)

1. **Familienname vs Vorname**
2. **Geburtsdatum vs Datum vs Geburtsort**
3. **Staatsangehörigkeit = indisch, not Indien**

Good. That's the mindset for safe points in form filling.

Next: **Schreiben Teil 2** — the short message task."
