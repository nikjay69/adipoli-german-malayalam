## Section 2: Format Recap — 10 Situations, 5 Texts (0:50–2:15)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Left side — 10 people in different situations (looking for a job, wanting to buy bread, needing a doctor). Right side — 5 signs.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, strategic]
"Wait. 10 situations but only 5 texts?
Yes! That's the trap.

Some situations are distractors — they have NO matching text. Your job is NOT to find a home for every person. Your job is to find the ONE sign that perfectly matches a certain situation.

*Ithil 10 aalukal undaakum, pakshe 5 signs-ne match cheyyan paadu. Distraction-il veezhthanda!*

🔊 **The Golden Strategy:** Start with the SIGN, not the situation. Look at the sign, find its keywords, THEN find the person who needs it."

## Section 3: The 4 'Power Signs' — What You Must Know (2:15–4:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: 4 large sign mockups appearing with keywords highlighted.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high-value, precise]
"There are 4 types of signs that ALWAYS come up.

[Sign 1]: **Öffnungszeiten** (Opening Hours)
🔊 **'Mo–Fr 8–18 Uhr, Sa 9–13 Uhr'**
- **Match:** Someone wanting to know if it's open on Tuesday or Saturday.

[Sign 2]: **Verbote** (Prohibitions)
🔊 **'Rauchen verboten!' / 'Kein Eingang!'**
- **Match:** Someone asking if they can smoke here or enter this door.

[Sign 3]: **Preise / Angebote** (Prices / Offers)
🔊 **'Sonderangebot: 1,99 €' / 'Alles 50% reduziert!'**
- **Match:** Someone looking for a cheap deal/discount.

[Sign 4]: **Anzeigen** (Classifieds)
🔊 **'Zimmer frei!' / 'Auto zu verkaufen!'**
- **Match:** Someone looking for a house or a car.

⏸️ **Quick Quiz: What is 'geschlossen'?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**CLOSED.** ✅"

## Section 4: Decoding Abbreviations — The A1 Secret (4:30–6:15)

### Visual: [TABLE: Abbreviation vs. Full Word vs. Meaning. Mo, Di, Mi, Do, Fr, Sa, So and EG, OG.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: focused, helpful]
"Signs are small, so they use abbreviations. You MUST know these.

[Days of the week]
- **Mo, Di, Mi, Do, Fr, Sa, So.**
*Do you see 'Do'? That's Donnerstag (Thursday). Don't confuse it with Di (Dienstag)!*

[Building levels]
- **EG** = Erdgeschoss (Ground floor)
- **OG** = Obergeschoss (Upper floor)

[Real Estate]
- **Zi.** = Zimmer (Room)
- **mit Bad/WC** = with bath/toilet

⏸️ **Try: What is 'Mo-Fr'?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Montag bis Freitag.** ✅"

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (4 seconds)

*Ithu polathe small logic puzzles aanu exam-il undaakuka!*"

## Section 6: Malayalam Logic — The 'Specifics' Trap (8:30–9:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: Vague situation vs. Specific sign.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: sharp, warning]
"In Kerala, if a sign says 'Food available', we know what kind of food. In German exams, look for the MOST specific match.

If Person X wants 'Indian food' and Sign A says 'International Food' while Sign B says 'Asian Food', Sign B is usually the closer match.

[EXAM-TIP] Look for **Negation**. If the situation says 'You don't want to spend much money' and the sign says 'Kostenlos' (Free) or 'Angebot' (Offer), that is your match. Don't let the word 'Kosten' (cost) trick you!"
