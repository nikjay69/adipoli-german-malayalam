## Module 5: Daily Routine (Mein Tag)

### Teaching Strategy
This is the MAKE-OR-BREAK grammar module. If students don't internalize present tense conjugation here, every later module suffers. The approach:
- Teach the pattern clearly with Malayalam parallels
- Drill it through daily routine (emotionally engaging)
- Introduce separable verbs as a known pattern, not a scary exception
- Build toward sustained output: "Describe your day" = 8+ correct sentences

## Module 13: Talking About the Past (Perfekt)

### Teaching Strategy
Perfekt is the second grammar pillar after present tense. The approach:
- "Sandwich" metaphor for haben/sein...ge-verb-t structure
- haben verbs first (majority), sein verbs second (limited set)
- Irregular participles as a memorization task with tricks
- Culmination: "Mein Wochenende" narrative

### Lesson 13-1: Perfekt with haben
**Videos:** v13-1-1 (10:00), v13-1-2 (10:00)

#### v13-1-1 Scene Architecture: "The Past Tense — Perfekt with haben"

| Scene | Time | Type | Content | Visual |
|-------|------|------|---------|--------|
| 1 | 0:00–1:00 | Hook | "Finally — talking about the past! 'What did you do yesterday?' After this, you can answer." | |
| 2 | 1:00–2:30 | Concept | The Perfekt sandwich: Subject + haben (conjugated) + ... + past participle (END). Visual sandwich diagram. | [DIAGRAM: sandwich/frame] |

## Module 3: Numbers & Time (Zahlen und Zeit)

### Teaching Strategy
Numbers seem simple but are an A1 KILLER in listening exams. The reversed tens system (fünfundzwanzig = 5-and-20) is the #1 trap. Time-telling with "halb" is the #2 trap. Scripts must drill these under speed/pressure.

**Videos:** v3-1-1 (10:00)

#### v3-1-1 Scene Architecture

| 2 | 1:00–3:00 | 0–12 | Each number spoken clearly. Malayalam number parallels where helpful (onnë = eins). Count along. | [SLIDE: 0-12 with audio] ⏸️ repeat |
