## Overview

Wave 4 modules in script priority order:
1. **Module 1** — Welcome to German (pronunciation foundation — everything builds on correct sounds)
2. **Module 7** — Shopping & Money (price conversations, numbers reinforcement, practical Akkusativ)
3. **Module 8** — My Home (Wohnungsanzeigen reading — classic Lesen item, prepositions intro)
4. **Module 11** — Work & Study (email writing — Schreiben prep, Malayali career relevance)
5. **Module 12** — Hobbies & Free Time (invitations — Schreiben Teil 2 template, "weil" clauses intro)

**Wave 4 unifying theme:** These modules are *vocabulary-rich and culturally engaging*. They don't introduce the heaviest grammar (that's Waves 1–3), but they fill critical practical gaps. Scripts should be lively, cultural-bridge heavy, and drill-oriented. Module 1 is a special case: it's the student's first contact with German and must build confidence above all.

## Module 1: Welcome to German! (Willkommen!)

### Teaching Strategy
Module 1 has a unique job: reduce fear and build confidence. Students arrive knowing zero German. By the end, they should be able to greet someone, introduce themselves at a basic level, and pronounce German sounds correctly. The tone must be warm, encouraging, and slow-paced. Malayalam bridges are essential — show students that German is NOT impossible by connecting sounds to familiar Malayalam/English parallels.

### Lesson 1-2: German Sounds & Alphabet
**Videos:** v1-2-1 (12:00), v1-2-2 (10:00)
**Exam section:** ALL — correct pronunciation is foundational for Hören, Sprechen, and reading aloud
**Priority:** HIGH — pronunciation errors compound. Fixing early is 10x cheaper than fixing late.

#### v1-2-1 Scene Architecture: "German Sounds - A Malayali's Guide"

| Scene | Time | Type | Content | Visual |
|-------|------|------|---------|--------|
| 1 | 0:00–0:15 | Pre-roll | | Branding |
| 2 | 0:15–1:00 | Hook | "German has 4 sounds that don't exist in Malayalam or English. Master these 4 and you'll sound better than 90% of Indian German learners." | [SLIDE: "4 Sounds That Change Everything"] |
| 3 | 1:00–2:30 | Umlauts — ä | ä = "eh" sound (like English "bed"). Malayalam parallel: similar to the 'e' in 'ente'. Examples: Mädchen, spät, Käse. | [HIGHLIGHT: ä with mouth position] ⏸️ repeat |
| 4 | 2:30–4:00 | Umlauts — ö | ö = "err" with rounded lips. No direct Malayalam parallel — DEMONSTRATE. "Say 'e' but round your lips like 'o'." Examples: schön, hören, Köln. | [HIGHLIGHT: ö with mouth position] ⏸️ repeat |
| 5 | 4:00–5:30 | Umlauts — ü | ü = "ee" with rounded lips. "Say 'ee' but push lips forward like kissing." Examples: München, über, Tür, grün. | [HIGHLIGHT: ü with mouth position] ⏸️ repeat |
| 6 | 5:30–7:00 | The "ch" sounds | Two "ch" sounds: after a/o/u = "akh" (like Malayalam kh), after e/i = softer "ich" sound (like blowing on glasses). Buch vs ich. | [HIGHLIGHT: ch positions] ⏸️ repeat |
| 7 | 7:00–8:00 | The "sch" sound | sch = "sh" (easy — just like English "ship"!). Schule, Schwester, schön. Malayalam parallel: "sh" in "shesham". | [HIGHLIGHT: sch] ⏸️ |
| 8 | 8:00–9:00 | The "z/ts" sound | z = "ts" (like "tsunami"). Zeit, Zug, zusammen. Common trap: Indians say "z" like English "zoo" — WRONG! | [HIGHLIGHT: z = ts] ⏸️ |
| 9 | 9:00–10:00 | The "w/v" swap | German w = English v! German v = English f! Wasser = "vah-ser", Vater = "fah-ter". This trips up EVERY Indian learner. | [HIGHLIGHT: w↔v swap] ⏸️ |
| 10 | 10:00–11:00 | Quick-fire pronunciation drill | 10 words flashed — student pronounces before hearing correct version. | ⚡ |
| 11 | 11:00–12:00 | Summary + practice assignment | "Record yourself saying these 10 words. Compare with the audio. Do this every day for a week." | [SLIDE: 10-word practice list] |

**Key narration beats:**
- "Only 4 truly new sounds" — reduces fear
- Malayalam parallels wherever possible (kh, sh)
- Physical instructions for mouth position (rounded lips, etc.)
- [EXAM-TIP] "If the examiner can't understand your pronunciation, you lose points in EVERY section — Hören, Sprechen, and even Lesen (reading aloud)"

## Module 7: Shopping & Money (Einkaufen)

### Teaching Strategy
Shopping brings numbers (Module 3) back under real-world pressure. Every price is a listening test. Articles get reinforced through buying things ("Ich kaufe EINEN Apfel" — Akkusativ!). Cultural bridges to Kerala shopping (kirana store vs Supermarkt, bargaining culture vs fixed prices) keep engagement high.
