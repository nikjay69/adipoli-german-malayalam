**Topic: Weak Declension (Schwache Deklination)**
"Weak declension" (Weak endings). Ithu eppozhum definitive articles (der, die, das) undaavumbol upayogikkunnath.
Structure: [Der/Die/Das] + [Adjective + Endings] + [Noun].

- **Nominativ**: "Der gute Kaffee." (The good coffee / Nalla coffee).
- **Akkusativ**: "Ich trinke den guten Kaffee." (I drink the good coffee).
- **Dativ**: "Mit dem guten Kaffee." (With the good coffee).
- **Genitiv**: "Des guten Kaffees." (Of the good coffee).

Note: In weak declension, all plural endings and all oblique cases (Akkusativ masculine, Dativ, Genitiv) end in "-en"! Very easy pattern. (All -en eayittulla word logical set aanu!).

**Topic: Mixed Declension (Gemischte Deklination)**
"Mixed declension" (Mixed endings). Ithu eppozhum indefinite articles (ein, eine, mein, dein, kein) undaavumbol upayogikkunnath.
Structure: [Ein/Meine/Kein] + [Adjective + Endings] + [Noun].

- **Nominativ**: "Ein guter Kaffee." (A good coffee).
- **Akkusativ**: "Ich trinke einen guten Kaffee."
- **Dativ**: "Mit einem guten Kaffee."
- **Genitiv**: "Meines guten Kaffees."

Note: Mixed follows definitively strong endings in Nominativ and Akkusativ (except for masculine -en in Akkusativ), and all -en in other cases. logical flow und!

**Topic: Summary Chart (The "The -en" Dominance)**
If you are in Dativ or Genitiv, OR plural with articles, adjective endings are almost ALWAYS **-en**!
- "Mit dem alten Mann" (Dativ)
- "Mit der schönen Frau" (Dativ)
- "Die neuen Autos" (Plural)

Kerala-yil "Nalla" common alla, but instead different forms of adjectives!
