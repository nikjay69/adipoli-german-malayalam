## Section 2: Formal Time — The 24-Hour Rule (0:50–4:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Digital clock 13:00 to 24:00. Formula appearing: [Stunde] + Uhr + [Minuten].]
### Narration: [ENERGY: precise, logical]
"Formal time is used for official things: trains, flights, appointments, and business hours. It's the 24-hour clock.

[Formula appears]
**[Hour] + Uhr + [Minute]**

No 'Viertel', no 'halb', no 'vor' or 'nach'. Just math!

[Examples]
🔊 **13:00** — dreizehn Uhr.
🔊 **15:30** — fünfzehn Uhr dreißig.
🔊 **08:15** — acht Uhr fünfzehn.
🔊 **20:45** — zwanzig Uhr fünfundvierzig.

*Formal time is very easy: You just say the numbers exactly as you see them! No 'half' or 'quarter' confusion. Just the digits.*

⏸️ **Try: 14:20?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**vierzehn Uhr zwanzig.** ✅

⏸️ **18:10?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**achtzehn Uhr zehn.** ✅"

## Section 3: Informal Time — The Everyday Chat (4:00–6:00)

### Visual: [SCENE: Two friends at a café. Clock showing 3:15. Speech bubble: "Viertel nach drei!"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: relaxed, conversational]
"In daily life, Germans go back to the 12-hour clock.

They use **Viertel (quarter)**, **halb (half)**, **nach (after)**, and **vor (before)**.

- **3:15** — Viertel nach drei.
- **3:30** — halb vier. *(Wait — half to four!)*
- **3:45** — Viertel vor vier.

*Informal time use cheyyumpol 'Uhr' parayilla. Direct 'Viertel nach drei' ennu parayum. 'Uhr' ennu kandaal athu official context aayirikkum.*

⏸️ **Say it: 5:15 (informal)!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Viertel nach fünf.** ✅"

## Section 4: Morning, Afternoon or Night? (6:00–7:30)

### Visual: [ICONS: Rising sun, high sun, moon. Terms: morgens, vormittags, nachmittags, abends, nachts.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: helpful, specifying]
"If you say 'Wir treffen uns um acht' (Let's meet at 8), how do you know if it's 8:00 AM or 8:00 PM? Use these time-of-day words.

- **morgens** — in the morning. (Um acht Uhr morgens.)
- **vormittags** — before noon. (Um elf Uhr vormittags.)
- **nachmittags** — in the afternoon. (Um drei Uhr nachmittags.)
- **abends** — in the evening. (Um acht Uhr abends.)
- **nachts** — at night. (Um eins Uhr nachts.)

*Malayalam logic pole thanne — 'raavile 8 mani' and 'vayikunneram 8 mani'. Exact same pattern!*

⏸️ **Say: 4:00 PM (informal)!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Um vier Uhr nachmittags.** ✅"

## Section 5: The Math Shift (7:30–8:45)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: 15:00 → (15-12) = 3:00 PM.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: practical]
"How to convert formal to informal fast?
If the number is bigger than 12, just **subtract 12**.

- **15 Uhr** → 15 - 12 = **3 Uhr nachmittags.**
- **20 Uhr** → 20 - 12 = **8 Uhr abends.**
- **22 Uhr** → 22 - 12 = **10 Uhr nachts.**

[EXAM-TIP] The Hören (listening) section will often play a **formal** time (like 17:30) but give you an **informal** option (like halb sechs) in the answer. You have to be able to jump between them instantly!"
