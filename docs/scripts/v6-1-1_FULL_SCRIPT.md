## Section 2: Core Vocab Block 1 — Basics (1:30–3:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: Basic foods with articles colour-coded — der=blue, die=red, das=green]
### Narration:

"Let's start with the basics. Articles are colour-coded — learn the colour with the word!"

| Article | Food | English | Memory Tip |
|---------|------|---------|------------|
| **das** Brot | bread | 🟢 "das Brot — the MOST German food" |
| **der** Reis | rice | 🔵 "*Nammalute Reis!* der Reis." |
| **das** Fleisch | meat | 🟢 "Fleisch — sounds like flesh" |
| **der** Fisch | fish | 🔵 "Fish → Fisch — easy!" |
| **die** Butter | butter | 🔴 "die Butter — yes, butter is feminine in German!" |
| **das** Ei / die Eier | egg / eggs | 🟢 "Ei = one egg, Eier = many" |

🔊 Repeat each: "das Brot... der Reis... das Fleisch... der Fisch... die Butter... das Ei..."

⏸️ "Cover the screen. Can you say all 6 with articles?"

## Section 3: Core Vocab Block 2 — Fruits & Vegetables (3:30–5:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Fruits and vegetables]
### Narration:

"Now fruits and vegetables — and two VERY useful collective words."

| Article | Food | English |
|---------|------|---------|
| **das** Obst | fruit (collective) |
| **das** Gemüse | vegetables (collective) |
| **die** Kartoffel | potato |
| **die** Tomate | tomato |
| **der** Apfel | apple |
| **die** Banane | banana |
| **die** Orange | orange |

"*Important:* 'das Obst' and 'das Gemüse' are collective words — no plural needed! 'Ich esse gern Obst' = I like eating fruit."

"*Kartoffel — Germans LOVE potatoes like Malayalis love rice. Kartoffelsalat, Kartoffelpüree, Bratkartoffeln...*"

🔊 Repeat: "die Kartoffel... der Apfel... die Tomate..."

⏸️ "Quick: What article is Kartoffel? ... DIE Kartoffel! 🔴"

## Section 4: The Accusative Connection (5:00–6:30)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: Accusative pattern with food]
### Narration:

"Now here's why food is PERFECT for grammar. Every time you say 'Ich esse...' or 'Ich trinke...', the food becomes the object — and that's ACCUSATIVE."

### Visual: [TABLE: Article changes in accusative]
| Nominative | Accusative | Change? |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **der** Fisch | **den** Fisch | ✅ der → den |
| **die** Tomate | **die** Tomate | ❌ no change |
| **das** Brot | **das** Brot | ❌ no change |

"Only masculine changes! der → den. That's it!"

**[A1-SAFE] The eating pattern:**
- "Ich esse **den** Fisch." (masculine → den)
- "Ich esse **die** Kartoffel." (feminine → no change)
- "Ich esse **das** Brot." (neuter → no change)

🔊 Repeat: "Ich esse den Fisch. Ich esse die Kartoffel. Ich esse das Brot."

⏸️ "Now try: 'I eat the apple.' What's the article? ... den Apfel! Masculine! Ich esse DEN Apfel."

"*Ithre ullu! Masculine maarunnu, baaki maattam illa. Simple!*"

---

## Section 5: Preference Sentences — gern/nicht gern (6:30–8:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: gern pattern with food]
### Narration:

"Now let's add what you LIKE and DON'T like. This pattern is gold for Sprechen!"

**[A1-SAFE] The gern pattern:**
- "Ich esse **gern** Reis." = I like eating rice.
- "Ich esse **nicht gern** Fleisch." = I don't like eating meat.

"Where does 'gern' go? RIGHT AFTER the verb!"

### Visual: [SLIDE: Pattern diagram]
```
Ich + esse + gern + [food]
Ich + trinke + nicht gern + [drink]
```

"Mein Lieblingsessen ist Biryani." = My favourite food is Biryani.

🔊 Repeat: "Ich esse gern Reis."
🔊 Repeat: "Ich esse nicht gern Fleisch."
🔊 Repeat: "Mein Lieblingsessen ist Dosa."

⏸️ "Now say 3 foods you like and 1 you don't like using this pattern."

## Section 6: Cultural Bridge — German vs Kerala Food (8:00–9:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Meal comparison — Germany vs Kerala]
### Narration:

"Quick cultural note that will help you in Germany:"

| Meal | Germany | Kerala |
|------|---------|--------|
| Frühstück (breakfast) | Brot mit Käse und Wurst | Puttu, dosa, appam, idli |
| Mittagessen (lunch) | Warm meal — the BIG meal | Rice + curry — also the big meal! |
| Abendessen (dinner) | Abendbrot — cold bread! | Chapati or rice, warm |

"*Yes, Germans eat COLD bread for dinner. Abendbrot = evening bread. Kerala-il athu sambhavikkilla! 😄*"

"[LIFE-TIP] When invited to a German home for Abendessen, don't expect a warm Kerala-style meal. Bread, cheese, and cold cuts is normal!"
