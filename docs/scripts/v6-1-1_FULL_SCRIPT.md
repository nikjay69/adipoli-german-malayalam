## Section 3: The Accusative Trick — Eating = Grammar (2:30–4:00)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: "Ich esse den Fisch." with article change highlighted]
### Narration: [ENERGY: eureka moment, clear]

"Here's the trick: every time you say 'Ich esse...' (I eat), the food becomes an OBJECT. That's the accusative case.

And here's the beautiful part — only ONE article changes:

[SLIDE: Table with arrows]
| Nominative | Accusative | Change? |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **der** Fisch | **den** Fisch | ✅ der → den |
| **die** Butter | **die** Butter | ❌ same |
| **das** Brot | **das** Brot | ❌ same |

Only masculine changes! der → den. That's the ENTIRE rule.

*Ithre ullu! Masculine maarunnu, baaki maattam illa.*

🔊 **Drill — say each:**
- 'Ich esse **den** Fisch.' (masculine → den!)
- 'Ich esse **die** Butter.' (feminine → no change)
- 'Ich esse **das** Brot.' (neuter → no change)

⏸️ **Try: 'I eat the rice.' What's the article? ... den Reis! Masculine! 'Ich esse DEN Reis.'**

You just used the accusative. Without pain. That's food grammar."

## Section 4: Fruits & Vegetables — 7 More Words (4:00–5:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: Fruits and vegetables, colour-coded]
### Narration: [ENERGY: brisk, building momentum]

"Now fruits and vegetables. Two power words first:

- **das Obst** 🟢 — fruit (collective, no plural needed)
- **das Gemüse** 🟢 — vegetables (same — collective)

'Ich esse gern Obst' = I like eating fruit. Done. No plural drama.

[SLIDE: Individual items appear]
Now the specifics:
- **die Kartoffel** 🔴 — potato. *Germans love Kartoffeln like Malayalis love rice.*
- **die Tomate** 🔴 — tomato.
- **der Apfel** 🔵 — apple.
- **die Banane** 🔴 — banana.
- **die Orange** 🔴 — orange.

🔊 **Repeat:** 'die Kartoffel... der Apfel... die Tomate...'

⏸️ **Quick fire — article?**
Kartoffel → ... die! Apfel → ... der! Tomate → ... die!

⏸️ **Accusative check: 'Ich esse __ Apfel.' What goes in the blank? ... DEN Apfel! Masculine changes!**"

## Section 5: The 'gern' Pattern — Likes & Dislikes (5:30–7:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: gern pattern diagram]
### Narration: [ENERGY: conversational, exam-focused]

"Now the GOLD pattern for Sprechen. How to say what you like eating:

[SLIDE: Formula]
```
Ich + esse + gern + [food]     = I like eating ___
Ich + esse + nicht gern + [food] = I don't like eating ___
```

'gern' goes RIGHT AFTER the verb. Always.

🔊 **Say it:** 'Ich esse gern Reis.'
🔊 **Say it:** 'Ich esse nicht gern Fleisch.'

Now the favourite food sentence:
**'Mein Lieblingsessen ist ___.'** = My favourite food is ___.

*'Lieblingsessen' — favourite food. Ithinu article venda. Just: 'Mein Lieblingsessen ist Biryani!'*

⏸️ **Your turn: Say 2 foods you like, 1 you don't like, and your Lieblingsessen. Four sentences. Go!**

Example: 'Ich esse gern Reis. Ich esse gern Obst. Ich esse nicht gern Fisch. Mein Lieblingsessen ist Dosa.'

That's 4 sentences for Sprechen. You're ready."

## Section 6: Germany vs Kerala — Food Culture (7:00–8:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Meal comparison — split screen]
### Narration: [ENERGY: storytelling, cultural]

"Quick cultural knowledge that saves you from shock in Germany:

[SLIDE: Side-by-side comparison]
| Meal | Germany | Kerala |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Frühstück** (breakfast) | Brot mit Käse und Wurst | Puttu, dosa, appam |
| **Mittagessen** (lunch) | Warm meal — the BIG meal | Rice + curry — also big! |
| **Abendessen** (dinner) | Abendbrot — COLD bread! 😱 | Chapati or rice, warm |

*Yes, Germans eat COLD bread for dinner. 'Abendbrot' = evening bread. Kerala-il athu sambhavikkilla!* 😄

[LIFE-TIP] Invited to a German home for Abendessen? Expect bread, cheese, and cold cuts. Not a warm Kerala-style meal!

⏸️ **Active recall: What's 'Frühstück'? ... breakfast! 'Abendessen'? ... dinner! What makes Abendessen surprising? ... it's cold bread!**"
