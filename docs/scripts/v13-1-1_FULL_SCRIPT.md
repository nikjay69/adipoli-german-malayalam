## Section 2: The Concept — The Sandwich Structure (1:30–3:00)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Animated sandwich — top bread = haben (conjugated, position 2), filling = rest of sentence, bottom bread = past participle (END)]
### Narration:
"German past tense works like a **sandwich**. Or if you remember from Module 5 — like the separable verb **frame**.

[DIAGRAM: Large animated sandwich building itself]

### The Perfekt Sandwich:
**[Subject] + [haben conjugated] + ... middle stuff ... + [past participle at END]**

Example:
**Ich** + **habe** + Deutsch + **gelernt**.

The conjugated verb (haben) goes in **position 2** — as always in German.
The past participle goes to the **END** — just like separable prefixes!

*Sandwich pole aalochikkuka: bread = haben ... gelernt, filling = middle-il enthokke und.*

[DIAGRAM: colour-coded sentence]
- Blue: **Ich** (subject)
- Red: **habe** (haben, conjugated — position 2)
- Black: Deutsch (middle stuff)
- Green: **gelernt** (past participle — END!)

This frame NEVER changes. Subject → haben → stuff → participle.

⏸️ **[PAUSE — Student identifies the parts: 'Er hat Fußball gespielt.' → Subject? haben form? Participle?]**"

## Section 3: haben Conjugation — Quick Review (3:00–4:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: haben conjugation, colour-coded in red]
### Narration:
"You already know haben from Module 4 (Ich habe einen Bruder). Quick review:

### haben conjugation:
| Person | Form | Example |
|--------|------|---------|
| ich | **habe** | Ich habe ... gelernt |
| du | **hast** | Du hast ... gespielt |
| er/sie/es | **hat** | Er hat ... gekocht |
| wir | **haben** | Wir haben ... gemacht |
| ihr | **habt** | Ihr habt ... gehört |
| sie/Sie | **haben** | Sie haben ... gearbeitet |

Notice: **er/sie/es hat** — no -e at the end! This is the irregular part. Everything else follows the normal pattern.

*Haben nammukku ariyaam — Module 4-il padichchu. Ippol athine past tense-nu use cheyyunnathaa!*

⏸️ **[PAUSE — Quick drill: 'ich...?' → 'habe', 'du...?' → 'hast', 'er...?' → 'hat']**"

## Section 4: The Malayalam Connection (4:00–4:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Side-by-side Malayalam and German verb changes]
### Narration:
"Good news — Malayalam also changes verbs for the past!

[TABLE: parallel]
| Malayalam | German |
|-----------|--------|
| cheyyunnu (doing) | mache (do) |
| **cheythu** (did) | **gemacht** (done) |
| kazhikkunnu (eating) | esse (eat) |

See? The concept is the same — the verb form changes to show past. In German, the change follows a clear pattern: **ge-** at the start, **-t** at the end (for regular verbs).

*Malayalam-ilum German-ilum same concept — verb form maarunnu! German-il ge...t pattern undu.*"

## Section 5: Building the Past Participle — Regular Verbs (4:30–6:30)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Large animated formula: ge- + STEM + -t, with colour coding: ge=green, stem=blue, t=red]
### Narration:
"For regular verbs, the past participle follows ONE formula:

### The Formula:
# ge- + STEM + -t

[DIAGRAM: machen → ge + mach + t → **gemacht**]

Let's build some:

[TABLE: Each row appears one by one with animation]

| Infinitive | Stem | Past Participle | Meaning |
|------------|------|-----------------|---------|
| **machen** | mach | **ge**mach**t** | done/made |
| **spielen** | spiel | **ge**spiel**t** | played |
| **lernen** | lern | **ge**lern**t** | learned |
| **kochen** | koch | **ge**koch**t** | cooked |
| **kaufen** | kauf | **ge**kauf**t** | bought |
| **hören** | hör | **ge**hör**t** | heard |
| **wohnen** | wohn | **ge**wohn**t** | lived |
| **fragen** | frag | **ge**frag**t** | asked |
| **sagen** | sag | **ge**sag**t** | said |
| **suchen** | such | **ge**such**t** | searched |

Ten regular verbs — all follow the SAME pattern. ge- + stem + -t. No exceptions!

⏸️ **[PAUSE — Student builds 3 participles from new verbs: tanzen → ? (getanzt), rauchen → ? (geraucht), brauchen → ? (gebraucht)]**

### Special case: stems ending in -t or -d

When the stem ends in **-t** or **-d**, add **-et** instead of just **-t** (for pronunciation):

- **arbeiten** → arbeit → ge**arbeit**et *(not 'gearbeitt' — impossible to pronounce!)*
- **warten** → wart → ge**wart**et
- **antworten** → antwort → ge**antwort**et

*Just an extra -e- for pronunciation. The pattern is still the same!*"

## Section 6: Putting It All Together — Full Sentences (6:30–8:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Sentences building themselves piece by piece, colour-coded: subject=blue, haben=red, middle=black, participle=green]
### Narration:
"Now let's put the sandwich together with real sentences:

[Each sentence builds piece by piece on screen]

1. **Ich habe Deutsch gelernt.**
   *I learned German.*
   [Ich] + [habe] + Deutsch + [gelernt]

2. **Du hast Fußball gespielt.**
   *You played football.*
   [Du] + [hast] + Fußball + [gespielt]

3. **Er hat Curry gekocht.**
   *He cooked curry.*
   [Er] + [hat] + Curry + [gekocht]

4. **Wir haben ein Buch gekauft.**
   *We bought a book.*
   [Wir] + [haben] + ein Buch + [gekauft]

5. **Sie hat Musik gehört.**
   *She listened to music.*
   [Sie] + [hat] + Musik + [gehört]

6. **Ich habe den ganzen Tag gearbeitet.**
   *I worked the whole day.*
   [Ich] + [habe] + den ganzen Tag + [gearbeitet]

  - 'you (du) / to play / tennis' → ?

### The Rule That Never Changes:
**haben = position 2. Past participle = LAST WORD.**

Even with long sentences:
- **Ich habe gestern Abend mit meiner Mutter am Telefon gesprochen.** — Past participle (gesprochen) is STILL at the end!

*Sandwich close cheyyenda sthalathu cheyyuka — past participle always at the END!*"

## Section 7: Common Mistakes — Don't Do This! (8:00–8:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Red X on wrong sentences, green checkmark on correct versions]
### Narration:
"Three mistakes everyone makes. Don't be everyone:

❌ **Ich habe gelernt Deutsch.** — Participle is NOT at the end!
✅ **Ich habe Deutsch gelernt.** — Participle goes to the END.

❌ **Ich habe Deutsch lernen.** — That's the infinitive, not participle!
✅ **Ich habe Deutsch gelernt.** — Past participle: ge- + stem + -t.

❌ **Ich hat gekocht.** — Wrong haben form for 'ich'!
✅ **Ich habe gekocht.** — ich = habe, er/sie/es = hat.

*Number one mistake: participle end-il vaykkaathirikkal. ALWAYS at the end. Sandwich close cheyyuka!*"
