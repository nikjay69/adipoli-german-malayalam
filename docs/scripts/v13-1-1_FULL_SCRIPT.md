## Section 2: The Sandwich — How Perfekt Works (0:50–2:30)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Animated sandwich. Top bread = haben (red, position 2). Filling = middle stuff. Bottom bread = past participle (green, END). Builds step by step.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, visual]
"German past tense = a **sandwich**.

[DIAGRAM builds]

### The Perfekt Sandwich:
**[Subject] + [haben] + ... stuff ... + [past participle]**

🔊 **Ich habe Deutsch gelernt.**

- **Ich** = subject
- **habe** = haben, conjugated — ALWAYS position 2 (red)
- **Deutsch** = middle stuff
- **gelernt** = past participle — ALWAYS at the END (green)

*Sandwich pole: bread = haben ... gelernt. Filling = middle-il enthokke und.*

This frame NEVER changes. No matter how long the sentence:

🔊 **Ich habe gestern mit meiner Schwester Curry gekocht.**

Participle? Still at the END. **gekocht.**

⏸️ Spot the participle: 'Er hat den ganzen Tag Fußball gespielt.' Which word is the past participle?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**gespielt.** ✅ Last word. Always."

| wir | **haben** |

## Section 4: Building Past Participles — The ge...t Formula (3:15–5:00)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Animated formula: ge- (green) + STEM (blue) + -t (red). Then table building row by row.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: methodical, building]
"For regular verbs, the past participle follows ONE formula:

### ge- + STEM + -t

[DIAGRAM: machen → ge + mach + t → **gemacht**]

Let's build them:

| Infinitive | → Past Participle |
|------------|-------------------|
| **machen** → **gemacht** | done/made |
| **spielen** → **gespielt** | played |
| **lernen** → **gelernt** | learned |
| **kochen** → **gekocht** | cooked |
| **kaufen** → **gekauft** | bought |
| **hören** → **gehört** | heard |
| **fragen** → **gefragt** | asked |
| **sagen** → **gesagt** | said |

Eight verbs — ALL the same pattern. ge- + stem + -t.

⏸️ Your turn — build the participle for **tanzen** (to dance):

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**getanzt.** ✅ ge + tanz + t.

⏸️ Now **suchen** (to search):

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**gesucht.** ✅

---

### Small twist: stems ending in -t or -d

Add **-et** instead of just -t (otherwise you can't pronounce it):

- **arbeiten** → ge**arbeit**et *(not 'gearbeitt'!)*
- **warten** → ge**wart**et
- **antworten** → ge**antwort**et

*Pronunciation-nu vendi extra -e- idunnu. Pattern same thanne.*"

## Section 7: The 3 Mistakes Everyone Makes (7:00–7:45)

### Visual: [SLIDE: ❌ / ✅ pairs, large and clear]
### Narration: [ENERGY: alert, emphatic]
"Three mistakes. Don't make them.

❌ **Ich habe gelernt Deutsch.** — Participle NOT at end!
✅ **Ich habe Deutsch gelernt.**

❌ **Ich habe Deutsch lernen.** — That's the infinitive, not the participle!
✅ **Ich habe Deutsch gelernt.** — ge- + stem + -t.

❌ **Ich hat gekocht.** — Wrong haben form!
✅ **Ich habe gekocht.** — ich = habe, er = hat.

⏸️ Fix this: 'Wir hat Fußball gespielt.'

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

✅ **Wir haben Fußball gespielt.** — wir = haben, not hat."
