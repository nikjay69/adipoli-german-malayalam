## Section 1: Hook — The Kerala Kudumbam (0:15–0:50)

### Visual: [SCENE: A big family gathering in Kerala (wedding style). "Chittappan, Ammavan, Mami...". German words appearing over them.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, relatable]
"In Kerala, we don't just have families. We have *kudumbams*. Uncles, aunts, cousins, in-laws — everyone is part of the circle.

In German, describing this big circle is actually easier than in Malayalam! They don't have ten different words for 'Uncle'. They have one. Today, we're building your extended family tree. Let's grow those roots!"

---

## Section 2: Uncles and Aunts — The Simpler Way (0:50–2:15)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Maternal side and Paternal side. Both pointing to: der ONKEL and die TANTE.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: steady, clear]
"Malayalam-il Ammavan, Chittappan, Valiyachan... ingane kuraye perundu. German-il athu simple aanu.

[Words appear]
🔊 **der Onkel** — *Uncle. Any uncle! Maternal, paternal, the one from the Gulf... he's just an Onkel.*
🔊 **die Tante** — *Aunt. Same rule!*

*Pro tip: 'Tante' rhymes with 'Aunty' if you say it with a German 'e' at the end!*

⏸️ **Try: My Uncle? (der Onkel)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**mein Onkel.** ✅

⏸️ **My Aunt? (die Tante)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**meine Tante.** ✅"

---

## Section 3: The Cousins — French Style (2:15–4:00)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: 1. der COUSIN (Male) | 2. die COUSINE (Female). Pronunciation guide appearing.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: attentive, phonetic]
"German took these words from French, so the pronunciation is a bit 'fancy'.

[Rows appear]
- **der Cousin** — *Pronounced like 'koo-zaN'. The 'n' is very soft, like you're almost not saying it.*
- **die Cousine** — *'koo-zee-nuh'. Clear and simple.*

*Malayalam-il nammal 'Cousin' ennu parayum, pakshe German-il gender matters! Male cousin aanenkil 'Cousin' (der), female aanenkil 'Cousine' (die).*

⏸️ **Say: My (male) cousin.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**mein Cousin.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: The Next Generation — Neffe and Nichte (4:00–5:30)

### Visual: [ICONS: Little boy (Neffe), Little girl (Nichte). "Nephew / Niece".]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"If you have brothers and sisters, they might have children.

🔊 **der Neffe** (Nephew) — *Think of English 'N-e-p-h-ew'.*
🔊 **die Nichte** (Niece) — *'nikh-tuh'. There's our 'ch' sound again!*

⏸️ **Say: 17? (No, wait - the Nichte! 'nikh-tuh')**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**die Nichte.** ✅"

---

## Section 5: The In-Laws — The 'Schwieger' Prefix (5:30–7:00)

### Visual: [FORMULA: Schwieger + [Family Word] = In-Law. Example: Schwieger + Vater = Schwiegervater.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: systematic]
"Married? Then you have 'In-Laws'. In German, we just add the word **'Schwieger'** (shvee-ger) in front.

[Table appears]
- **der Schwiegervater** (Father-in-law)
- **die Schwiegermutter** (Mother-in-law)
- **der Schwiegersohn** (Son-in-law)
- **die Schwiegertochter** (Daughter-in-law)

*Easy rule: If you know the basic words, you just stick 'Schwieger' in front and you're done!*

⏸️ **What's 'Mother-in-law'?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Schwiegermutter.** ✅"

---

## Section 6: Malayalam Comparison — The Relationship Depth (7:00–8:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: Malayalam (Complex) vs. German (Simple). Focus on 'Uncle' mapping.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, structural]
"In Kerala, we treat relationships with many specific names to show status and family side.

In Germany, the focus is on gender and basic category. Whether it's your mother's brother or your father's brother, they are both just **'mein Onkel'**.

*German-il relatives-inte names orkkunathu chila nerathe nammude Malayali logic-ekkkaalum easy aanu!*

[EXAM-TIP] In the **Sprechen** (speaking) exam, if you forget a specific family word, just use 'mein Verwandter' (my relative). Better to be general and fluent than specific and stuck!"

⚡ Uncle? → **der Onkel!**

Adipoli! You've just expanded your family tree significantly."
