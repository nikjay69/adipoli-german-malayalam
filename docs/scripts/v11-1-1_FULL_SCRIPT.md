### Scene 3: The Gender Rule (1:00–2:30)
**[TABLE: Masculine / Feminine profession pairs]**

| Masculine | Feminine | English |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| der Lehrer | die Lehrerin | teacher |
| der Arzt | die Ärztin | doctor |
| der Student | die Studentin | student |
| der Ingenieur | die Ingenieurin | engineer |

**Narrator:**
> "THE rule for German professions: masculine is the base form. Add '-in' for feminine."
>
> "der Lehrer → die LehrerIN"
> "der Student → die StudentIN"
> "der Ingenieur → die IngenieurIN"
>
> "Sometimes there's an umlaut change too:"
> "der Arzt → die ÄrztIN (a → ä)"
> "der Koch → die KöchIN (o → ö)"
>
> ⏸️ **[Practice: What's the feminine of 'der Musiker'? → die Musikerin!]**
>
> "This is one of German's most REGULAR patterns. Learn the masculine form, add '-in', done!"
>
> "Malayalam comparison: we also have some of this! 'adhyaapakan' vs 'adhyaapika' — same concept, different ending."

### Scene 7: Saying Your Profession (7:00–8:00)
**[SLIDE: Three ways to state your profession]**

**Narrator:**
> "Three ways to say what you do:"
>
> "Pattern 1: 'Ich bin Krankenpflegerin.' — I am a nurse. NO article! In German, you drop 'eine/ein' with professions."
>
> "Pattern 2: 'Ich bin ... von Beruf.' — I am a ... by profession. 'Ich bin Ingenieur von Beruf.' More formal."
>
> "Pattern 3: 'Ich arbeite als Programmiererin.' — I work as a programmer. Uses 'als' = as."
>
> ⏸️ **[Say your profession using all three patterns]**
>
> "IMPORTANT: NO article! 'Ich bin EIN Arzt' is grammatically possible but sounds odd. Just: 'Ich bin Arzt.' Direct and clean."

### Scene 9: Forms & Exam Practice (9:00–10:30)
**[SLIDE: Form with Beruf field highlighted]**

**Narrator:**

> "For Sprechen Teil 1: 'Was machen Sie beruflich?' or 'Was sind Sie von Beruf?' — answer with: 'Ich bin [profession].' or 'Ich arbeite als [profession].'"

> "Fun fact: Germans LOVE asking this question when they meet someone new. It's one of the first things they'll ask after your name and where you're from. In Kerala, people might ask about your family first — in Germany, it's your JOB!"
