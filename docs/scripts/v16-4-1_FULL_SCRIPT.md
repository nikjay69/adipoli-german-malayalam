## Section 2: The Master Rule — Quick Review (0:45–1:45)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Normal sentence with verb in position 2 → arrow showing verb sliding to the end after connector]
### Narration:
🎬 [Energy: clear, precise]

"Let's lock the rule one more time:

**Normal sentence:**
> Ich **bin** krank.
Verb in position 2. Normal.

**Subordinate clause:**
> ..., weil ich krank **bin**.
After the connector — weil, dass, wenn — the conjugated verb **goes to the very end**.

[SLIDE: Three connectors side by side]
- **weil** = because (reason)
- **dass** = that (reporting/thinking)
- **wenn** = when / if (condition/time)

Three different meanings. **ONE rule.** Verb goes to the end. Always.

Nammude Malayalam-il entha karyam? Verb normally end-il varum! 'Ñān Deutsch padikkunnu, kāraṇam ñān Germany-il jeevikkāṇam **āgrahikkunnu**.' Malayalam normal order = German subordinate order!"

⏸️ Say the rule out loud: "After weil, dass, wenn — verb goes to the END."

## Section 3: weil — Upgrade Your Reasons (1:45–3:15)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "weil = because → REASON"]
### Narration:
🎬 [Energy: warm, familiar — "you know this"]

"**weil** already ninte friend aanu from Module 12. Let's upgrade with slightly longer sentences:

**Short (you already know this):**
> Ich lerne Deutsch, **weil** ich in Deutschland arbeiten **möchte**.

**Longer (new level!):**
> Ich kann nicht kommen, **weil** ich morgen eine Prüfung **habe**.
> Er ist müde, **weil** er gestern bis 23 Uhr gearbeitet **hat**.

Notice something? Even with Perfekt (gearbeitet **hat**), the helping verb — **hat** — still goes to the end!

[SLIDE: Perfekt in weil-clause: "...weil er gearbeitet **hat**" with arrow to hat at end]

Two verbs in a weil-clause? The conjugated one (habe/hat/bin/ist/kann/muss) goes to the end. The other stays where it is.

⏸️ Now you try. Complete this sentence:
'Ich bleibe zu Hause, weil es draußen kalt ...' — what verb goes at the end?"

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (5 seconds)

"**ist**! 'Ich bleibe zu Hause, weil es draußen kalt **ist**.' ✅

⏸️ One more: 'Sie kommt nicht, weil sie arbeiten ...'?"

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (5 seconds)

"**muss**! 'Sie kommt nicht, weil sie arbeiten **muss**.' ✅"

## Section 4: dass — Reporting What You Think and Know (3:15–5:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "dass = that → THINKING / SAYING / KNOWING"]
### Narration:
🎬 [Energy: fresh, new — "unlock #2"]

"Now the second connector: **dass**. English-il 'that' — but German-il verb end-il pokum.

**When do we use dass?** When you report thoughts, opinions, or what someone said.

**Trigger verbs** — these words love dass:
- **Ich denke,** dass ...  (I think that...)
- **Ich glaube,** dass ... (I believe that...)
- **Ich hoffe,** dass ...  (I hope that...)
- **Ich weiß,** dass ...   (I know that...)
- **Er sagt,** dass ...    (He says that...)

Let's build three sentences:

[SLIDE: Building one sentence at a time]

**1.** Ich denke, **dass** Deutsch interessant **ist**.
*(I think that German is interesting.)*

⏸️ Repeat: 'Ich denke, dass Deutsch interessant ist.'

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (5 seconds)

**2.** Ich hoffe, **dass** du morgen **kommst**.
*(I hope that you come tomorrow.)*

⏸️ Repeat.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (5 seconds)

**3.** Er sagt, **dass** er kein Fleisch **isst**.
*(He says that he doesn't eat meat.)*

⏸️ Repeat.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (5 seconds)

Good! Same rule — dass pushes the verb to the end. Exactly like weil.

> [EXAM-TIP] 📝 In **Schreiben**, 'Ich denke, dass...' and 'Ich hoffe, dass...' make your writing sound more mature without risky grammar. Safe complexity!"

## Section 5: wenn — Conditions and Repeated Actions (5:00–6:45)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "wenn = when / if → CONDITION / TIME"]
### Narration:
🎬 [Energy: energetic, practical — "unlock #3"]

"Third connector: **wenn**. Two meanings:

**Meaning 1: IF (condition)**
> **Wenn** es regnet, bleibe ich zu Hause.
*(If it rains, I stay home.)*

**Meaning 2: WHEN (repeated / present)**
> **Wenn** ich nach Hause komme, koche ich.
*(When I come home, I cook.)*

Same word, same rule. Verb → end.

Let's build:

**1.** Wenn ich Zeit **habe**, lese ich gern.
*(When I have time, I like to read.)*

⏸️ Repeat: 'Wenn ich Zeit habe, lese ich gern.'

*(When it's cold, I put on a jacket.)*

⏸️ Repeat.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (5 seconds)

**3.** Wenn du Hilfe **brauchst**, ruf mich an!

⏸️ Repeat.

## Section 5b: The Word-Order Flip ⚠️ (6:45–7:30)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: wenn-clause FIRST → main clause starts with VERB]
### Narration:
🎬 [Energy: alert — "important trap!"]

"Oru important trick! When the **wenn-clause comes FIRST**, the main clause flips — it starts with the verb:

**wenn-clause first:**
> **Wenn** es regnet, **bleibe** ich zu Hause.

See? After the comma, the main clause starts with **bleibe** (verb), not **ich** (subject).

**Main clause first (normal):**
> Ich bleibe zu Hause, **wenn** es regnet.

Both sentences mean the same thing. The difference? Where the wenn-clause sits.

**Simple rule:** wenn first → main verb jumps to position 1 after the comma.

'Ithokke complicated aano?' ennu thonnunnundo? Actually, Malayalam-ilum same aanu — 'Mazha peythaal, ñān veetil irikum.' Condition first, then result. Same flow!

⏸️ Try converting: 'Ich koche, wenn ich nach Hause komme.' → Now put wenn first."

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (6 seconds)

"'**Wenn** ich nach Hause **komme**, **koche** ich.' ✅ Verb-verb at the comma — that's the pattern!"

## Section 8: Common Mistakes Spotlight (9:30–9:50)

### Visual: [SLIDE: ❌ vs ✅]
### Narration:
🎬 [Energy: quick, fix-it]

"Three common mistakes — avoid these:

**❌** Ich denke, dass Deutsch **ist** interessant.
**✅** Ich denke, dass Deutsch interessant **ist**.
→ Verb must go to the END, not stay in position 2!

**❌** Wenn es regnet, **ich bleibe** zu Hause.
**✅** Wenn es regnet, **bleibe ich** zu Hause.
→ After wenn-clause first, the main verb jumps to position 1!

**❌** Ich lerne Deutsch **dass** ich es brauche.
**✅** Ich lerne Deutsch, **weil** ich es brauche.
→ dass ≠ weil! dass = reporting/thinking, weil = giving a reason."
