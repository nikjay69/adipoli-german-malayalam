## Section 1: Hook — The German Calendar Sync (0:15–0:50)

### Visual: [SCENE: Friendly German showing you their phone calendar full of colors. "Hast du am Montag um drei Uhr Zeit?"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: direct, professional]

'We'll see' isn't a plan in Germany. 'Monday at 3:00' is a plan.

Today, we're combining everything: the days, the numbers, and the time. You'll move from 'What time is it?' to 'Let's meet at 5:00!'. This is your conversation engine. Let's build it!"

---

## Section 2: Question Words — Wann vs Um wie viel Uhr (0:50–2:15)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: Wann? (When?) — General. Um wie viel Uhr? (At what time?) — Specific.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, simple]
"To make a plan, you need to ask.

- **Wann?** (When?) — *The big picture.*
- **Um wie viel Uhr?** (At what time?) — *The exact hour.*

[Examples]
🔊 **'Wann haben Sie Zeit?'** (When do you have time? — formal)
🔊 **'Um wie viel Uhr treffen wir uns?'** (At what time do we meet? — casual)

⏸️ **Try: At what time? (Informal)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Um wie viel Uhr?** ✅"

---

## Section 3: The Preposition Trio — am, um, im (2:15–4:30)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: am + Tag | um + Uhr | im + Monat/Jahreszeit. Three colored boxes.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: focused, instructional]
"The biggest grammar trap in scheduling? Choosing the right small word (preposition).

[Rules appear]
- **am** — for days. (**am** Montag, **am** Freitag.)
- **um** — for time. (**um** drei Uhr, **um** halb fünf.)
- **im** — for months and seasons. (**im** Januar, **im** Sommer.)

*Note: Germans are very consistent here. Day = AM. Time = UM. Month = IM.*

⏸️ **Drill: 3:00? (um or am?)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**um drei Uhr.** ✅

⏸️ **Friday? (um or am?)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**am Freitag.** ✅

⏸️ **Summer? (im or am?)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**im Sommer.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: Time Ranges — von ... bis ... (4:30–6:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Digital Clock Range: 09:00 ←----------→ 17:00. Formula: von [X] bis [Y].]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"When does the shop open? How long is the course? Use **von (from)** and **bis (to)**.

🔊 **'Ich arbeite von neun bis fünf Uhr.'**
🔊 **'Der Kurs ist von Montag bis Freitag.'**

*Ithu nammude business hours and appointment ranges-nu useful aanu. 'Von ... bis ...' combo eppolum correct aayirikkum.*

⏸️ **Say it: From Monday to Wednesday!**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Von Montag bis Mittwoch.** ✅"

## Section 5: Role-Play 1 — The Doctor (6:00–8:15)

### Visual: [SCENE: Doctor's receptionist desk. Dialogue appearing line by line.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: professional, coaching]
"Let's make a formal appointment.

🔊 Receptionist: **'Guten Tag, Praxis Schmidt. Wie kann ich Ihnen helfen?'**
🔊 You: **'Guten Tag. Ich möchte einen Termin, bitte.'**
🔊 Receptionist: **'Haben Sie am Dienstag um zehn Uhr Zeit?'**

⏸️ **How do you answer: 'Yes, that works!'?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**'Ja, das geht. Vielen Dank!'** ✅

*Note: 'Das geht' — literally 'That goes'. Use it as 'That's fine!'.*

🔊 Receptionist: **'Alles klar. Bis Dienstag um zehn Uhr!'**"

## Section 6: Role-Play 2 — The Friend (8:15–10:00)

### Visual: [SCENE: Two friends on WhatsApp/Chat screen. Icons for cinema and coffee.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: relaxed, friendly]
"Let's make a casual plan.

🔊 Friend: **'Hi! Hast du morgen Zeit?'**
🔊 You: **'Ja, gerne! Wann?'**
🔊 Friend: **'Können wir uns am Samstag um halb fünf treffen?'**

⏸️ **How do you say: 'No, but what about Sunday?'?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (5 seconds)
**'Nein, am Samstag habe ich keine Zeit. Aber am Sonntag?'** ✅

🔊 Friend: **'Okay, Sonntag geht auch! Bis dann!'**"

## Section 7: Malayalam Logic — The Punctuality Culture (10:00–11:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: "Malayali 3:00 PM" (3:15) vs "German 3:00 PM" (2:55).]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, relatable]
"In Kerala, if you say 'meeting at 3', arriving at 3:15 is often normal.

In Germany, that is a disaster. If you have a **Termin** at 3:00, the German arrives at 2:55.

Being **'pünktlich'** (on time) is a sign of respect. If you're going to be late (even by 5 minutes!), send a message: **'Entschuldigung, ich komme fünf Minuten später.'**

[EXAM-TIP] The examiners love the word **'pünktlich'**. Use it in your speaking tasks: 'Ich bin immer pünktlich!' — they'll believe you!"

## Section 8: Speed Recap (11:30–12:30)

### Visual: [FLASHCARDS: pronoun + day + time. Student builds a sentence.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, timer]
"Final challenge. I give the day and time, YOU build the full 'on/at' sentence.

1. **Montag + 9:00?** → **Am Montag um neun Uhr!**
2. **Samstag + halb drei?** → **Am Samstag um halb drei!**
3. **März + Sommer?** → **Im März / Im Sommer!**
4. **9:00 to 5:00?** → **Von neun bis fünf!**
5. **Today?** → **Heute!**

Excellent! If you've got 'am-um-im' locked, you're the master of the calendar."
