## Why this revision exists

This v2.1 pass fixes two common problems:
- learners focus too much on "30 words" and not enough on the **3 content points**
- learners memorize one informal template and then use it in a more formal situation

So this version now teaches:
- a cleaner **read → plan → write → check** workflow
- a safer **informal vs formal** decision rule
- shorter, more reusable message building blocks
- stronger self-checking before time runs out

## Section 3: The 4-Step Workflow (1:25–2:20)

### Visual: [CHECKLIST CARD]
```text
1. Read
2. Plan
3. Write
4. Check
```
### Narration: [ENERGY: coach-like]
"Use this same workflow every time.

### 1. Read
Who are you writing to? What are the 3 points?

### 2. Plan
Decide: informal or formal?
Then think: one short line for each point.

### 3. Write
Greeting + 3 short content lines + closing.

### 4. Check
Did you hit all 3 points? Is the register right? Is the message still simple?

*Read cheyyuka. Plan cheyyuka. Write cheyyuka. Pinne check cheyyuka. Ee order safe aanu.*"

## Section 4: Register Decision — Informal or Formal? (2:20–3:20)

### Visual: [two-column card: INFORMAL / FORMAL]
### Narration: [ENERGY: practical, protective]
"This is the decision that saves many marks.

### Informal
Friend, classmate, cousin, known person
- **Liebe Anna,** / **Lieber Max,**
- **Viele Grüße** / **Liebe Grüße**

### Formal
Office, company, landlord, doctor's office, unknown contact
- **Sehr geehrte Frau Weber,**
- **Sehr geehrter Herr Khan,**
- **Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren,**
- **Mit freundlichen Grüßen**

Safe rule:
if the relationship is professional or official, go formal.

[EXAM-TIP] When in doubt in an official context, formal is safer than casual.

⏸️ "Arztpraxis" — informal or formal?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

Formal. Exactly."

## Section 5: The Golden Message Shape (3:20–4:10)

### Visual: [template skeleton builds line by line]
```text
[Greeting]

[Point 1]
[Point 2]
[Point 3]

[Closing]
[Your name]
```
### Narration: [ENERGY: reassuring]
"This is the whole shape.

Not one huge paragraph.
Not six complicated sentences.
Just this:
- greeting
- 3 short lines
- closing

One point per sentence is usually the safest choice.
If two points fit naturally into one short sentence pair, that's also okay.
But never let one bullet disappear.

*Oru bullet = oru sentence enn karuthiyaal miss kuravakum.*"

## Section 7: Worked Example 2 — Formal Message (5:15–6:30)

### Visual: [teacher/course prompt with 3 ticks appearing]
### Narration: [ENERGY: careful, score-safe]
"Now a more formal case.

**Prompt:** You cannot come to the German course tomorrow.
Write to **Frau Weber**.
- apologize
- give the reason
- ask about homework

Safer exam version:

```text
Sehr geehrte Frau Weber,

es tut mir leid. Ich kann morgen nicht zum Kurs kommen.
Ich bin krank.
Können Sie mir bitte die Hausaufgaben schicken?

Mit freundlichen Grüßen
Arun
```

Why this works:
- point 1: apology ✅
- point 2: reason ✅
- point 3: homework question ✅
- register: formal ✅

Notice the style: small, safe sentences. No fancy grammar needed."

## Section 9: The A1-Safe Sentence Bank (7:40–8:35)

### Visual: [compact printable card]
### Narration: [ENERGY: toolkit mode]
"Here are some high-utility message lines.

### Apology / cancellation
- **Es tut mir leid.**
- **Ich kann leider nicht kommen.**
- **Ich bin krank.**
- **Ich muss arbeiten.**

### Planning / suggesting
- **Ich komme um ...**
- **Treffen wir uns um ...**
- **Können wir uns am ... treffen?**
- **Danach können wir ...**

### Questions
- **Können Sie mir bitte ... schicken?**
- **Wann beginnt ... ?**
- **Was soll ich mitbringen?**

### Social lines
- **Vielen Dank für die Einladung.**
- **Herzlichen Glückwunsch zum Geburtstag!**

Memorize a few. Reuse them safely."
