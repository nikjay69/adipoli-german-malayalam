## Section 3: The Golden Template (1:45–3:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Template skeleton — greeting, body, closing colour-coded]
```
Liebe/Lieber [Name],     ← GREETING (gender match)

[Sentence 1 — Point 1]   ← Content point 1
[Sentence 2 — Point 2]   ← Content point 2
[Sentence 3 — Point 3]   ← Content point 3

Viele Grüße              ← CLOSING
[Your Name]
```

### Narration: [ENERGY: confident, steady]
"Memorize this. Use it every time.

**Greeting:** 'Lieber' for a man, 'Liebe' for a woman, plus their name.

**Body:** One sentence per content point. Three points, three sentences. Short and correct beats long and broken.

**Closing:** 'Viele Grüße' works for almost everything informal.

*Template ready. Ellam ithil fit aakum.*

Now — formal or informal? How do you know?

[SLIDE: Decision flowchart]
- Prompt says 'Ihr Freund / Ihre Freundin' → **informal** (Liebe/Lieber + Viele Grüße)
- Prompt says 'Ihre Lehrerin / eine Firma' → **formal** (Sehr geehrte/r + Mit freundlichen Grüßen)

[EXAM-TIP] 90% of exam prompts are informal. Default to 'Liebe/Lieber + Viele Grüße' unless you see Firma, Büro, or a clear formal relationship.

⏸️ Quick: Friend named Maria — what greeting? Say it.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

'Liebe Maria,' — perfect."

```
- Wann kommen Sie wieder?
- Fragen Sie nach den Hausaufgaben.
```

Notice: writing to your *teacher*, Frau Weber. Still informal in a German course context, but respectful.

[SLIDE: Model answer, points ticked off]
```
Liebe Frau Weber,

ich kann morgen leider nicht zum Kurs kommen,
weil ich krank bin.
Ich komme am Mittwoch wieder.
Können Sie mir bitte die Hausaufgaben schicken?

Viele Grüße

```

Checkpoint:
- ✅ Point 1 — Why? → 'weil ich krank bin'
- ✅ Point 2 — When back? → 'am Mittwoch'
- ✅ Point 3 — Homework? → 'Können Sie mir bitte die Hausaufgaben schicken?'

Three useful phrases from this:
- **ich kann leider nicht kommen** — I unfortunately can't come
- **weil ich krank bin** — because I'm sick
- **Können Sie mir bitte ... schicken?** — Can you please send me...?

[EXAM-TIP] Memorize 2-3 per category. They'll cover almost every exam prompt.

## Section 8: 5 Mistakes That Cost Marks (8:00–9:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: 5 numbered mistakes — ❌ → ✅ corrections]
### Narration: [ENERGY: alert, preventive]
"Five mistakes — learn them now, avoid them on exam day.

**#1: No greeting or closing.**
Always: 'Liebe/Lieber [Name],' ... 'Viele Grüße, [your name].'

**#2: Missing a content point.**
After writing, count: Point 1? ✅ Point 2? ✅ Point 3? ✅

**#3: Writing too much.**
30 words is enough. 100 words = more errors, wasted time. *Simple and correct > fancy and broken.*

**#4: Wrong register.**
'Ihr Freund' → informal. 'Eine Firma' → formal. Match your greeting.

**#5: Using risky vocabulary.**
If you're not sure about a word, use a simpler one. 'Ich komme am Samstag' beats a complex sentence with 3 errors.

⏸️ Which mistake do YOU think you'd make? Be honest.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)"

- **Hook cut from 45s to 30s:** Removed redundant setup, went straight to "3 sentences = 12.5%"

- **Energy cues added** at every section transition for narrator guidance
- **Sentence bank trimmed:** Removed "Thanking/Responding" sub-section to reduce cognitive load — kept the 4 most exam-relevant categories
