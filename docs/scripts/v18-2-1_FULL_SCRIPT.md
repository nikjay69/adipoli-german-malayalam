## Section 2: The Exam Format (1:00–2:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Annotated real exam prompt example]
```
Situation: Ihr Freund Marco hat morgen Geburtstag.
Schreiben Sie eine Nachricht an Marco.

- Glückwunsch zum Geburtstag
- Geschenk: Was bringen Sie mit?
- Wann kommen Sie zur Party?
```

### Narration:
"Here's what you'll see on exam day. Three parts:

**One:** A situation — who are you writing to and why?

**Two:** The person's name — you'll use this in your greeting.

**Three:** Exactly 3 content points — these are your THREE sentences.

[EXAM-TIP] You MUST hit all 3 content points. Miss even one and you lose significant marks. The content points are your checklist — tick them off as you write."

## Section 3: The Golden Template (2:00–3:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Template skeleton with colour-coded parts]
```
Liebe/Lieber [Name],     ← GREETING (matches gender)

[Sentence 1 — Point 1]   ← HIT content point 1
[Sentence 2 — Point 2]   ← HIT content point 2
[Sentence 3 — Point 3]   ← HIT content point 3

Viele Grüße              ← CLOSING
[Dein Name]              ← YOUR name
```

### Narration:
"This is your golden template. Memorize it. Use it every time.

**Greeting:** Start with 'Lieber' for a man, 'Liebe' for a woman, followed by their name. If it's informal (friend, family), this is perfect. If it's formal — we'll cover that too.

**Body:** One sentence per content point. Three points, three sentences. Don't write an essay — short and correct beats long and broken.

**Closing:** 'Viele Grüße' works for almost everything informal. Then YOUR name.

[A1-SAFE] This template works for 95% of Schreiben Teil 2 prompts. Don't reinvent the wheel — just fill in the blanks.

*Template ready. Ellam ithil fit aakum. Namukku nokaam!*"

**Informal (friends, family):**

|----------|---------|
| Liebe Maria, / Lieber Tom, | Viele Grüße |
| Hallo Maria, | Liebe Grüße / LG |

**Formal (teacher, office, doctor):**

| Greeting | Closing |
|----------|---------|
| Sehr geehrte Frau Schmidt, | Mit freundlichen Grüßen |
| Sehr geehrter Herr Müller, | MfG (abbreviation) |

[EXAM-TIP] 90% of exam prompts are informal — friend, classmate, colleague. Use 'Liebe/Lieber + Name' and 'Viele Grüße' as your default.

⏸️ Say it: 'Liebe Maria, ... Viele Grüße, [your name].'

### Visual: [SLIDE: Exam prompt on left, model answer building on right]
### Narration:
"Let's build our first message together.

**Prompt:**
```
Ihr Freund Marco hat morgen Geburtstag.
- Glückwunsch
- Geschenk: was bringen Sie mit?
- Wann kommen Sie?
```

Watch me build this using the template:

**Greeting:** Marco is a man → 'Lieber Marco,'

**Point 1 — Glückwunsch:** 'Herzlichen Glückwunsch zum Geburtstag!'

**Point 2 — Geschenk:** 'Ich bringe eine Flasche Wein mit.'

**Point 3 — Wann:** 'Ich komme um 19 Uhr.'

**Closing:** 'Viele Grüße, [Name]'

[SLIDE: Complete message]
```
Lieber Marco,

herzlichen Glückwunsch zum Geburtstag!
Ich bringe eine Flasche Wein mit.
Ich komme um 19 Uhr.

Viele Grüße
Lakshmi
```

Count the words: about 20. That's fine! You don't need exactly 30 — anywhere from 20 to 40 is good. And we hit ALL 3 points. ✅✅✅

*Simple alle? Oru point — oru sentence. Template use cheythu. Done!*"

## Section 9: Common Mistakes (8:45–9:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: 5 mistakes with ❌ and corrections ✅]
### Narration:
"Five mistakes that cost students marks every year:

**Mistake 1:** ❌ Forgetting the greeting or closing.
Always start with 'Liebe/Lieber [Name],' and end with 'Viele Grüße, [your name].'

**Mistake 2:** ❌ Not hitting all 3 content points.
The prompt gives you 3 points — those are your CHECKLIST. After writing, count: did I cover point 1? Point 2? Point 3?

**Mistake 3:** ❌ Writing too much.
30 words is enough. Writing 100 words wastes time AND introduces more errors. *Simple and correct > fancy and broken.*

**Mistake 4:** ❌ Wrong register.
If the prompt says 'Ihr Freund' (your friend) → informal. 'Ihr Lehrer' (your teacher) → could be either. 'Eine Firma' (a company) → formal. Match your greeting to the relationship!

**Mistake 5:** ❌ Using words you're not sure about.
[EXAM-TIP] If you're not 100% sure about a word, use a simpler one. 'Ich komme am Samstag' is BETTER than a fancy sentence with 3 errors."
