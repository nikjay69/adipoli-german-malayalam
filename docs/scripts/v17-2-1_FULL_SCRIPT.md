## Section 2: Teil 1 — Short Messages (0:50–3:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Hören Teil 1 — 6 short recordings, 1 MC question each"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: teaching, methodical]
"Teil 1: 6 short recordings — voicemails, announcements, phone messages. One multiple-choice question per recording.

**Your 3-step strategy:**
1. **Read the question FIRST** — you get time before each audio
2. **Listen for keywords** — times, places, names, numbers
3. **Don't panic at unknown words** — focus on the answer, not full comprehension

### Teach: Keyword Categories

[SLIDE: 4 keyword types with icons]
Before you listen, identify which type the question asks for:
- ⏰ **Time:** um ___ Uhr, am Montag, morgen, heute
- 📍 **Place:** in der ___, am Bahnhof, bei ___
- 🔢 **Number:** Telefonnummer, Hausnummer, Preis
- 🎬 **Action:** kommen, anrufen, mitbringen, treffen

### Drill: Let's Try One

[SLIDE: 'Sie hören eine Nachricht auf dem Anrufbeantworter.']

Read the question first:
**'Wann ist der neue Termin?'**
A) Montag 14 Uhr
B) Dienstag 10 Uhr
C) Mittwoch 10 Uhr

What keyword type? ⏰ Time. Listen for a day and a time.

🔊 [Audio]:
'Hallo Frau Meier, hier ist die Praxis Dr. Schmidt. Ihr Termin am Montag um 14 Uhr muss leider verschoben werden. Können Sie bitte am Dienstag um 10 Uhr kommen? Bitte rufen Sie uns zurück. Danke!'

⏸️ What did you catch? Say your answer.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**B — Dienstag um 10 Uhr.** You heard 'Montag 14 Uhr' AND 'Dienstag 10 Uhr.' But the Montag appointment was *verschoben* (moved). The NEW one is Dienstag.

### Apply: Why This Worked

You identified the keyword type (time), listened for day + Uhr, and caught the change. That's the whole strategy."

## Section 3: Teil 2 — Dialogue Traps (3:00–5:15)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Hören Teil 2 — 4 short dialogues, richtig/falsch or MC"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: alert, slightly faster]
"Teil 2: 4 short everyday dialogues — at a shop, on the phone, with a colleague.

### Teach: The Correction Trap

The #1 trap in Teil 2: information gets CORRECTED during the dialogue. The first thing you hear is NOT the answer — the final agreement is.

*Ivide aanu trap — aadhyam parayunnathu answer alla, final agreement aanu answer!*

### Drill: Catch the Change

🔊 [Audio]:
Person A: 'Treffen wir uns um 15 Uhr im Café?'
Person B: '15 Uhr ist schlecht. Können wir um 16 Uhr?'
Person A: 'Ja, 16 Uhr passt. Bis dann!'

**'Wann treffen sich die Personen?'**
A) 15 Uhr
B) 16 Uhr
C) 17 Uhr

⏸️ What's the answer?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**B — 16 Uhr.** First suggestion was 15, but it changed. Always track the FINAL decision.

### Drill 2: Harder One

🔊 [Audio]:
Person A: 'Ich hole dich am Hauptbahnhof ab.'
Person B: 'Nein, komm lieber zum Alexanderplatz. Das ist einfacher.'
Person A: 'Okay, Alexanderplatz. Um halb drei?'
Person B: 'Ja, perfekt.'

**'Wo treffen sich die Personen?'**
A) Hauptbahnhof
B) Alexanderplatz
C) Zu Hause

⏸️ Answer?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**B — Alexanderplatz.** Same pattern: first suggestion (Hauptbahnhof) got replaced.

### Apply: The Rule

[EXAM-TIP] In Teil 2, listen for 'nein', 'lieber', 'doch', 'aber' — these signal a correction. The answer comes AFTER the correction word."

## Section 4: Teil 3 — The Two-Pass Method (5:15–7:15)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Hören Teil 3 — 1 longer recording, heard TWICE, 5 richtig/falsch"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: strategic, calm]
"Teil 3: One longer recording — a monologue or interview. 5 richtig/falsch questions. You hear it TWICE.

### Teach: Two-Pass Strategy

- **First listen:** Get the topic. Answer the easy ones. Mark unsure questions with '?'
- **Second listen:** Focus ONLY on your '?' questions. Ignore the ones you already answered.

*Randam praavisyam — full attention unanswered questions-il mathram!*

### Drill: Full Teil 3 Practice

Read all 5 questions first — 10 seconds.

[SLIDE: Questions appear]
1. Maria kommt aus Deutschland. → R / F
2. Maria ist Lehrerin. → R / F
3. Maria spricht drei Sprachen. → R / F
4. Maria kocht nicht gern. → R / F
5. Maria macht einen A1-Kurs. → R / F

⏸️ Read them. Notice: questions 1 and 5 are about FACTS. Questions 3 and 4 have TRAPS (number, negation).

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (5 seconds)

🔊 [Audio — first listen]:
'Hallo, ich bin Maria. Ich komme aus Spanien und wohne seit zwei Jahren in Berlin. Ich arbeite als Lehrerin in einer Grundschule. Ich spreche Spanisch, Deutsch und ein bisschen Französisch. Am Wochenende koche ich gern. Ich mache oft spanisches Essen für meine deutschen Freunde. Nächste Woche fange ich einen Deutschkurs an — B1!'

⏸️ Mark your answers. Which ones are you unsure about?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (5 seconds)

Now listen again — focus on your uncertain ones.

🔊 [Audio — second listen, same text]

⏸️ Final answers?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

### Apply: Answers

[SLIDE: Answers appear one by one]
1. Maria kommt aus Deutschland → **Falsch** (Spanien)
2. Maria ist Lehrerin → **Richtig** ✅
3. Maria spricht drei Sprachen → **Richtig** (Spanisch, Deutsch, Französisch = 3) ✅
4. Maria kocht nicht gern → **Falsch** ('koche ich gern' — the 'nicht' in the question is the trap!)
5. Maria macht einen A1-Kurs → **Falsch** (B1, not A1!)

How many did you get? 4 or 5 = excellent. Under 3 = practice the two-pass method more."

## Section 5: Malayali Listening Traps (7:15–8:15)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "5 Traps for Malayali Ears" with audio examples]
### Narration: [ENERGY: specific, helpful]
"Five traps Malayali students hit most often:

**Trap 1: Similar numbers**
🔊 dreizehn (13)... dreißig (30)...
⏸️ Hear the difference? '-zehn' = teens, '-zig' = tens.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**Trap 2: halb + Uhr**
halb drei = **2:30**, NOT 3:30. It means 'half TO three.'
*Malayalam-il 'moonnu mani' ennaal 3:00. German-il 'halb drei' = 2:30. Completely different logic!*

**Trap 3: Negation flip**
'nicht am Montag, **sondern** am Dienstag' — the real answer comes AFTER 'sondern.'

**Trap 4: Speed panic**
*Speed kandu pedikkanda!* Exam audio is slower than real German. Trust the recording.

**Trap 5: Similar sounds**
Kirche (church) vs Kirsche (cherry) — one consonant changes everything. Context helps: are they talking about Sunday or cooking?

⏸️ Quick — what does 'halb vier' mean? Say the time.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

3:30. Half TO four."

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (4 seconds)

[Steps appear]
1. Read the question first
2. Identify the keyword type (time/place/number/action)
3. Listen for that keyword — ignore the rest

⏸️ Teil 2 — what's the #1 trap?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

Information gets CORRECTED. Listen for the FINAL answer.

⏸️ Teil 3 — what's the two-pass method?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

First listen: easy answers + mark uncertain. Second listen: focus only on uncertain ones.

### Your Daily Practice Plan (15 minutes)

[SLIDE: 5-step practice framework]
1. Listen to one exercise — audio only, no transcript
2. Answer the question
3. Listen again WITH transcript visible
4. Circle the keywords you missed
5. Add missed words to your vocabulary review

[EXAM-TIP] Keep your transcript HIDDEN until after you answer. Looking while listening is training a different skill."
