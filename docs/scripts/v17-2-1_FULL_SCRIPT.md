## Section 2: Exam Map — 3 Parts, 3 Listening Jobs (0:45–1:25)

### Visual: [TABLE: Hören overview]
| Teil | What you hear | Items | Your main job |
|------|---------------|-------|---------------|
| 1 | Short messages / announcements | 6 | Catch one key fact |
| 2 | Short dialogues | 4 | Catch the final decision |
| 3 | Longer announcement / monologue | 5 | Track several details without panicking |

### Narration: [ENERGY: structured]
"Three parts. Three different listening jobs.

**Teil 1:** catch one key fact.
**Teil 2:** catch the final answer after changes or corrections.
**Teil 3:** stay calm and track several details across a longer audio.

[EXAM-TIP] Before every audio, read the question first. If you listen without a target, everything sounds fast. If you know what you're hunting for, the audio becomes much easier."

## Section 3: Teil 1 — Short Messages (1:25–3:10)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Teil 1 — Read first. Hunt one fact."]
### Narration: [ENERGY: teaching, precise]
"Teil 1 gives you six short recordings: voicemail, station announcement, office message, shop info.

The winning method is simple:

1. Read the question first
2. Decide the keyword type
3. Listen only for that type

### Visual: [4 icons appear]
- ⏰ **Time**
- 📍 **Place**
- 🔢 **Number / price**
- 👤 **Name / person**

Let's do one.

### Visual: [SLIDE: Question]
**Wann ist der neue Termin?**
A) Montag 14 Uhr  
B) Dienstag 10 Uhr  
C) Mittwoch 10 Uhr

Before the audio even starts, you already know: I need **day + time**.

🔊 **Audio:**
'Hallo Frau Meier, hier ist die Praxis Dr. Schmidt. Ihr Termin am Montag um 14 Uhr muss leider verschoben werden. Können Sie bitte am Dienstag um 10 Uhr kommen? Danke.'

⏸️ **Your answer?** (3s)

✅ **B — Dienstag um 10 Uhr.**

Why do students miss this? Because they hear the first time expression and stop thinking.

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: "verschoben" → arrow to new time]
The real answer comes after the change.

*First number kettal mathi ennu vichaarikkaruthu. Context complete aayal mathram tick cheyyu.*"

## Section 4: Teil 1 Mini-Drill — Numbers, Places, Negation (3:10–3:55)

### Visual: [3 rapid cards]
### Narration: [ENERGY: fast, game-like]
"Quick drill. Tell me what kind of answer to expect.

**Question 1:** 'Wo treffen sich die Teilnehmer?'  
⏸️ What keyword type? (2s)

✅ **Place.**

**Question 2:** 'Wie viel kostet das Ticket?'  
⏸️ (2s)

✅ **Number / price.**

**Question 3:** 'Kommt Frau Keller am Freitag?'  
⏸️ (2s)

✅ Watch for **ja / nein / nicht / doch**.

This is the whole Teil-1 mindset: don't listen widely — listen narrowly."

## Section 5: Teil 2 — Dialogues and the Final Decision Trap (3:55–5:50)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Teil 2 — First idea is often NOT the answer"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: alert, practical]
"Teil 2 gives you four short dialogues. Here the biggest trap is **correction**.

The first idea appears.
Then someone changes it.
The changed version is the answer.

Listen to this:

🔊 **Audio:**
A: 'Treffen wir uns um 15 Uhr im Café?'
B: '15 Uhr ist schlecht. Können wir um 16 Uhr?'
A: 'Ja, 16 Uhr passt.'

**Wann treffen sich die Personen?**
A) 15 Uhr  
B) 16 Uhr  
C) 17 Uhr

⏸️ **Answer?** (2s)

✅ **B — 16 Uhr.**

### Visual: [SLIDE: correction words]
Watch for words like:
- **aber**
- **nein**
- **lieber**
- **doch**
- **nicht ... sondern ...**

They often signal: the answer is changing.

Now one more.

🔊 **Audio:**
A: 'Ich hole dich am Hauptbahnhof ab.'
B: 'Nein, komm lieber zum Alexanderplatz.'
A: 'Okay, Alexanderplatz. Um halb drei?'
B: 'Ja, perfekt.'

**Wo treffen sich die Personen?**
A) Hauptbahnhof  
B) Alexanderplatz  
C) zu Hause

⏸️ **Answer?** (2s)

✅ **B — Alexanderplatz.**

And one more trap inside that dialogue: **halb drei = 2:30**, not 3:30.

*Ithu Malayali learners-inu classic trap aanu. 'Half three' English logic follow cheyyaruthu. German-il half TO the next hour aanu.*"

## Section 6: Teil 3 — Longer Audio, Two-Pass Method (5:50–7:45)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Teil 3 — First listen = map. Second listen = confirm."]
### Narration: [ENERGY: calm, strategic]
"Teil 3 is longer. That scares students. But the strategy is very clear.

### First listen:
- understand the topic
- answer the easy items
- mark unsure ones with a question mark

### Second listen:
- focus only on the unsure items
- confirm or correct

Let's practice.

### Visual: [Questions appear first]
1. Maria kommt aus Deutschland.  
2. Maria ist Lehrerin.  
3. Maria spricht drei Sprachen.  
4. Maria kocht nicht gern.  
5. Maria macht einen B1-Kurs.

⏸️ **Read the questions now. Which answers need country, job, number, negation, level?** (4s)

Good. Now listen.

🔊 **Audio:**
'Hallo, ich bin Maria. Ich komme aus Spanien und wohne seit zwei Jahren in Berlin. Ich arbeite als Lehrerin in einer Grundschule. Ich spreche Spanisch, Deutsch und ein bisschen Französisch. Am Wochenende koche ich gern. Nächste Woche fange ich einen Deutschkurs an — B1.'

⏸️ **Mark your answers. Which ones are still uncertain?** (4s)

Now second listen — focus only on the shaky ones.

🔊 **Audio repeats**

⏸️ **Final answers?** (3s)

### Visual: [Answer reveal]
1. Deutschland → **Falsch**  
2. Lehrerin → **Richtig**  
3. drei Sprachen → **Richtig**  
4. kocht nicht gern → **Falsch**  
5. B1-Kurs → **Richtig**

Notice how the traps work:
- wrong country
- negation added in the question
- exact number of languages

Teil 3 rewards calm checking — not heroic guessing."

## Section 7: 4 High-Value A1 Listening Traps (7:45–8:40)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "4 traps to catch before they catch you"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: sharp, memorable]
"These four traps appear again and again:

**Trap 1: number confusion**  
*dreißig* vs *dreizehn*

**Trap 2: time trap**  
*halb vier* = 3:30

**Trap 3: correction trap**  
'not Monday — Tuesday'

**Trap 4: negation trap**  
*nicht, kein, leider nicht, doch*

⏸️ **Quick one: 'nicht am Freitag, sondern am Samstag' — what's the answer day?** (2s)

✅ **Samstag.**

[EXAM-TIP] When you hear a correction word, don't lock your answer yet. Wait for the final information."
