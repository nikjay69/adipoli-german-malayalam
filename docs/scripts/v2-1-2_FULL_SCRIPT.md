## Section 2: The 3 Key Questions You Must Recognize (0:50–2:00)

### Visual: [Three question cards appear one by one. Keywords highlighted: `schreibt`, `buchstabieren`, `bitte`]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, coaching]
"There are three questions you must understand immediately.

[SLIDE: Question 1]
🔊 **Wie schreibt man das?**
How do you write that?

[SLIDE: Question 2]
🔊 **Können Sie das buchstabieren?**

[SLIDE: Question 3]
🔊 **Bitte buchstabieren Sie Ihren Namen.**
Please spell your name.

Same job. Same skill. Don't freeze just because the wording changes.

*Ithu nammade call-center experience pole aanu.* In English also, people ask: "Can you spell that?" Same survival skill. Different alphabet.

Now your response pattern:

🔊 **Ja, gern. N-I-K-H-I-L.**
Yes, gladly. N-I-K-H-I-L.

Short. Calm. Clear.

⏸️ Repeat after me: **Ja, gern.**

## Section 3: How to Spell Naturally — Slow Beats Fast (2:00–3:30)

### Visual: [Animated name on screen: NIKHIL. Letters appear one at a time with slight pause markers between them. Then a second version appears too fast with a red X.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: practical, reassuring]
"When you spell in German, your goal is **clarity**, not speed.

Wrong mindset:
[SLIDE: `NIKHIL` rushed together ❌]
'Mmnikhiljayanth...'

Correct mindset:
[SLIDE: `N - I - K - H - I - L` ✅]
**N - I - K - H - I - L.**

Tiny pauses between letters. Not dramatic. Just clean.

If they don't catch it, don't get embarrassed. Use one of these rescue lines:

🔊 **Langsam?** — Slowly?
🔊 **Noch einmal?** — Once more?
🔊 **Ich buchstabiere noch einmal.** — I'll spell it once again.

*Malayali perukal konjam long aayirikkum. Athu oru problem alla.* Your job is not to have a "German-friendly" name. Your job is to stay patient and spell it clearly.

⏸️ Spell this with me: **A-N-N-A**

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

⏸️ Now: **V-I-S-H-N-U**

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)"

### Name 4: Sreelakshmi
🔊 **S-R-E-E-L-A-K-S-H-M-I**
Yes — long name. Yes — still possible.

The trick is chunking.
[SLIDE: `S-R-E-E` | `L-A-K-S-H-M-I`]
Break a long name into two smaller rhythm blocks.

You can even say:
🔊 **Langsam, bitte. Ich buchstabiere in zwei Teilen.**
Slowly, please. I'll spell it in two parts.

That is not weakness. That is professionalism.

[EXAM-TIP] In Goethe A1 Sprechen Teil 1, if they ask you to spell your name, a slow accurate answer is much better than a fast messy one.

## Section 5: Vorname, Nachname, Familienname — Form Survival (5:15–6:45)

### Visual: [German registration form zoom-in. Fields highlighted one by one: `Vorname`, `Nachname/Familienname`, `Geburtsname`]
### Narration: [ENERGY: structured, simplifying]
"Now the form part. Germany loves forms. So you must know these labels.

[SLIDE: Field map]
- **Vorname** = first name
- **Nachname** / **Familienname** = last name / surname
- **Geburtsname** = birth name, usually relevant after marriage

Let's do one example.

If the name is **Nikhil Jayanth**:
- **Vorname:** Nikhil
- **Nachname:** Jayanth

Simple.

But for many Malayalis, naming conventions can be more complicated — personal name, father's name, house name, initials.

German forms usually want just this:
**First name** + **family name**

*Overthinking venda.* Don't try to explain your whole family history inside one small box.

If you are unsure, match your passport spelling exactly. That is the safest rule.

⏸️ Quick check: `Vorname` means what?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**First name.** ✅

⏸️ `Familienname` means what?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

**Last name / surname.** ✅"

## Section 6: Live Form Fill — Name + Date Without Confusion (6:45–8:15)

### Visual: [Simple form appears. Fields: Vorname, Nachname, Geburtsdatum, Adresse. A sample learner profile card sits beside it.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: guided, steady]
"Let's fill a mini form together.

[FORM CARD]
Name: Anjali Nair
Date of birth: 14 August 1998
Address: Kochi

Now we fill:

- **Vorname:** Anjali
- **Nachname:** Nair
- **Geburtsdatum:** **14.08.1998**
- **Adresse / Ort:** Kochi

Notice the date format again:
🔊 **Tag.Monat.Jahr**
Day. Month. Year.

Not American month-day-year.

*Ithu especially important aanu.* One small date mistake can create real confusion on German forms.

Also remember: write only what the field asks for.
If the field says **Ort**, write the city.
If it says **Adresse**, write the full address.

[SLIDE: Golden Rule badge]
**Read the label twice. Then write.**

⏸️ If the form asks `Vorname`, do you write your family name?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

No. Only your first name."
