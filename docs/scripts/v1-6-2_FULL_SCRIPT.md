## Section 2: Scenario 1 — Formal Email (A Professional Message) (1:00–3:30)

### Visual: [SCENE: A professional email window on a computer.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: methodical, clear]
"You need to write to your professor, a company, or an office (like the Bürgeramt).

[SLIDE: The Formal Email Structure]
**1. Salutation (Opening)**
**2. The Message**
**3. Closing**
**4. Your Name**

### The Opening (Salutation)
If you know the name:
- **Sehr geehrte Frau Müller,** (Dear Mrs. Müller,)
- **Sehr geehrter Herr Schmidt,** (Dear Mr. Schmidt,)

If you don't know the name:
- **Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren,** (Dear Ladies and Gentlemen,)

### The Closing
- **Mit freundlichen Grüßen** (With kind regards)

*Note: After the comma in the opening, the next word starts with a LOWERCASE letter! (unless it's a noun).*

[EXAM-TIP]
This is a common trap. **'Sehr geehrte Frau Müller, / ich möchte ...'** — the 'i' must be lowercase. Examiners watch this like a hawk! 🦅"

## Section 3: Scenario 2 — Informal Message (A Friend or Family) (3:30–5:15)

### Visual: [SCENE: A WhatsApp-style message screen.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: friendly, light]
"Now, you're texting a friend. It's much simpler.

[SLIDE: The Informal Message Structure]
### The Opening
- **Liebe Anna,** (Dear Anna - for women)
- **Lieber Tom,** (Dear Tom - for men)
- **Hallo [Name],** (Works for everyone!)

### The Closing
- **Liebe Grüße** (Best wishes)
- **Viele Grüße** (Many wishes)
- Or simply: **Bis bald!** (See you soon!)

*WhatsApp-il it's even easier. 'Hey' or 'Hi' is enough. But for the exam, use 'Liebe/Lieber' — it shows you know the rules.*

⏸️ **Try writing in your head:** Opening for a friend named Manu. ⏸️ 🔊 **'Lieber Manu,'**"

## Section 4: Special Keyboard Rules — ä, ö, ü, ß (5:15–6:45)

### Visual: [SCENE: An English keyboard. Red circles over where the German letters should be.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: practical, technical]
"What if your keyboard doesn't have the German special letters?
Don't panic! There is a formal 'backup' system that Germans and examiners always accept.

[SLIDE: The Backup Rule]
- **ä** → **ae**
- **ö** → **oe**
- **ü** → **ue**
- **ß** → **ss**

Example: **München** can be written as **Muenchen**.
Example: **Straße** can be written as **Strasse**.

*Nammude mobile-il press and hold cheyyaam. Pakshe computer-il typed letters-inu alternative use cheyyaam.*

⏸️ **Try this:** How do you write 'Büro' (office) without a 'ü'? ⏸️ 🔊 **'Buero'**."

## Section 5: The "Schreiben Teil 2" Exam Plan (6:45–8:15)

### Visual: [SCENE: A sample exam task: Write to your teacher (Frau Meyer). You're sick and can't come to class.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: strategic, coaching]
"Let's build a real exam answer.

**Task:** Write to Frau Meyer. Say:
1. You are sick.
2. You cannot come to class.
3. Bis morgen (See you tomorrow).

**Answer:**
**Sehr geehrte Frau Meyer,** (Opening - Formal)
**ich bin krank.** (Point 1)
**Ich komme nicht zum Kurs.** (Point 2)
**Bis morgen!** (Point 3)
**Mit freundlichen Grüßen,** (Closing)
**[Your Name]** (Sign off)

Look at that. Only 15 words. But it's a PERFECT 5-point answer. 

*German long aakanam ennu parayaruthu. Exam-il short and correct sentences aanu mark tharunnathennu orthaal mathi.*"
