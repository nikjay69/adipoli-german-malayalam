## Section 2: The 6 Hobby Verbs You Need First (0:45–2:05)

### 🎬 [Energy: focused, building — "6 verbs, that's your starter kit"]
### Visual: [6 clean verb cards only]
### Narration:
"Start small. These six hobbies are enough to build real answers.

🔊 **lesen** — to read  
🔊 **kochen** — to cook  
🔊 **Musik hören** — to listen to music  
🔊 **Fußball spielen** — to play football  
🔊 **schwimmen** — to swim  
🔊 **spazieren gehen** — to go for a walk

Now the magic word:
🔊 **gern**

`gern` means you like doing something.

- **Ich lese gern.**
- **Ich koche gern.**
- **Ich höre gern Musik.**
- **Ich spiele gern Fußball.**

### Mini rule
Verb first, **gern** after the verb.
Not: **Ich gern koche** ❌
But: **Ich koche gern** ✅

*Malayalam-il "enikku cooking ishtam aanu" pole aanu idea. German-il simple pattern: **Ich + verb + gern**.*

### Drill
Repeat after me:
- Ich lese gern.
- Ich koche gern.
- Ich höre gern Musik.
- Ich spiele gern Fußball.

⏸️ [pause]"

## Section 3: Say More Than "My Hobby Is..." (2:05–3:25)

### 🎬 [Energy: leveling up — "three patterns from basic to impressive"]
### Visual: [Three sentence frames only]
### Narration:
"You need three answer patterns.

### Pattern 1 — easiest
🔊 **Mein Hobby ist Lesen.**

Good, but a little stiff.

### Pattern 2 — more natural
🔊 **Ich lese gern.**
🔊 **Ich koche gern.**

This is the best everyday pattern.

### Pattern 3 — stronger personal answer
🔊 **In meiner Freizeit lese ich gern.**
🔊 **In meiner Freizeit höre ich gern Musik.**

That phrase — **In meiner Freizeit** — makes you sound more complete without becoming complicated.

### Drill
I say the hobby. You say the sentence.
- lesen → ?
⏸️ [pause]
- kochen → ?
⏸️ [pause]
- spazieren gehen → ?
⏸️ [pause]

Possible answers:
- **Ich lese gern.**
- **Ich koche gern.**
- **Ich gehe gern spazieren.**"

## Section 4: Add Frequency, Not a Long Speech (3:25–4:35)

### 🎬 [Energy: practical — "one word makes your answer sound real"]
### Visual: [4 frequency chips]
### Narration:
"To sound more real, add one time phrase.

🔊 **oft** — often  
🔊 **manchmal** — sometimes  
🔊 **am Wochenende** — on weekends  
🔊 **jeden Tag** — every day

Now build short personal lines:
- **Ich lese oft.**
- **Ich koche am Wochenende gern.**
- **Ich höre jeden Tag Musik.**
- **Manchmal spiele ich Fußball.**

Important: at A1, short is better than fancy.
You do **not** need a six-sentence performance every time someone asks about hobbies.
You need **2 or 3 clear sentences**.

### Micro-output
Say two true sentences about yourself now:
1. one hobby
2. one frequency line

⏸️ [pause]"

## Section 5: Ask About Hobbies — Exam and Real-Life Transfer (4:35–5:55)

### 🎬 [Energy: interactive — "don't just answer, ask back"]
### Visual: [Formal / informal split]
### Narration:
"Now don't just answer. Learn to ask back.

### Informal
🔊 **Was machst du in deiner Freizeit?**  
🔊 **Was sind deine Hobbys?**

### Formal
🔊 **Was machen Sie in Ihrer Freizeit?**  
🔊 **Was sind Ihre Hobbys?**

### One strong follow-up
🔊 **Wie oft machen Sie das?** — How often do you do that?

This matters for two reasons:
1. real conversation should continue
2. in speaking tasks, you may need to ask a question, not only answer

### Drill
Choose the correct version.
You speak to a friend → ?
⏸️ [pause]
**Was machst du in deiner Freizeit?**

You speak in the exam / formal setting → ?
⏸️ [pause]
**Was machen Sie in Ihrer Freizeit?**

*Conversation nirtharuthu. Answer kazhinjal ask back cheyyanam.*

[EXAM-TIP] In Sprechen Teil 2, you may need to ASK your partner about hobbies, not just answer. Practice the question forms, not only answers."

## Section 7: Exam-Safe Speaking Model — 3 Sentences, Not 10 (7:25–8:35)

### 🎬 [Energy: exam coach — "this formula is your safety net"]
### Visual: [Card: Hobby answer formula]
### Narration:
"If the speaking exam touches hobbies, this is a very safe answer formula:

1. **In meiner Freizeit ...**
2. **Ich ... gern.**
3. **Am Wochenende / Oft / Manchmal ...**

### Example
**In meiner Freizeit lese ich gern. Ich höre auch gern Musik. Am Wochenende koche ich oft mit meiner Familie.**

That is enough.

### Important speaking truth check
Many learners think: more sentences = more points.
Not necessarily.
At A1, a short, correct, understandable answer is stronger than a long risky answer.

⏸️ **PAUSE TASK:** Build your own 3-sentence answer now.

[EXAM-TIP] In Sprechen Teil 1, hobby questions are common. A 3-sentence answer (hobby + detail + frequency) is safer and stronger than a memorized 8-sentence speech."

## Section 8: Preference Upgrade — `nicht gern`, `lieber`, `am liebsten` (8:35–9:35)

### 🎬 [Energy: leveling up — "three words unlock opinions"]
### Visual: [Simple preference ladder]
### Narration:
"One small upgrade block.

🔊 **Ich schwimme nicht gern.** — I don't like swimming.  
🔊 **Ich koche lieber.** — I prefer cooking.  
🔊 **Am liebsten lese ich.** — I like reading best.

These three forms are enough for beginner-level preference.

### Mini contrast
- **Ich spiele gern Fußball, aber ich schwimme nicht gern.**
- **Ich höre gern Musik, aber am liebsten koche ich.**

Do not turn this into a grammar lecture.
Just memorize the useful shape:
- **nicht gern**
- **lieber**
- **am liebsten**

### Drill
Complete it:
**Ich höre gern Musik, aber am liebsten ...**
⏸️ [pause]"

## Section 9: Fast Culture Bridge — What Counts as a Hobby? (9:35–10:10)

### 🎬 [Energy: cultural insight — "quick bridge, not a lecture"]
### Visual: [Kerala / Germany lifestyle icons]
### Narration:
"A small culture note.

In Germany, hobbies are part of identity.
People may ask about them very early.

That can include:
- sport
- music
- cooking
- hiking
- reading
- club activities

So when someone asks about hobbies, it is not a trick question. It is normal social connection.
