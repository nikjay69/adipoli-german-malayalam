## Section 3: The 'zehn' Pattern — 13 to 19 (2:15–4:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: Unit + zehn = Teen. Color-coded: [drei] + [zehn] = dreizehn.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"For 13 to 19, the rule is simple: **Unit + zehn**.

[Rows appear]
- **dreizehn** (13) — drei + zehn
- **vierzehn** (14) — vier + zehn
- **fünfzehn** (15) — fünf + zehn

*Stop! Did you hear that? It's literally 'three-ten', 'four-ten', 'five-ten'. Malayalam-il 13 ennu parayumpol 'pathu-moonnu' ennu parayilla, pakshe German-il 'moonnu-pathu' style aanu!*

## Section 4: The 16 and 17 Trap — The 'Shortcut' Rule (4:30–6:00)

### Visual: [WARNING ICON: "The Shortcuts!". 16 — sechszehn (crossed out) → sechzehn. 17 — siebenzehn (crossed out) → siebzehn.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: attentive, warning]
"Here come the 'shortcuts'. For 16 and 17, German drops a few letters to make it easier to say.

[Logic cards]
- **sechs** (6) + **zehn** (10) → **sechzehn** (16). *The 's' is gone!*
- **sieben** (7) + **zehn** (10) → **siebzehn** (17). *The 'en' is gone!*

🔊 **Listen:** sechzehn... siebzehn.
Don't say 'sechSSSzehn'. It's too much work!

⏸️ **Say 16 after me: sechzehn.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**sechzehn.** ✅

⏸️ **Say 17: siebzehn.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**siebzehn.** ✅"

## Section 6: Malayalam Logic — Reversing the Order (7:30–8:45)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Malayalam 13 (പതിമൂന്ന്) vs German 13 (dreizehn). Units highlighted.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, structural]
"In Malayalam, we say **പതി (ten) + മൂന്ന് (three) = പതിമൂന്ന്**.
In German, they flip it: **drei (three) + zehn (ten) = dreizehn**.

It's literally the mirror image of how we think! For the teens, just remember: **Small digit FIRST, big digit (10) SECOND.**

[EXAM-TIP] Numbers 11-20 are most frequent in Hören Teil 1 (Track/Platform numbers or flight gates). Listen for the 'zehn' at the end — if you hear it, you know it's a teen number!"
