## Section 2: What Makes a Verb Reflexive? (0:35–1:45)

🎬 [Energy: Clear, conceptual — "aha moment" pacing]

**NARRATOR:**
"A reflexive verb is when the subject does something TO ITSELF.

Normal: 'Ich wasche **das Auto**.' — I wash the car. The car is the object.
Reflexive: 'Ich wasche **mich**.' — I wash myself. I am BOTH subject AND object.

The action bounces back — like a mirror.

Think of it as an arrow. Normal verb: the arrow goes from YOU to something else. Reflexive: the arrow comes right back to YOU."

[VISUAL: Diagram — stick figure with arrow pointing to a car (normal) vs. stick figure with curved arrow pointing back to itself (reflexive). Label: "sich = the mirror word"]

**NARRATOR:**
"Malayalam-il ee concept und! 'Ñān enne tanne kazhukkunnu.' 'Enne tanne' = myself. German-il 'mich' = enne tanne. Same idea — German just puts it in one small word."

⏸️ [PAUSE — 3 seconds] "So: normal verb = action goes to something else. Reflexive = action comes back to you. Clear? Good."

## Section 7: The Dativ Reflexive Twist (8:00–9:00)

🎬 [Energy: Careful, precise — "this is tricky but manageable"]

**NARRATOR:**
"One more level. Sometimes the reflexive pronoun is Dativ, not Akkusativ.

When does that happen? When there's ANOTHER Akkusativ object in the sentence.

Compare:
'Ich wasche **mich**.' — I wash myself. 'Mich' = Akkusativ. No other object.
'Ich wasche **mir** die Hände.' — I wash my hands. 'Die Hände' = Akkusativ object. So the reflexive drops to Dativ: **mir**.

The rule: if the sentence already has an Akkusativ object (like 'die Hände'), the reflexive pronoun becomes Dativ.

Good news: only 'ich' and 'du' actually change!

Akkusativ: mich, dich, sich, uns, euch, sich
Dativ: **mir**, **dir**, sich, uns, euch, sich

'sich', 'uns', and 'euch' are the SAME in both cases. Only 'mich→mir' and 'dich→dir' change."

[VISUAL: Side-by-side:
| | Akkusativ | Dativ |
| ich | mich | **mir** |
| du | dich | **dir** |
| er/sie/es | sich | sich |
| wir | uns | uns |
| ihr | euch | euch |
| sie/Sie | sich | sich |
Arrow highlighting: only mich↔mir and dich↔dir differ!]

⏸️ [PAUSE — 4 seconds] "Practice: 'Ich putze mir die Zähne.' — I brush my teeth. Say it: Ich putze mir die Zähne."

**NARRATOR:**
"For A1, don't stress about this. Just remember: 'Ich wasche MIR die Hände' and 'Ich putze MIR die Zähne' — these two fixed phrases cover 90% of Dativ reflexive at A1 level."

> **[EXAM-TIP]:** In Schreiben or Sprechen about your daily routine, "Ich putze mir die Zähne" (I brush my teeth) is a great sentence to include. It shows grammar awareness beyond basic A1.
