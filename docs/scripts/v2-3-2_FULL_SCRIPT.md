## Section 2: Why 'sein' Is Different (0:50–1:45)

### Visual: [TABLE: regular verb 'mach|en' vs 'sein'. mach|e, mach|st... vs bin, bist...]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, simple]
"Most German verbs follow a nice, neat pattern. You take the stem and add an ending.

But **sein**? It's a rebel. It has its own unique forms for every person.

I know, I know — why? Because it's OLD. The oldest, most used verbs in every language are always the weird ones. English is the same: 'I AM' but 'You ARE'.

We don't learn the rule for 'sein'. We learn the SHAPES. Let's build them."

## Section 3: The 6 Shapes of 'sein' (1:45–4:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Pronoun | Conjugation | Meaning. Rows appearing one by one with rhythmic sound effects.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: rhythmic, teaching]
"Listen closely. These must become second nature.

[Row 1 appears]
🔊 **ich bin** — I am. (Ich bin Arun.)

[Row 2]
🔊 **du bist** — you are (casual). (Du bist mein Freund.)

[Row 3]
🔊 **er / sie / es ist** — he/she/it is. (Er ist Ingenieur. Sie ist Ärztin.)

[Row 4 — The plurals]
🔊 **wir sind** — we are. (Wir sind aus Kerala.)

[Row 5]
🔊 **ihr seid** — you all are. (Ihr seid Studenten.) — *Watch out: d sounds like t at the end!*

[Row 6]
🔊 **sie / Sie sind** — they / you (formal) are. (Sie sind sehr freundlich.)

⏸️ **Try: I am...?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**ich bin.** ✅

⏸️ **You are (casual)...?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**du bist.** ✅

⏸️ **He is...?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**er ist.** ✅"

## Section 6: Malayalam Parallel — 'aanu' vs 'sein' (7:30–8:45)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: Malayalam 'aanu' vs German conjugations.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: reassuring, cultural]
"In Malayalam, we have one power word: **ആണ് (aanu)**.
- Njan aanu. (I am)
- Nee aanu. (You are)
- Avan aanu. (He is)

Malayalam is easier here — the verb doesn't change!
German is more like English — the verb shifts for every person.

[EXAM-TIP] The #1 'sein' mistake? Saying 'Ich ist' (like 'I is'). In the speaking exam, this is a clear signal that your foundations are shaky. ALWAYS say 'Ich bin'. ALWAYS."
