## SECTION 3 — FUN SLANG (Block 1: 4 words)
🎬 [Energy: playful, a bit mischievous — these are the spicy ones]

**NARRATOR:**

Now the FUN stuff. These are words you'll hear at parties, among young people, on YouTube. But NOT in your exam!

Number one:

🇩🇪 **Geil!** — awesome, amazing, cool

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "Geil!"]**

⚠️ Quick warning: the original meaning of "geil" is... let's say "excited" in a way you wouldn't say to your grandmother. But TODAY, especially among younger people, it just means "awesome." "Das Essen ist geil!" = The food is amazing!

Adipoli ennu parayan pole — slang aanu, but everyone understands. 😄

Number two:

🇩🇪 **Krass!** — crazy, extreme, intense

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "Krass!"]**

Krass can be positive OR negative. "Krass, du hast den Job bekommen!" (Wow, you got the job!) or "Krass, der Preis!" (Crazy, that price!). Context decides.

Number three:

🇩🇪 **Alter!** — Dude! Bro!

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "Alter!"]**

This is literally "old man" but it means "dude." Just like Malayalam-il "machane!" — technically it means brother-in-law, but you say it to everyone. Same energy, same logic! 😄

Number four:

🇩🇪 **Na?** — Hey, what's up?

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "Na?"]**

The shortest greeting in German. Just "Na?" And the answer? Also "Na?" 😄

Two letters. One question. Complete greeting. Germans are efficient even with slang!

## SECTION 4 — EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (Block 2: 4 phrases)
🎬 [Energy: practical, conversational — these are daily-use survival phrases]

**NARRATOR:**

Now — 4 everyday expressions you'll hear in EVERY German conversation. Not really slang, but not in your textbook either:

🇩🇪 **Keine Ahnung** — no idea

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "Keine Ahnung"]**

You'll hear this 10 times a day. "Wann kommt der Bus?" — "Keine Ahnung." It's the most honest phrase in German. 😄

🇩🇪 **Kein Bock** — don't feel like it, can't be bothered

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "Kein Bock"]**

"Hast du Bock auf Kino?" = Do you feel like going to the cinema? "Nee, kein Bock." = Nah, don't feel like it.

Bock literally means a male goat. Don't ask why. German is weird sometimes. 🐐

## SECTION 4b — EVERYDAY EXPRESSIONS (Block 2 continued: 2 more + recall)
🎬 [Energy: still conversational, building on the flow]

**NARRATOR:**

Two more essential everyday phrases:

🇩🇪 **Bescheid sagen** — to let someone know, to inform

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "Bescheid sagen"]**

This one is actually almost formal! You'll hear it at work, from friends, everywhere. "Sag mir Bescheid!" = Let me know! One of the most useful German phrases period.

🇩🇪 **Lass mal** — let's do it! OR forget it

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "Lass mal"]**

Wait — it means BOTH "let's do it" and "forget it"? Yes! Context and tone decide. Welcome to German. 😄

"Lass mal ins Kino gehen!" = Let's go to the cinema! (enthusiastic)
"Lass mal." = Forget it. / Never mind. (flat tone)

## SECTION 5 — WHATSAPP GERMAN (Block 3: 4 abbreviations)
🎬 [Energy: modern, digital — like reading someone's phone screen]

**NARRATOR:**

German WhatsApp is its OWN language. Your German friend sends you a message and... you can't read it. Not because your German is bad — because they're using abbreviations!

The 4 you MUST recognize:

🇩🇪 **LG** = **L**iebe **G**rüße — kind regards (friendly sign-off)

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "LG — Liebe Grüße"]**

You already learned "Liebe Grüße" in email writing (Module 11, remember?). LG is just the WhatsApp shortcut.

🇩🇪 **MfG** = **M**it **f**reundlichen **G**rüßen — formal sign-off

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "MfG — Mit freundlichen Grüßen"]**

⚠️ **[CULTURE-TIP]:** If someone signs with MfG in WhatsApp, they're being deliberately formal or a bit cold. In a work email? Normal. In WhatsApp to a friend? Something is wrong. 😬

🇩🇪 **vllt** = **v**ie**ll**eich**t** — maybe

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "vllt — vielleicht"]**

🇩🇪 **hdl** = **h**ab **d**ich **l**ieb — love you (casual, among friends/family)

⏸️ **[PAUSE — say it: "hdl — hab dich lieb"]**

Not romantic "I love you" (that's "Ich liebe dich") — hdl is more like a warm friend/family sign-off.

Kerala-yil WhatsApp-il nammal "k" ennu type cheyyunnu "okay" ennu parayan. Germans "vllt" type cheyyunnu "vielleicht" ennu parayan. Same laziness, different language! 😄

## SECTION 6 — REGISTER AWARENESS: The Three Levels
🎬 [Energy: teacher-mode — this is actually important for real life]

**NARRATOR:**

Here's the MOST useful thing in this whole video. Not a single word — but a CONCEPT:

German has THREE registers. Three levels of formality. And using the WRONG one is a social mistake.

| Level | When | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Formal** | Boss, professor, Amt, strangers over 40 | "Sehr geehrte Frau Müller, könnten Sie mir bitte helfen?" |
| **Standard** | Colleagues, acquaintances, shops | "Hallo Frau Müller, können Sie mir helfen?" |
| **Slang** | Close friends, WhatsApp, parties | "Hey! Hilfst du mir kurz?" |

Malayalam-il SAME concept undu!

- **Formal:** "Ningal-kku sugham aano?" (to an elder/stranger)
- **Standard:** "Sukhalle?" (to someone you know)
- **Slang:** "Eda, enthu mwone?" (to your best friend)

Ningal/Nee/Eda = Sie/Du/Alter! Same system! 😄

⚠️ **[CULTURE-TIP]:** When in doubt → go formal. Athaan safe. Germans will TELL you "Du kannst 'du' sagen" — you can say 'du.' Wait for that invitation. Never assume.

## SECTION 7 — SAME MESSAGE, THREE REGISTERS
🎬 [Energy: practical demonstration — see the difference]

**NARRATOR:**

Let's see one real situation in all 3 registers. You want to invite someone to the cinema:

**WhatsApp to your friend:**
"Hey! Bock auf Kino heute? 🎬"

**Email to a colleague:**
"Hallo Anna, hast du heute Abend Lust, ins Kino zu gehen?"

**Email to your professor:**
"Sehr geehrter Herr Professor Schmidt, ich würde mich freuen, wenn wir gemeinsam ins Kino gehen könnten."

⏸️ **[PAUSE — look at all three. Same idea. COMPLETELY different language.]**

Same invitation. Three completely different messages. And a German would INSTANTLY know if you used the wrong register.

⚠️ **[CULTURE-TIP — EXAM WARNING]:** In your Goethe exam — Schreiben Teil 2 — you write a short message. The prompt tells you WHO you're writing to. That decides your register. Friend = informal. Teacher/Kursleitung = formal. Get this wrong and you lose points even if your German is perfect!

Your slang register? Leave it outside the exam room. Cool, Super, Toll — those are safe. But Geil, Alter, Kein Bock? Save them for the party AFTER you pass. 😄
