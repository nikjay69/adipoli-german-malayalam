## Section 2: The 'Super 5' — Most Common Personality Traits (0:50–4:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Adjective | Translation | Pronunciation. Icons for each trait.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: steady, instructional]
"These are the five words you'll use every single day. Master these first!

[Rows appear]
- 🔊 **nett** — *'net'. Just like English 'net', means nice or kind.*
- 🔊 **freundlich** — *'froynt-likh'. From 'Freund' (friend). Friendly!*
- 🔊 **lustig** — *'loos-tig'. Funny. Someone who makes you laugh.*
- 🔊 **intelligent** — *Exactly like English. Just pronounce every syllable: 'in-teh-lee-gent'.*
- 🔊 **fleißig** — *'fly-sig'. This is the big one! Hardworking and diligent.*

*Nett, lustig, fleißig... ivayellam use cheythu ninte friend-ne describe cheyyaan ready aayirikkum! 'Fleißig' ennu parayunnathu German-il oru vallya compliment aanu.*

⏸️ **Try: Funny?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**lustig.** ✅

⏸️ **Hardworking?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**fleißig.** ✅"

## Section 3: The 'Not So Nice' Traits — Being Honest (4:00–6:00)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: Negative traits in a separate box. Focus on using them carefully.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, cautionary]
"Sometimes, you have to be honest. But in German, we stay polite!

[List appears]

- **langweilig** (boring) — *'lang-vye-likh'. Literally 'long duration'.*
- **streng** (strict) — *'shtreng'. Like 'strong', but for rules!*

⏸️ **Say: My brother is lazy! (Mein Bruder ist faul.)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

⏸️ **Wait — is he really? No, we say it with love!**"

## Section 4: Sentences with 'und' and 'aber' (6:00–7:30)

### Visual: [FORMULA: Person + ist + [Trait 1] + [und/aber] + [Trait 2]. Color-coded logic cards.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, logical]
"Don't just say one word. Connect them!

[Connectors]
- **und** (and) — *For traits that go together.*
- **aber** (but) — *For contrast.*

[Examples]
🔊 **'Er ist intelligent und freundlich.'**
🔊 **'Meine Mutter ist nett, aber streng.'**
🔊 **'Er ist lustig, aber faul.'**

*'Und' and 'aber' use cheythu ninte sentence-ne level up cheyyaam!*

⏸️ **Try: She is nice AND funny.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Sie ist nett und lustig.** ✅"

## Section 5: Malayalam Logic — The Compliment Culture (7:30–8:45)

### Visual: [TABLE: German 'fleißig' vs Malayalam equivalent 'nalla paniyidukkunna aallu'.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, structural]
"In Kerala, we respect people who work hard. We have different local phrases for it.

In Germany, **'fleißig'** is the gold standard. Whether it's a student, an employee, or a child helping at home, calling them 'fleißig' is the best thing you can say.

*Malayalam-il nammal 'nalla paniyidukkunna aallu' ennu parayana pole, German-il 'fleißig' ennu parayaam!*

[EXAM-TIP] The exam will often give you a description like 'He doesn't like to work'. You have to choose the word **'faul'**. Or 'He makes everyone laugh' → choose **'lustig'**. Linking behavior to the personality word is key!"
