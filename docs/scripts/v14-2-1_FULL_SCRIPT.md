## Section 2: Arriving — 3 Sentences (0:45–2:15)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Bürgeramt entrance → Wartenummer machine → waiting area]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, step-by-step]
"You arrive at the office. Three things happen: check in, wait, get called.

### Sentence 1: Announcing your appointment
**'Guten Tag, ich habe einen Termin um zehn Uhr.'**
*(Hello, I have an appointment at 10 o'clock.)*

⏸️ Say it: 'Guten Tag, ich habe einen Termin um zehn Uhr.' — replace the time with YOUR appointment time.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (4 seconds)

### Sentence 2: If you DON'T have an appointment
**'Kann ich bitte einen Termin machen?'**
*(Can I please make an appointment?)*

### Sentence 3: Introducing yourself
**'Mein Name ist [your name].'**

Simple. Not 'Ich heiße' — at offices, 'Mein Name ist' sounds more formal and appropriate.

*Kerala-yil office-il chennal 'sir, oru help veNam' ennokke start cheyyum. Germany-yil formality aanu — but simple formality. 'Guten Tag' + your purpose. Athu mathram.*

⏸️ **Your turn — role-play arriving:** Say your greeting + appointment + name. All three, in order.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (6 seconds)

[SLIDE: 1. 'Guten Tag, ich habe einen Termin um ___ Uhr.' 2. 'Mein Name ist ___.']

### Key Vocabulary:
- **der Termin** — appointment (THE most important office word)
- **die Wartenummer** — waiting number (take one from the machine!)
- **der Schalter** — counter"

## Section 3: At the Counter — The Golden Dialogue (2:15–4:45)

### Visual: [DIALOGUE: Split screen — officer on left, you on right. Each line appears as spoken]
### Narration: [ENERGY: teaching, then role-play energy]
"Your number is called. You sit down. Here's the GOLDEN DIALOGUE — this is almost word-for-word what happens at a Bürgeramt Anmeldung visit.

[DIALOGUE: Lines appearing one by one with audio cue]

**Sachbearbeiterin:** 'Guten Tag, bitte nehmen Sie Platz. Was kann ich für Sie tun?'
*(Please sit down. What can I do for you?)*

**You:** 'Guten Tag, ich möchte mich anmelden.'
*(Hello, I'd like to register my address.)*

**Sachbearbeiterin:** 'Haben Sie Ihren Pass dabei?'
*(Do you have your passport with you?)*

**You:** 'Ja, hier bitte.'
*(Yes, here please.)*

**Sachbearbeiterin:** 'Und die Wohnungsgeberbestätigung?'

**You:** 'Ja, hier ist sie.'

**Sachbearbeiterin:** 'Gut, bitte füllen Sie dieses Formular aus.'
*(Good, please fill out this form.)*

That's sentences 4, 5, and 6 right there:
- **'Ich möchte mich anmelden.'** — stating your purpose
- **'Ja, hier bitte.'** — handing over documents
- **'Ja, hier ist sie/er/es.'** — confirming you have something

⏸️ **Role-play time!** I'll be the officer. When I say 'Haben Sie Ihren Pass dabei?' — you say...

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

'Ja, hier bitte.' 

And when I say 'Und die Wohnungsgeberbestätigung?' — you say...

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

'Ja, hier ist sie.' 

*Note: 'sie' because Wohnungsgeberbestätigung is feminine (die). For 'der Pass' you'd say 'hier ist er'. Don't stress — 'hier bitte' works for everything.*"

## Section 4: Your Safety Net — 2 Sentences (4:45–6:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Large text — the two most important sentences, golden border]
### Narration: [ENERGY: emphatic, reassuring]
"Now — the two most important sentences in this ENTIRE video. Your safety net.

### Sentence 7: When you don't understand
**'Entschuldigung, können Sie bitte langsam sprechen?'**
*(Excuse me, can you please speak slowly?)*

### Sentence 8: When you need help with the form
**'Können Sie mir bitte helfen? Ich verstehe dieses Wort nicht.'**
*(Can you please help me? I don't understand this word.)*

*Ee randu sentence parayaan madi undenkil, Germany-yil EVERY office visit oru adventure aakum. Pakshe ee sentence paranjaal — staff HELPS you. They appreciate that you're trying.*

[EXAM-TIP] Both of these are Sprechen Teil 3 patterns — 'Können Sie bitte ___?' is the universal polite request form. Learn it for the exam AND for life.

⏸️ Say both sentences out loud. Then say them again faster.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (6 seconds)

These two sentences alone will save you in 80% of office situations. Memorize them."

## Section 5: Finishing Up — 2 Sentences (6:00–7:00)

### Visual: [DIALOGUE: Continuation of the counter scene]
### Narration: [ENERGY: calm, practical]
"The officer processes your form. You're almost done. Two final sentences:

### Sentence 9: Checking if you're done
**'Ist das alles?'**
*(Is that everything?)*

### Sentence 10: Asking about next steps
**'Wann bekomme ich die Bestätigung?'**
*(When will I get the confirmation?)*

For the Anmeldung, the answer is usually: 'Sofort' (immediately) or 'In zwei Wochen per Post' (the Steuer-ID letter).

⏸️ Quick — what do you ask to check if the visit is over? And to ask about the confirmation?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (4 seconds)

[SLIDE: 'Ist das alles?' + 'Wann bekomme ich die Bestätigung?']"

## Section 6: Document Checklist by Office (7:00–8:15)

### Visual: [DIAGRAM: 3 columns — Bürgeramt / Bank / Ausländerbehörde, each with document icons]
### Narration: [ENERGY: brisk, organized]
"Quick reference — what to bring to the three offices you'll visit first:

### Bürgeramt (Anmeldung):
Reisepass + Mietvertrag + Wohnungsgeberbestätigung + Terminbestätigung

### Bank (Kontoeröffnung):
Reisepass + Anmeldebestätigung (from Bürgeramt) + Steuer-ID (comes by post)

### Ausländerbehörde:
Reisepass + Visum + Arbeitsvertrag or Zulassungsbescheid + Krankenversicherung + Passfoto

*Golden rule: ORIGINALS + 2 copies of everything. Oru folder-il organize cheythu koNdu pokuka.*

⏸️ Which office visit is YOUR next one? Name the documents you need for it.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (5 seconds)"

## Section 8: Cultural Note + Outro (9:30–10:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "Professional ≠ Cold 🤝"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: warm, understanding]
"One cultural thing. German office workers are professional, not warm. They won't offer you chai or make small talk. That's not rudeness — it's efficiency.

*Kerala-yil 'onnu irikku, chaaya kudikku' ennokke parayum. Germany-yil business first — but that means YOUR visit is FASTER. 10 minutes in, done, out.*

Be polite, be organized, have your documents ready. They will help you.

And remember: **'Können Sie bitte langsam sprechen?'** — this one sentence transforms every office visit from stressful to manageable.
