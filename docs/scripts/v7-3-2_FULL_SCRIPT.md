## Section 2: Upper Body Basics — Hemd, Pullover, Jacke (0:50–2:30)

### Visual: [ICONS: Shirt, Sweater, Jacket, Coat. Articles highlighted.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: steady, instructional]
"Let's start from the top. Don't forget the **Articles**!

[Words appear]
- 🔊 **das Hemd** — *Shirt. Think of 'Hem' (edge) of a shirt.*
- 🔊 **der Pullover** — *Sweater. Just like English 'Pullover', but say the 'P' and 'v' (f) clearly.*
- 🔊 **die Jacke** — *Jacket. Almost same as English.*
- 🔊 **der Mantel** — *Coat. Think of a 'Mantle'.*

*Jacke (die) feminine aanu, Mantel (der) masculine aanu. Ithu orkkanam!*

⏸️ **Try: The (male) sweater?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**der Pullover.** ✅"

---

## Section 3: Lower Body & Feet — Hose, Rock, Schuhe (2:30–4:30)

### Visual: [ICONS: Trousers, Skirt, Dress, Shoes. Plural alert for 'Schuhe'.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"Moving down.

[Words appear]
- 🔊 **die Hose** — *Trousers. Note: it's singular in German! Not 'Hosen' unless you have two pairs.*
- 🔊 **der Rock** — *Skirt. (No, not a stone!)*
- 🔊 **das Kleid** — *Dress. Think of 'Clothing' (Kleidung).*
- 🔊 **die Schuhe** — *Shoes. Almost always plural.*

*Dress neutral (das) aanu, Skirt masculine (der) aanu. Grammar-il gender semantic gender-nu anusharichu aayirikkilla.*

⏸️ **Say: The trousers.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**die Hose.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: The Color Match — Adjective Endings (Definite) (4:30–6:30)

### Visual: [FORMULA: der/die/das + [adjective] + '-e' + [clothing]. Examples: das rotE Kleid, die grünE Hose.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: attentive, instructional]
"Now, let's add some color! When you say 'THE red dress' (not just 'the dress is red'), the adjective gets a tiny ending.

[The "e" rule]
After **der/die/das**, usually the adjective ends in **-e**.

🔊 **'das rote Kleid'** (the red dress)
🔊 **'die grüne Hose'** (the green trousers)
🔊 **'der blaue Mantel'** (the blue coat)

*Ithu simple aanu — 'der/die/das' kandaal adjective-il '-e' cherukkanam. Ithu 'Adjective Endings'-inte first step aanu!*

⏸️ **Try: The black shirt. (das Hemd)**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**das schwarze Hemd.** ✅"

---

## Section 5: Malayalam Logic — "Size" of Language (6:30–8:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Malayalam 'Chuvappa uduppu' vs German 'das rote Kleid'.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, structural]
"In Malayalam, adjectives like 'Chuvappa' don't change regardless of what comes after them.

In German, the adjective 'dances' with the article.
- If it's 'The red dress' → **das rote Kleid**.
- If it's 'A red dress' → **ein rotes Kleid**. (Wait, that's next level! Don't worry yet).

*Malayalam-il simple aayathu German-il kurachu 'decoration' venam. Decoration is the ending!*

[EXAM-TIP] If you're descriptive in your speaking exam (Sprechen), like saying 'Ich trage ein blaues Hemd' instead of just 'Ich trage Hemd', you get much higher marks!"

## Section 6: Speed Challenge — Wardrobe Wipeout (8:00–10:00)

### Visual: [FLASHCARDS: Article + Adj + Clothing. Student says the full phrase.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, rapid-fire]
"Rapid fire. Put it together!

⚡ die (f) + grün + Hose? → **die grüne Hose!**
⚡ das (n) + weiß + Hemd? → **das weiße Hemd!**
⚡ der (m) + schwarz + Mantel? → **der schwarze Mantel!**
⚡ die (f) + rot + Jacke? → **die rote Jacke!**
⚡ das (n) + blau + Kleid? → **das blaue Kleid!**
⚡ der (m) + braun + Rock? → **der braune Rock!**

Excellent! You're styled and ready."

### Visual: [SUMMARY SLIDE: Upper Body vs Lower Body list. The '-e' ending rule table.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: calm, closing]

3. After **der/die/das**, add **-e** to the adjective.
4. **'Schuhe'** is plural, so it's always 'die'.
