## PART A: SPRECHEN TEIL 2 — Questions from Word Cards

---

## Section 2: The Format (1:15–2:15)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Exam setup — two candidates + examiner, word cards on table]
### Narration:
"In Sprechen Teil 2, you and another candidate take turns.

You draw a word card. On the card is ONE WORD or a short phrase — like:

[Cards appear one by one]
- **Preis?**
- **Uhrzeit?**
- **Name?**
- **Adresse?**
- **Hobby?**
- **Beruf?**

Your job: turn that word into a COMPLETE QUESTION and ask your partner.

Your partner answers. Then THEY draw a card and ask YOU.

You do this 3-4 times each.

[EXAM-TIP] The examiner is NOT looking for fancy German. They want to see: can you form a question? Can you answer one? That's it."

## PART B: SPRECHEN TEIL 3 — Polite Requests

---

## Section 5: The Format (6:00–6:45)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Situation card example — "Sie sind am Bahnhof. Sie möchten eine Fahrkarte kaufen."]
### Narration:
"Teil 3 is different. Instead of a word card, you get a SITUATION card.

The card describes a scenario: 'You are at the train station. You want to buy a ticket.'

Your job: formulate a POLITE REQUEST to solve the situation.

This is practical German. This is what you'll actually DO in Germany every day. The exam is testing: can you get what you need by speaking German?"

---

## Section 6: The 6 Golden Request Patterns (6:45–8:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: building up — pattern → meaning → example situation]
### Narration:

"Six request patterns will carry you through ANY situation card. Here they are, from most common to most formal:

### Key Vocabulary Overlay:

**Pattern 1: Ich hätte gern...**
'Ich hätte gern einen Kaffee, bitte.'
*Enikkë oru coffee vēnam please — athānu ithu.*
This is THE restaurant/shop pattern. Use it when you want to order or buy something.

⏸️ **Say: 'Ich hätte gern ...' — pick any item.**

**Pattern 2: Ich möchte gern...**
'Ich möchte gern einen Termin machen.'
Slightly different from 'Ich hätte gern' — this is more about wanting to DO something, not just order an item.

⏸️ **Say: 'Ich möchte gern ...'**

**Pattern 3: Können Sie mir bitte...?**
'Können Sie mir bitte helfen?'
'Können Sie das bitte wiederholen?'
This is your HELP pattern. Use it when you need someone to do something for you.

⏸️ **Say: 'Können Sie mir bitte ...'**

**Pattern 4: Kann ich bitte... haben?**
'Kann ich bitte die Speisekarte haben?'
'Kann ich bitte ein Glas Wasser haben?'
When you want to receive something specific.

**Pattern 5: Entschuldigung, wo ist...?**
'Entschuldigung, wo ist die Toilette?'
'Entschuldigung, wo ist der Bahnhof?'
Navigation and finding things. Always start with 'Entschuldigung' — it's polite and gets attention.

**Pattern 6: Gibt es hier...?**
'Gibt es hier ein Restaurant?'
'Gibt es hier einen Geldautomaten?'
When you want to know if something EXISTS nearby.

[A1-SAFE] These six patterns cover 95% of all real-life requests at A1 level. You don't need anything fancier."

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & Rescue Strategies (10:30–11:15)

### Visual: [SLIDE: ❌ Common mistakes and ✅ Fixes]
### Narration:

"Three mistakes students make in Teil 2 and 3:

❌ **Mistake 1: Forgetting 'bitte'**
'Geben Sie mir ein Wasser' sounds rude.
✅ 'Können Sie mir bitte ein Wasser geben?' — polite!
*'Bitte' is like 'please' — NEVER forget it in a request.*

❌ **Mistake 2: Using informal 'du' with the examiner**
'Kannst du mir helfen?' → Too informal for the exam!
✅ 'Können SIE mir helfen?' → Always use 'Sie' in the exam.

❌ **Mistake 3: Panicking when you don't know a word**
You want to ask about a 'Geldautomat' but you forget the word?
✅ Describe it! 'Entschuldigung, ich brauche Geld. Wo kann ich Geld bekommen?' — the examiner will understand!

[EXAM-TIP] **The golden rule: simple and correct beats complex and broken.** 'Wo ist das?' is better than a grammatically wrong long sentence."
