## Section 2: Formal vs. Informal — Pick Your Greeting (1:00–3:30)

### Visual: [SPLIT SCREEN: 1. Wohnung (Formal) | 2. WG (Informal). Greetings appearing side by side.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: steady, instructional]
"First, who are you writing to?

[If it's a Landlord (Formal)]
1. 🔊 **'Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren,'**
   *(Dear Sir/Madam, — Note: Use this if you don't have a name).*
2. 🔊 **'Sehr geehrter Herr [Name],'** / **'Sehr geehrte Frau [Name],'**

[If it's a WG (Informal)]
1. 🔊 **'Hallo zusammen!'** / **'Liebe WG,'**

*Landlord-in-ae 'Sehr geehrte' ennu polum formal aayi ezhuthuka. WG aayi-irunnal 'Hallo' mathi.*

⏸️ **Try: Formal (Dear Sir/Madam).**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren.** ✅"

---

## Section 3: The Introduction — Who are you? (3:30–5:30)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: "Mein Name ist...", "Ich bin... Jahre alt.", "Ich komme aus...". Icons: Name tag, ID card, Flag.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"Now, the body. Tell them about yourself. Keep it short!

[Standard Intro]
- 🔊 **'Mein Name ist [Nikhil].'**
- 🔊 **'Ich bin [25] Jahre alt.'**
- 🔊 **'Ich komme aus Indien / Kerala.'**
- 🔊 **'Ich bin Student / Ich arbeite als...'**

*Ningalude details quick-aayi ezhuthaam alle? Ithu ninte profile aanu!*

⏸️ **Say: I'm 26 and I'm from Kerala.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**Ich bin sechsundzwanzig und komme aus Kerala.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: The Request — "Ich suche..." (5:30–7:30)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: "Ich suche ab sofort ein Zimmer.", "Gibt es noch ein freies Zimmer?". Counter: Ich suche... ab... (from).]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"Why are you writing?

[The Pitch]
- 🔊 **'Ich suche ab sofort ein Zimmer.'** *(I'm looking for a room starting now).*
- 🔊 **'Gibt es noch ein freies Zimmer?'** *(Is there still a free room?)*

[Wait! Grammar Check!]
- 🔊 **'es gibt'** (there is) + **Accusative** (the object).
- So, **'ein Zimmer'** (neuter) or **'eine Wohnung'** (fem).

*Accusative is important. 'Es gibt einen Tisch' masculine aayi mariyal '-en' varum!*

⏸️ **Ask: Is there a free room?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Gibt es ein freies Zimmer?** ✅"

---

## Section 5: Malayalam Logic — "Respect" (7:30–9:30)

### Visual: [TABLE: Malayalam 'Sir/Madam' vs German 'Sehr geehrte...'. Respectful tone markers.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, structural]
"In Malayalam, we have different levels of respect (Ningal vs Ningalkku).

German is the same. If you write 'Hallo' to a formal landlord, it might be seen as rude. If you write 'Sehr geehrte' to a student WG, it might be seen as too stiff!

*Culture respect-il ninnu thanne thodangunnu. Correct tone pick cheythaal ninte chances boost aakum!*

[EXAM-TIP] The **Schreiben** part of the A1 exam almost always asks for a short message. Use **'Mit freundlichen Grüßen'** for formal, and **'Viele Grüße'** for informal. This is worth points!"

---

## Section 6: The Closing — "Mit freundlichen Grüßen" (9:30–11:30)

### Visual: [HIGHLIGHT: 1. "Mit freundlichen Grüßen" (Formal) | 2. "Viele Grüße" (Informal). Signature line animation.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: attentive, instructional]
"Never just end with your name.

[Formal Closing]
🔊 **'Mit freundlichen Grüßen'**
*(Friendly regards — Use this ALWAYS for landlords or companies).*

[Informal Closing]
🔊 **'Viele Grüße'** / **'Beste Grüße'**
*(Best regards — Use for WGs or friends).*

*Signature line position simple aanu. End-il name-nu munne ezhuthaam!*

⏸️ **Try: Formal regards.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**Mit freundlichen Grüßen.** ✅"

---

## Section 7: Speed Challenge — Message Builder (11:30–13:30)

### Visual: [FLASHCARDS: Scenario (WG vs Landlord). Student picks the greeting and closing.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, rapid-fire]
"Final challenge. Form the message!

⚡ Formal Greeting? → **'Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren!'**
⚡ Intro yourself? → **'Mein Name ist...'**
⚡ Ask about room? → **'Gibt es ein Zimmer?'**
⚡ Formal Closing? → **'Mit freundlichen Grüßen!'**
⚡ Informal Greeting (WG)? → **'Hallo zusammen!'**
⚡ Informal Closing (WG)? → **'Viele Grüße!'**

Excellent! You've just sent your first application!."
