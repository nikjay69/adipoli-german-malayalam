## Script

**Narration (Malayalam/Manglish):**
Namskaram machane! Aadyathe lesson-il nammal Apotheke-yute system-ne kurichu samsarichu. Ini, practical aayittu chennal ningalkku shop-il "Common Medicines" engane vanganam ennu nokkaam.

**Topic: Greeting and Helping Phrases**
Apotheke-yile Pharmacist-ode polite aayittu request cheyyaam.

- "Können Sie mir etwas empfehlen?" (Can you recommend me something? / Enikku enthenkilum recommend cheyyaamo?)
- "Ich brauche etwas gegen ..." (I need something for/against ...)

If you have a specific ailment:
- "... Erkältung" (cold)
- "... Halsschmerzen" (sore throat)
- "... Zahnschmerzen" (toothache)

**Topic: Painkillers (Schmerzmittel)**
"Schmerzmittel" ennu parayunnathu common painkillers. 
- "Haben Sie ein Schmerzmittel?" (Do you have a painkiller?)
- "Zwei Packungen Ibuprofen, bitte." (Two packs of Ibuprofen, please).

Important Note: Paracetamol common aayirikkum, but wait, stronger ones like 600mg or 800mg Ibuprofen usually rezeptpflichtig aanu. Pharmacist mathram suggestion tharum. Simple 400mg "rezeptfrei" aayirikkum.

**Topic: Creams and Ointments**
- "die Creme" (cream) — for normal dry skin or rashes.
- "die Salbe" (ointment) — deeper treatment for pain or skin issues. "Ich brauche eine Salbe gegen Rückenschmerzen." (I need an ointment for back pain).

**Topic: Bandages and Supplies**
- "das Pflaster" (band-aid): "Ein Päckchen Pflaster, bitte." (A packet of band-aids).
- "der Verband" (bandage): "Ich brauche einen Verband für mein Bein."

**Topic: Dosage Instructions (Einnahme)**
Pharmacist-ode dosage chodhikkunnathe important aanu.

- "Wie oft soll ich das nehmen?" (How often should I take this?)
- "Wie viele Tabletten täglich?" (How many tablets daily?)

Possible Answers:
- "Zweimal täglich" (twice daily).
- "Nach dem Essen" (after meals).
- "Vor dem Schlafen" (before sleep).
- "Mit viel Wasser" (with lots of water).

**Topic: Payment and Final Interaction**
"Danke, das ist alles." (Thanks, that's all).
"Was kostet das insgesamt?" (How much is it in total?)

The Pharmacist will wish you: "Gute Besserung!" (Get well soon! / Vegam sugham aakatte!).
Ningalkku "Danke, gleichfalls" (Thanks, you too! / actually "same to you", but for wishes like 'have a nice day') ennum parayaam. But mostly just "Danke!" is enough.

**Closing:**
Common medicines and interaction phrases — ippo Apotheke-yil ningalkku confident aayittu vanganam ennu ariyaam. Stay safe!

Adutha lesson-il nammal "Emergency!" (Notfall!) patti samsarikkaam. Until then, tschüss!
