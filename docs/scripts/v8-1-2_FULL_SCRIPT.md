## Section 1: Hook — The Empty Room (0:15–0:50)

### Visual: [SCENE: An empty room. Animated furniture pieces flying in: a bed, a table, a chair. Labels: "das Bett", "der Tisch", "der Stuhl".]
### Narration: [ENERGY: high, engaging]

Today, we're learning the names of the things that make a house a HOME in German. Whether you're at IKEA or describing your room to a friend, these are the words you need. Let's start building!"

## Section 2: Sitting & Eating — Tisch, Stuhl, Sofa (0:50–2:30)

### Visual: [ICONS: Table, Chair, Sofa, Lamp. Articles highlighted: der, der, das, die.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: steady, instructional]
"First, the essentials for your living room.

[Words appear]
- 🔊 **der Tisch** — *Table. (Think of 'Dish' on a 'Tish').*
- 🔊 **der Stuhl** — *Chair. (Think of 'Tool' for sitting).*
- 🔊 **das Sofa** — *Sofa. Same word, but neuter!*
- 🔊 **die Lampe** — *Lamp. Easy!*

*Notice a pattern? Most basic furniture like Tisch and Stuhl are masculine (der). It's a good guess if you forget!*

⏸️ **Try: The (male) chair?**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**der Stuhl.** ✅"

---

## Section 3: Sleeping & Storing — Bett, Schrank, Regal (2:30–4:30)

### Visual: [ICONS: Bed, Wardrobe, Shelf. "Schrank" emphasized.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"Now for the bedroom.

[Words appear]
- 🔊 **das Bett** — *Bed. Just one letter different.*
- 🔊 **der Schrank** — *Cupboard/Wardrobe. This is a big word. Repeat: Schrank.*
- 🔊 **das Regal** — *Shelf/Bookshelf.*

*Schrank (der) is masculine, Bett (das) is neuter. Your clothes go in the Schrank, you go in the Bett!*

⏸️ **Say: The wardrobe.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)
**der Schrank.** ✅"

---

## Section 4: Kitchen Power — Kühlschrank, Herd, Spülmaschine (4:30–6:30)

### Visual: [ICONS: Fridge, Stove, Dishwasher. Compound word breakdown: Kühl+Schrank.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: building, logical]
"The kitchen has its own 'Geräte' (appliances).

[Words appear]
- 🔊 **der Kühlschrank** — *Fridge. Literally 'Cool-Cupboard'.*
- 🔊 **der Herd** — *Stove/Oven.*
- 🔊 **die Spülmaschine** — *Dishwasher. (Spülen = to rinse/wash).*

*Kühlschrank is just 'cool' + 'Schrank'. German compound words are like Lego!*

⏸️ **Try: The (cool) fridge.**
### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)
**der Kühlschrank.** ✅"

---

## Section 5: Malayalam Logic — "Set" and "Furniture" (6:30–8:00)

### Visual: [TABLE: Malayalam 'Settee', 'Almarah', 'Kattil' vs German 'Sofa', 'Schrank', 'Bett'.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: cultural, structural]
"In Kerala, we often use words like 'Settee' or 'Almarah' (which actually comes from Portuguese!).

German has its own unique set. For example, 'Schrank' covers everything from a kitchen cupboard to a bedroom wardrobe. And just like in Malayalam, where we might say 'kattil' for bed, 'Bett' is the absolute essential.

*Nammude home-ile furniture memories German-ile words-umaayi connect cheyyuka. Almarah = Schrank. Easy!*

[EXAM-TIP] If you describe your room in the **Sprechen** part, use adjectives too! 'Mein Bett ist groß' (My bed is big). It makes you sound much more fluent."

## Section 7: Summary & Bridge (10:00–11:00)

### Visual: [SUMMARY SLIDE: Furniture list by room. The 'der' furniture pattern highlighted.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: calm, closing]
"🎯 **The Furniture Checklist:**

2. **Bett, Sofa, Regal** are neuter (**das**).
3. **Lampe** is feminine (**die**).
4. Compound words like **Kühlschrank** take the gender of the LAST word.
