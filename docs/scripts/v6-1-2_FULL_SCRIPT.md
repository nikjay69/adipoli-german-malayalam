## Section 2: The Description Formula — One Sentence Is Enough (0:45–1:45)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Formula card — "Das ist + [what] + aus/mit + [key ingredient]"]
### Narration: [ENERGY: clear, empowering]
"You don't need a cooking show explanation. You need ONE formula:

[SLIDE: Formula]
🔊 **Das ist ___ aus / mit ___.**
That is ___ made from / with ___.

Examples:
- 🔊 **Das ist Reis mit Curry.** — That is rice with curry.
- 🔊 **Das ist ein Pfannkuchen aus Reismehl.** — That is a pancake from rice flour.

Two words make this formula work:
- **aus** = made from (the base ingredient)
- **mit** = with (what's added)

*Simple aanu. Recipe alla, description aanu.*

⏸️ Repeat: **Das ist Reis mit Curry.**

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)"

## Section 4: The Spice Conversation — 'Ist das scharf?' (5:00–6:20)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Spice vocabulary with heat-level scale: mild → würzig → scharf → sehr scharf]
### Narration: [ENERGY: playful, practical]
"Germans will ask one question more than any other:

🔊 **Ist das scharf?** — Is that spicy?

Your answers:

[SLIDE: Three response options]
- 🔊 **Nein, das ist mild.** — No, that's mild.
- 🔊 **Ja, ein bisschen scharf.** — Yes, a little spicy.
- 🔊 **Ja, sehr scharf!** — Yes, very spicy!

Honest tip: what is 'mild' for us is often 'scharf' for Germans. Adjust your scale! 😄

*Nammude 'normal spicy' avarde 'very spicy' aanu. Be kind.*

Two more useful words:
- **würzig** — flavourful / well-seasoned (positive!)
- **süß** — sweet

🔊 **Das ist würzig, aber nicht scharf.** — That's flavourful, but not spicy.

This sentence is your diplomatic weapon.

⏸️ How do you say 'Is that spicy?' ... **Ist das scharf?** ✅

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)"

## Section 5: Dietary Preferences — What You Eat and Don't Eat (6:20–7:40)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Dietary preference cards with icons]
### Narration: [ENERGY: serious but supportive]
"In Germany, people ask about food restrictions early. This is normal and polite. Be ready.

### The core sentences:

🔊 **Ich bin Vegetarier.** (male) / **Ich bin Vegetarierin.** (female)
I'm vegetarian.

🔊 **Ich esse kein Schweinefleisch.**
I don't eat pork.

🔊 **Ich esse kein Rindfleisch.**
I don't eat beef.

🔊 **Ich bin vegan.**
I'm vegan.

🔊 **Ich habe eine Allergie gegen Nüsse.**
I have a nut allergy.

[SLIDE: Key food words]
- **das Schweinefleisch** — pork
- **das Rindfleisch** — beef
- **das Hähnchen** — chicken
- **die Nüsse** — nuts

*Religious food preferences are completely normal in Germany. Germans ask, you answer, nobody judges. Be direct.*

⏸️ How do you say 'I don't eat pork'? ... **Ich esse kein Schweinefleisch.** ✅

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

[EXAM-TIP] In Sprechen, if they ask about Essen and you mention a restriction, that's a perfectly normal, complete A1 answer. Don't overthink it."

## Section 6: Mini Dialogue — WG Dinner (7:40–9:00)

### Visual: [DIALOGUE scene: Kitchen in a shared flat. Speech bubbles appear step by step.]
### Narration: [ENERGY: applied, confidence-building]
"Let's put everything together. You're at a WG dinner — your flatmates are cooking together.

---

**Anna:** Was kochst du heute?
*(What are you cooking today?)*

**You:** Ich koche Biryani. Das ist Reis mit Gewürzen und Hähnchen.
*(I'm cooking biryani. That's rice with spices and chicken.)*

**Anna:** Oh, lecker! Ist das scharf?
*(Oh, yummy! Is that spicy?)*

**You:** Ein bisschen. Aber ich mache es mild für dich.
*(A little. But I'll make it mild for you.)*

**Anna:** Super! Ich esse gern Reis. Aber ich esse kein Schweinefleisch.
*(Great! I like eating rice. But I don't eat pork.)*

**You:** Kein Problem. Das ist Hähnchen.
*(No problem. That's chicken.)*

**Anna:** Perfekt. Danke!

---

Short. Natural. Real.

⏸️ Now you say the 'You' lines. Try all three.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (8 seconds)

Notice: you used the description formula, the spice answer, AND a dietary clarification. Three skills in one conversation."
