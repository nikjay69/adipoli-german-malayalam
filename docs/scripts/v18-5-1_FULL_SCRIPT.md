### Scene 3: How Teil 3 Works (1:00–2:00)
**[SLIDE: Exam format diagram]**

**Narrator:**
> "Here's exactly what happens:"
>
> "1. The examiner gives you a situation card — for example: 'Sie sind im Deutschkurs. Sie verstehen ein Wort nicht.'"
>
> "2. You read the situation and make an appropriate polite request."
>
> "3. Your partner responds naturally."
>
> "4. Then your partner gets a card, and YOU respond."
>
> "Total time: about 2 minutes. Usually 2–3 situation cards each."
>
> "Scoring: They check if you can make a clear, polite request. Not perfection — communication!"

### Scene 4: The Three Magic Request Phrases (2:00–3:30)
**[SLIDE: Three key patterns — highlighted in color]**

**Narrator:**
> "You need THREE phrases. That's it. These cover 90% of Teil 3 situations."
>
> 🔊 **Pattern 1:** "Können Sie mir bitte...?" — Can you please...?
> "Können Sie mir bitte helfen?" — Can you please help me?
>
> 🔊 **Pattern 2:** "Könnten Sie bitte...?" — Could you please...? (more polite)
> "Könnten Sie bitte langsamer sprechen?" — Could you please speak more slowly?
>
> 🔊 **Pattern 3:** "Darf ich...?" — May I...?
> "Darf ich das Fenster öffnen?" — May I open the window?
>
> ⏸️ **[Repeat each pattern twice]**
>
> *"Moonu phrases mathi — Können Sie, Könnten Sie, Darf ich. Ithu manasilaakkiyal Teil 3 done!"*

### Scene 5: The Three Response Patterns (3:30–4:30)
**[SLIDE: Response templates]**

**Narrator:**
> "When your partner makes a request, you respond. Three safe patterns:"
>
> 🔊 **Yes, of course:** "Ja, natürlich!" / "Ja, gern!" / "Ja, klar!"
>
> 🔊 **Yes with addition:** "Ja, natürlich! Was brauchen Sie?" / "Ja, bitte! Hier ist er."
>
> 🔊 **Polite no:** "Tut mir leid, das geht leider nicht." / "Leider nicht, aber..."
>
> "IMPORTANT: In the exam, mostly say YES. It keeps the conversation flowing and shows you can interact."
>
> ⏸️ **[Practice: Someone asks 'Können Sie mir bitte helfen?' — respond!]**

### Scene 7: Politeness Boosters (7:30–8:30)
**[SLIDE: Politeness word list]**

**Narrator:**
> "Small words make a BIG difference in Teil 3:"
>
> 🔊 "bitte" — please (ALWAYS use it)
> 🔊 "Entschuldigung" — excuse me (great opener)
> 🔊 "danke" / "vielen Dank" — thank you
> 🔊 "gern" — gladly (perfect for responses)
> 🔊 "natürlich" — of course
> 🔊 "leider" — unfortunately (for polite refusals)
>
> "TIP: Start with 'Entschuldigung', include 'bitte', end with 'Danke'. That's your politeness sandwich!"
>
> *"Entschuldigung + bitte + Danke = full marks sandwich! Easy aanu!"*

---

### Scene 8: Common Mistakes to Avoid (8:30–9:15)
**[SLIDE: ❌ vs ✅ examples]**

**Narrator:**
> "Avoid these common traps:"
>
> ❌ "Gib mir den Stift!" — Too aggressive! This isn't how you ask.
> ✅ "Darf ich den Stift bitte benutzen?"
>
> ❌ Saying nothing because you forgot the word.
> ✅ Use a simpler version: "Können Sie mir bitte helfen?"
>
> ❌ Only saying "Ja" when someone asks you something.
> ✅ "Ja, natürlich! Kein Problem!"
>
> "Remember: the examiners want to see INTERACTION. Short is fine, rude is not."
