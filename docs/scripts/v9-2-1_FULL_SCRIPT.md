### Scene 3: The Magic Sentence (1:30–3:00)
**[HIGHLIGHT: "Eine Fahrkarte nach Berlin, bitte." — large, bold]**

**Narrator:**
> "This is the MOST important sentence in this entire lesson. Ready?"
>
> "'Eine Fahrkarte nach Berlin, bitte.'"
>
> ⏸️ **[Repeat: 'Eine Fahrkarte nach Berlin, bitte.']**
>
> "That's it. That ONE sentence buys any ticket. Let me break it down:"
>
> **[Text highlights word by word]**
> "Eine Fahrkarte — one ticket. 'Fahr' = travel, 'Karte' = card. Travel card = ticket."
> "nach Berlin — to Berlin. Replace the city and you go ANYWHERE."
> "bitte — please. Always say bitte!"
>
> "Want to go to Munich? 'Eine Fahrkarte nach München, bitte.'"
> "Hamburg? 'Eine Fahrkarte nach Hamburg, bitte.'"
> "Frankfurt? Same pattern!"
>
> ⏸️ **[Say the sentence for YOUR dream destination in Germany]**
>
> "This is like 'oru ticket [place name]-kku' — but in German."

### Scene 4: One-Way vs Round Trip (3:00–4:30)
**[SLIDE: einfach ↔ hin und zurück comparison]**

**Narrator:**
> "The counter person will ask: 'Einfach oder hin und zurück?'"
>
> "einfach — one way. Literally 'simple'. One direction only."
> "hin und zurück — there AND back. Round trip."
>
> "'Oru vazhikku mathiyo, back-um veno?' — that's the question!"
>
> ⏸️ **[Repeat: 'Hin und zurück, bitte.']**
> ⏸️ **[Repeat: 'Einfach, bitte.']**
>
> "Easy! If someone asks and you don't understand, just say: 'Hin und zurück' — round trip is almost always the better deal anyway."

---

### Scene 5: First or Second Class (4:30–5:30)
**[SLIDE: erste Klasse vs zweite Klasse]**

**Narrator:**
> "Next question: 'Erste oder zweite Klasse?'"
>
> "Erste Klasse — first class. More legroom, quieter, more expensive."
> "Zweite Klasse — second class. Normal seats. Most people use this."
>
> "'AC coach vs general compartment pole think chey!' — except both are comfortable in Germany. 😄"
>
> ⏸️ **[Repeat: 'Zweite Klasse, bitte.']**
>
> "99% of the time you want zweite Klasse. Unless someone else is paying! 😄"

### Scene 6: Asking for Discounts (5:30–6:30)
**[SLIDE: "Gibt es eine Ermäßigung?" highlighted]**

**Narrator:**
> "ALWAYS ask this question: 'Gibt es eine Ermäßigung?'"
>
> "Ermäßigung = discount. Student discounts, BahnCard discounts, group discounts — they exist, but you have to ASK."
>
> "'Gibt es eine Ermäßigung für Studenten?' — Is there a student discount?"
>
> ⏸️ **[Repeat: 'Gibt es eine Ermäßigung?']**
>
> "If you have a BahnCard: 'Ich habe eine BahnCard 25.' That gets you 25% off!"
>
> "Tip: India-yilum discount chodikkum alle? Germany-yilum chodikkuka — but politely. No bargaining though! Fixed prices."

### Scene 7: Reading the Departure Board (6:30–8:00)
**[SLIDE: Annotated Fahrplan / departure board]**

**Narrator:**
> "Before you board, you need to READ the departure board. Let's decode it."
>
> **[Board appears with labels highlighting one by one]**
>
> "die Abfahrt — departure. This is the YELLOW board at the station."
> "die Ankunft — arrival. This is the WHITE board."
> "das Gleis — platform. 'Gleis 7' = Platform 7."
> "die Uhrzeit — time. In 24-hour format! 14:30 = 2:30 PM."
> "die Verspätung — delay. If you see this... welcome to Deutsche Bahn reality! 😄"
> "das Ziel — destination. Where the train is going."
>
> ⏸️ **[Look at the board: Which platform does the train to Hamburg leave from? What time?]**
>
> "In Kerala, train delays are expected. In Germany, people get VERY upset about delays. 'Die Bahn hat Verspätung' is practically a national joke!"

### Scene 8: The Golden Ticket Dialogue (8:00–10:00)
**[DIALOGUE: Full Schalter scene]**

**Narrator:**
> "Now the complete dialogue. This is your GOLDEN TICKET DIALOGUE — memorize this and you can buy any ticket in Germany."

**[Dialogue appears line by line with role labels]**

> **Reisender:** "Guten Tag! Eine Fahrkarte nach Hamburg, bitte."
> **Beamter:** "Einfach oder hin und zurück?"
> **Reisender:** "Hin und zurück, bitte."
> **Beamter:** "Erste oder zweite Klasse?"
> **Reisender:** "Zweite Klasse. Gibt es eine Ermäßigung für Studenten?"
> **Beamter:** "Ja, mit Studentenausweis bekommen Sie 25 Prozent Rabatt."
> **Reisender:** "Super! Kann ich mit Karte zahlen?"
> **Beamter:** "Natürlich. Das macht fünfundvierzig Euro. Ihr Zug fährt um vierzehn Uhr dreißig auf Gleis sieben."
> **Reisender:** "Vielen Dank!"
> **Beamter:** "Gute Reise!"

⏸️ **[Read through once more. Then try playing the 'Reisender' role while I play the 'Beamter'.]**

**Narrator:**
> "Let's unpack the key phrases:"
> "'Das macht fünfundvierzig Euro.' — That comes to 45 euros. 'Das macht...' is how Germans say the total."
> "'Gute Reise!' — Have a good trip! A nice way to end."
>
> "Notice: the counter person gave you THREE important pieces of info: price (45€), time (14:30), and platform (Gleis 7). In the Hören exam, these are EXACTLY the details they test!"

### Scene 10: The 'nach' Rule (10:30–11:30)
**[SLIDE: nach vs in die]**

**Narrator:**
> "One grammar tip about 'nach'."
>
> "For most cities and countries WITHOUT articles: nach."
> "'Nach Berlin. Nach München. Nach Indien. Nach Deutschland.'"
>
> "For countries WITH articles: in die / in den / in die."
> "'In die Schweiz.' 'In die Türkei.' 'In den Iran.' 'In die USA.'"
>
> "How do you know which countries have articles? Short answer: most don't. The exceptions are die Schweiz, die Türkei, die USA, der Iran, and a few others. If in doubt, use 'nach' — Germans will understand you."
>
> ⏸️ **[Repeat: 'Eine Fahrkarte nach München.' 'Ich fahre in die Schweiz.']**
