## Section 2: Teil 1 — Signs & Notices (0:45–3:00)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Lesen Teil 1 format — sign image + richtig/falsch question]
### Narration: [ENERGY: teaching, clear]
"Teil 1: Five signs or short notices — each with one richtig/falsch question.

**The rule: Read the QUESTION first. Then find the answer in the sign.**

Don't read the sign top to bottom. Hunt for what the question asks.

Let's try it:

[SLIDE: Doctor's office sign]
```
Praxis Dr. Müller
Sprechzeiten:
Mo–Fr  8:00–12:00
Mi     15:00–18:00
Samstag: geschlossen
```

**Question:** 'Die Praxis ist am Samstag geöffnet.' — Richtig oder Falsch?

⏸️ What's your answer? Don't overthink — just scan for 'Samstag'.

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**Falsch!** Samstag → geschlossen. You didn't need to know 'Sprechzeiten' means consultation hours. You just needed: Samstag + geschlossen = not open.

*Bus stop-il board vaayikkumbol ningal ella word-um vaayikkaarilla — 'Kochi', 'platform 3', 'delayed' — key words scan cheyyum. German signs-um athu pole thanne!*

Let's do one more:

[SLIDE: Supermarket offer]
```
SONDERANGEBOT!
Bio-Milch  nur 0,99€
Gültig bis 15. März
Max. 2 pro Person
```

**Question:** 'Man kann 5 Flaschen Milch kaufen.' — Richtig oder Falsch?

⏸️ Scan for a number...

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**Falsch!** 'Max. 2 pro Person' — numbers in the sign directly contradict the question's number. Number questions? Check numbers first.

[EXAM-TIP] Teil 1 strategy: Read question → find keyword in sign → match or mismatch = your answer. Takes 30 seconds per question."

## Section 3: Teil 2 — Match Texts to Headings (3:00–5:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Teil 2 format — 5 short texts, 8 headings (3 are traps)]
### Narration: [ENERGY: methodical, strategic]
"Teil 2: Five short texts — ads, event notices, snippets. Eight headings to choose from. THREE headings are extras that don't match anything. Traps.

**The technique: Topic keywords — not translation.**

Here are the 5 topic families that cover 90% of Teil 2:

[SLIDE: Topic keyword families appearing one by one]

| Topic | Spot These Words |
|-------|-----------------|
| 🏠 Housing | Wohnung, Zimmer, Miete, ab sofort |
| 💼 Jobs | Stelle, Arbeit, Bewerbung |
| 📚 Courses | Kurs, lernen, Anfänger, Anmeldung |
| 🎵 Events | Konzert, Fest, Uhr, Eintritt |
| 🛋️ For Sale | verkaufen, gebraucht, günstig, Preis |

*Malayalam-il header vaayichumbol 'veedu', 'joli', 'course' enna words kaNDaal topic manasilaakamaallo? German-ilum SAME — topic words nokkiyal heading select cheyyaam.*

⏸️ **Try this one.** Read the text and match it to a topic:

[SLIDE: Short text]
```
Schöne 2-Zimmer-Wohnung in Neukölln.
60 m², Balkon, ab 1. April frei.
Miete: 650€ warm.
```

Which topic family? Housing, Jobs, Courses, Events, or For Sale?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**Housing!** Zimmer, Wohnung, Miete — three housing keywords. Heading: 'Wohnung zu vermieten'. Easy.

Now — **the elimination trick:** After each match, CROSS OFF the heading. The pool shrinks from 8 → 7 → 6 → 5 → 4. By the last text, you only have 4 options. Better odds.

⏸️ If you've matched 4 texts and the last heading options are 'Konzert am Wochenende' and 'Gebrauchte Möbel' — and the last text mentions 'Eintritt' and 'Samstag' — which heading?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**Konzert am Wochenende.** Eintritt (admission) + Samstag (weekend) = event."

## Section 4: Teil 3 — Emails & Longer Texts (5:30–7:45)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Teil 3 format — email text + 5 richtig/falsch questions]
### Narration: [ENERGY: careful, precise]
"Teil 3: One longer text — usually an email or short letter. Five richtig/falsch questions. This is the hardest Lesen part.

**The technique: Question-first reading.** Same as Teil 1, but for a longer text.

1. Read question 1
2. Scan the text for the relevant sentence
3. Answer
4. Next question

DON'T read the whole email first. Go question by question.

[SLIDE: Email example]
```
Liebe Anna,

ich bin seit zwei Wochen in meiner neuen Wohnung
in Hamburg. Die Wohnung ist klein, aber gemütlich.
Am Samstag mache ich eine Einweihungsparty.
Komm bitte um 18 Uhr. Es gibt Essen und Musik.

Liebe Grüße,
Sara
```

⏸️ **Question 1:** 'Sara wohnt jetzt in Berlin.' — Scan for the city name...

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

'in Hamburg' — not Berlin. **Falsch.**

⏸️ **Question 2:** 'Die Wohnung ist groß.' — Scan for the description...

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

'klein, aber gemütlich' — small, not big. **Falsch.**

⏸️ **Question 3:** 'Die Party beginnt um 20 Uhr.' — Scan for the time...

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (2 seconds)

'um 18 Uhr' — not 20. **Falsch.** Numbers mismatch again — always check the numbers.

*Nokkiyoo — oru question-nu oru sentence! Full email vaayikkaaNam ennilla. Question vaayikku, text-il SCAN cheyyuka, answer edukkuka. Athre ullu.*"

## Section 5: The 4 Traps (7:45–8:45)

### Visual: [SLIDE: "4 Lesen Traps ⚠️" — each appearing with an example]
### Narration: [ENERGY: alert, warning]
"Four traps the exam uses against you:

**Trap 1: Hidden negation.** Text says 'Sara kann NICHT kommen.' Question says 'Sara kommt zur Party.' → Falsch! Watch for 'nicht', 'kein', 'nie'.

**Trap 2: City/name swap.** Text says 'Hamburg', question says 'Berlin'. Both cities — but different! Don't assume.

**Trap 3: Time/day switch.** 'Am Freitag' in text, 'Am Samstag' in question. One day off = falsch.

**Trap 4: Extra headings (Teil 2).** Three headings match nothing. Don't force a fit — if it doesn't match clearly, it's probably a trap heading.

⏸️ **Trap check:** A text says 'Die Bibliothek ist am Montag geschlossen.' The question says 'Am Montag ist die Bibliothek geöffnet.' What trap is this?

### Practice Pause: ⏸️ (3 seconds)

**Trap 1 + Trap 3 combined!** 'geschlossen' vs 'geöffnet' — hidden negation through opposites. Always check: does the text say the SAME thing as the question, or the opposite?"
