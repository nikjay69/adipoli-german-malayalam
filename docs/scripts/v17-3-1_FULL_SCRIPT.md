## Section 2: Lesen Teil 1 — Signs & Short Notices (1:30–3:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Lesen Teil 1 structure]
### Narration:

"Teil 1: You see 5 short texts — signs, notices, labels, ads. Each has a richtig/falsch question."

"These are REAL German signs. Train stations, supermarkets, doctors' offices, apartment buildings."

**[EXAM-TIP] The scanning technique:**
"Don't read the whole sign. Read the QUESTION first. Then find the answer in the sign."

### Visual: [NOTICE/AD: Doctor's office sign]
```
Praxis Dr. Müller
Sprechzeiten:
Mo–Fr  8:00–12:00
Mi     15:00–18:00
Samstag: geschlossen
```

Question: "Die Praxis ist am Samstag geöffnet." — Richtig oder Falsch?

⏸️ "You don't need to know 'Sprechzeiten' means consultation hours. You just need: Samstag → geschlossen → FALSCH."

🎯 **EXAM TIP:** Look for the keyword from the question IN the text. Match or mismatch = your answer.

### Visual: [NOTICE/AD: Supermarket sign]
```
SONDERANGEBOT!
Bio-Milch  nur 0,99€
Gültig bis 15. März
Max. 2 pro Person
```

Question: "Man kann 5 Flaschen Milch kaufen." — Richtig oder Falsch?

⏸️ "Key phrase: 'Max. 2 pro Person' — so 5 is FALSCH."

"*Numbers aanu hint — number questions-il number nokkiyal mathi!*"

## Section 3: Lesen Teil 2 — Matching Texts to Headings (3:30–5:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Lesen Teil 2 structure]
### Narration:

"Teil 2: You get 5 short texts (newspaper snippets, online ads, event notices) and 8 headings. Match each text to the correct heading. 3 headings are extra — traps!"

**[EXAM-TIP] The elimination method:**
1. Read ALL headings first. Understand the topic of each.
2. Read text 1 → find the matching heading → cross it off.
3. Continue. The pool shrinks each time!

### Visual: [SLIDE: Example headings]
```
a) Wohnung zu vermieten
b) Deutschkurs für Anfänger
c) Stellenangebote im Krankenhaus
d) Konzert am Wochenende
e) Gebrauchte Möbel zu verkaufen
```

### Visual: [NOTICE/AD: Short text]
```
Schöne 2-Zimmer-Wohnung in Neukölln.
60 m², Balkon, ab 1. April frei.
Miete: 650€ warm.
Kontakt: wohnung@berlin.de
```

⏸️ "Which heading matches? ... (a) Wohnung zu vermieten!"

"How did I know? Zimmer, Wohnung, Miete — all apartment words. I didn't need to understand every detail."

**[A1-SAFE] High-frequency topic keywords for Teil 2:**
| Topic | Keywords |
|-------|----------|
| Housing | Wohnung, Zimmer, Miete, ab sofort |
| Jobs | Stelle, Arbeit, Bewerbung, Krankenhaus |
| Courses | Kurs, lernen, Anfänger, Anmeldung |
| Events | Konzert, Fest, Uhr, Eintritt |
| For sale | verkaufen, gebraucht, günstig, Preis |

"*Ee keywords arinjaal — heading match cheyyan easy!*"

## Section 4: Lesen Teil 3 — Emails & Short Texts (5:30–7:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: Lesen Teil 3 structure]
### Narration:

"Teil 3: One longer text — usually an email, letter, or blog post. 5 richtig/falsch questions."

"This is the hardest Lesen part because the text is longer. But the strategy is the same: DON'T read everything first. Go question by question."

**[EXAM-TIP] Question-first reading:**
1. Read question 1.
2. Scan the text for the relevant sentence.
3. Answer.
4. Move to question 2.

### Visual: [SLIDE: Example email]
```
Liebe Anna,

wie geht es dir? Ich bin seit zwei Wochen in meiner neuen Wohnung 
in Hamburg. Die Wohnung ist klein, aber gemütlich. Ich habe ein 
Zimmer, eine Küche und ein Bad. Am Samstag mache ich eine kleine 
Einweihungsparty. Kannst du kommen? Es gibt Essen und Musik. 
Komm bitte um 18 Uhr.

Liebe Grüße,
Sara
```

Questions:
1. "Sara wohnt jetzt in Berlin." → Scan → "in Hamburg" → **Falsch**
2. "Die Wohnung ist groß." → Scan → "klein, aber gemütlich" → **Falsch**
3. "Sara macht am Samstag eine Party." → Scan → "Am Samstag mache ich eine... Party" → **Richtig**
4. "Die Party beginnt um 20 Uhr." → Scan → "um 18 Uhr" → **Falsch**
5. "Es gibt Essen bei der Party." → Scan → "Es gibt Essen und Musik" → **Richtig**

⏸️ "See how each question maps to one sentence? You don't need to understand the whole email."

"*Oru question-nu oru sentence — athre ullu! Full email vayikkanda.*"

## Section 5: Common Lesen Traps (7:30–8:30)

### Visual: [SLIDE: 4 Lesen Traps]
### Narration:

**Trap 1: Negation hiding**
"The text says 'nicht mehr' or 'kein' — the question says 'richtig' about the positive. Watch for negation!"

**Trap 2: Similar but different**
"The text says 'München', the question says 'Berlin'. Both are cities — but they're different! Don't assume."

**Trap 3: Time confusion**
"'Am Freitag' in the text, 'Am Samstag' in the question. One day difference = falsch."

**Trap 4: Extra headings in Teil 2**
"3 headings don't match anything. Don't force a match if it doesn't fit. Move on and come back."

🎯 **EXAM TIP:** If you're unsure, skip the question and come back. Don't waste 3 minutes on one question.

### Visual: [SLIDE: 3 Takeaways]
🎯 **3 Things to Remember:**

2. Keywords > full comprehension — match topic words, numbers, names.
3. Don't spend more than 2 minutes on any single question — skip and return.
