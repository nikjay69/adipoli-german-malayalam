### Narration:
"The Goethe A1 exam has 4 sections. Let me walk you through the big picture.

[HIGHLIGHT: Hören]
Section 1: HÖREN — Listening. 20 minutes. Worth 25 points. You listen to audio recordings and answer questions.

[HIGHLIGHT: Lesen]
Section 2: LESEN — Reading. 25 minutes. Worth 25 points. You read texts and answer questions.

[HIGHLIGHT: Schreiben]
Section 3: SCHREIBEN — Writing. 20 minutes. Worth 25 points. You fill out a form AND write a short message.

These three sections happen TOGETHER in one session. Total: about 65 minutes.

Then you get a BREAK.

[HIGHLIGHT: Sprechen]
Section 4: SPRECHEN — Speaking. About 15 minutes. Worth 25 points. You speak with an examiner and another candidate.

Total exam: 4 sections, 100 points. You need 60 points to pass — that's 60%.

[EXAM-TIP] You can compensate between sections! If you score 50% in one section but 70% in others, you can still pass. But try to score well in ALL sections.

*100-il 60 — athra difficult alla. Nammukku kazhiyum!*"

## Section 3: Hören — Listening (3:00–5:00)
### Visual: [SLIDE: Hören breakdown with 3 parts]
| Part | What You Hear | What You Do | Items | Points |
|------|--------------|-------------|-------|--------|
| Teil 1 | Short messages, announcements | Richtig oder Falsch | 6 items | ~8 pts |
| Teil 2 | Short dialogues | Choose a/b/c | 4 items | ~8 pts |
| Teil 3 | Longer announcements | Match situations | 5 items | ~9 pts |

### Narration:
"Let's break down HÖREN — the listening section.

You'll hear audio recordings. Each recording plays TWICE. You answer questions while listening.

Teil 1: Short messages. Maybe a phone message, a train announcement, or someone leaving a voicemail. You decide: Richtig oder Falsch? True or false?
6 items. About 8 points.

Teil 2: Short dialogues between two people. A conversation at a shop, doctor, or office. You listen and pick the right answer from a, b, or c.
4 items. About 8 points.

Teil 3: Longer announcements. A museum tour, a weather forecast, a public service announcement. You match situations to descriptions.
5 items. About 9 points.

⏸️ Remember: everything plays TWICE! Use the first listen to get the general idea. Use the second listen to confirm your answer.

STRATEGY — THE GOLDEN RULE FOR HÖREN:

[EXAM-TIP] Read the questions BEFORE the audio plays! You have a few seconds before each recording starts. READ THE QUESTION. Identify the KEY WORDS you need to listen for. Then when the audio plays, your brain is already searching for the right information.

*Ithinte importance enthaanennal — nammale SURPRISE cheyyaanulla chance exam-inu kodukkanda!*

Time math: 20 minutes for about 15 items. That's more than 1 minute per item, including the two listens. Don't panic. You have time."

## Section 4: Lesen — Reading (5:00–7:00)
### Visual: [SLIDE: Lesen breakdown with 3 parts]
| Part | What You Read | What You Do | Items | Points |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------|--------|
| Teil 1 | Notices, signs, short texts | Match to situations | 5 items | ~8 pts |
| Teil 2 | Ads (Anzeigen) | Richtig oder Falsch | 5 items | ~8 pts |
| Teil 3 | Short emails/messages | Choose a/b/c | 5 items | ~9 pts |

### Narration:
"LESEN — the reading section. 25 minutes.

Teil 1: Short texts — notices on a bulletin board, signs in a supermarket, a message on a door. You match each text to a situation. 'Frau Müller sucht eine Wohnung' — which notice matches?
⏸️

Teil 2: Advertisements. Apartment ads, course ads, event flyers. Richtig oder Falsch — is the statement true based on the ad?

Remember Module 8? Wohnungsanzeigen? *Athokke ivide use aavum!*

Teil 3: Short emails or letters. Someone writes to a friend, a teacher sends a message. You read and answer multiple choice.

STRATEGY:

[EXAM-TIP] DON'T read the text first! Read the QUESTION first. Then scan the text for the answer. This saves MASSIVE time.

For Teil 2 — ads: underline KEY DETAILS in the ad (price, time, location). Then check each statement against your underlined details. Cross out wrong answers. The right one becomes obvious.

Time math: 25 minutes for 15 items = about 1.5 minutes per item. Start with Teil 1 (easiest). Move to Teil 3 (emails are usually clear). Save Teil 2 (ads) for last if you're running low on time, because ads require careful reading.

Abbreviation reminder — you'll need these:
Mo/Di/Mi/Do/Fr/Sa/So = weekdays
PLZ = Postleitzahl, Str. = Straße, Tel. = Telefon
KM, WM, NK, EBK = apartment ad abbreviations from Module 8!

*Abbreviations arinjillenkil marks pokunnu. Arinjaal free marks kittunnu!*"

## Section 5: Schreiben — Writing (7:00–9:00)
### Visual: [SLIDE: Schreiben breakdown with 2 parts]
| Part | What You Write | Time Suggestion | Points |
|------|---------------|----------------|--------|
| Teil 1 | Fill out a form (5 fields) | 5 minutes | ~12-13 pts |
| Teil 2 | Write a short message (30 words, 3 points) | 15 minutes | ~12-13 pts |

### Narration:
"SCHREIBEN — writing. 20 minutes. This is where Modules 2, 11, 14, and 18 come together!

Teil 1: Fill out a form. 5 fields. You already know this from Module 14!

The 5 Golden Fields: Familienname, Vorname, Geburtsdatum, Wohnort, Kontakt. Maybe also Beruf, Familienstand, or Staatsangehörigkeit.

⏸️ Quick test: What date format does Germany use? ... TT.MM.JJJJ! Day first, then month, then year. With DOTS, not slashes!

Spend about 5 minutes on Teil 1. Check your answers TWICE. These are FREE points — don't lose them!

Teil 2: Write a short message — about 30 words. You get a situation and 3 content points. You MUST hit all 3 points.

[SLIDE: Example Schreiben Teil 2 prompt]
```
You can't come to German class tomorrow.
Write to your teacher:
- Why you can't come
- When you will come back  
- Ask about homework
```

Your answer template:
```
Liebe Frau Müller,

ich kann morgen leider nicht zum Kurs kommen,
weil ich krank bin. Ich komme am Mittwoch 
wieder. Können Sie mir bitte die Hausaufgaben 
schicken?

Viele Grüße
Nikhil
```

Count: ✅ Point 1 (reason: krank), ✅ Point 2 (Mittwoch zurück), ✅ Point 3 (Hausaufgaben). 30 words. PERFECT.

[EXAM-TIP] The GOLDEN RULE of Schreiben Teil 2: greeting + 3 sentences (one per content point) + closing. That's the formula. Simple and correct ALWAYS beats fancy and wrong. If you don't know a word, use a simpler one.

*30 words — athu 3-4 sentences maathram aanu. WhatsApp message-il koode ezhuthum nammale!*

STRATEGY: Write Teil 1 first (5 minutes). Then Teil 2 (15 minutes). Don't spend too long thinking — just use the template from Module 18."

## Section 6: Sprechen — Speaking (9:00–10:30)
### Visual: [SLIDE: Sprechen breakdown with 3 parts]
| Part | What You Do | Time | Points |
|------|-----------|------|--------|
| Teil 1 | Introduce yourself (6 topics) | ~2 min | ~8 pts |
| Teil 2 | Ask questions from word cards | ~2 min | ~8 pts |
| Teil 3 | Make polite requests from situation cards | ~2 min | ~9 pts |

### Narration:
"SPRECHEN — speaking. This happens separately, usually in the afternoon or on a different day.

You'll be with ONE other candidate and ONE or TWO examiners. Don't worry — they're friendly!

Teil 1: Sich vorstellen — introduce yourself. Remember the Golden Self-Introduction from Module 2 and 18?

🔊 'Hallo! Ich heiße Nikhil. Ich bin 28 Jahre alt. Ich komme aus Kerala in Indien. Ich wohne jetzt in Berlin. Ich spreche Malayalam, Englisch und ein bisschen Deutsch. Ich bin IT-Spezialist von Beruf. In meiner Freizeit lese ich gern. Danke!'

That's about 2 minutes. You've practiced this. It should feel automatic by now.

⏸️ Can you say YOUR version right now? Try it! 2 minutes!

Teil 2: Wort-Karten — word cards. You get a card with a word like 'Kino?' or 'Preis?' and you must form a question.

'Kino?' → 'Wann beginnt der Film im Kino?'
'Preis?' → 'Wie viel kostet das?'

Teil 3: Alltags-Situationen — everyday situation cards. You must make a polite request.

'You want to buy a train ticket' → 'Eine Fahrkarte nach München, bitte.' or 'Ich hätte gern eine Fahrkarte nach München.'

[EXAM-TIP] Start every request with 'Entschuldigung' and include 'bitte'. Politeness = extra points!

*Exam-il shy aakkaruthu! Confident aayi samaarikkuka — mistake vannaaalum continue cheyyuka. Examiners ninne encourage cheyyaan try cheyyum.*

If you blank or make a mistake — DON'T STOP. Just continue or rephrase. The examiners are not trying to fail you!"

## Section 7: Scoring & Passing (10:30–11:15)
### Visual: [TABLE: Scoring breakdown]
| Section | Max Points | You Need |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Hören | 25 | ~15 (60%) |
| Lesen | 25 | ~15 (60%) |
| Schreiben | 25 | ~15 (60%) |
| Sprechen | 25 | ~15 (60%) |
| **TOTAL** | **100** | **60 (60%)** |

### Narration:
"Let's talk numbers.

Total: 100 points. You need 60 to pass. That's 60%.

Each section is worth 25 points. You CAN compensate across sections — if you score 20 in Lesen but only 12 in Hören, you're still at 32 out of 50 for those two. The total matters.

But aim for at least 15 in each section. That's your safety margin.

Where are the EASIEST points?

1. Schreiben Teil 1 — form filling. You KNOW this. 12-13 practically free points.
2. Sprechen Teil 1 — self-introduction. You've memorized this. Easy 6-8 points.
3. Lesen — reading takes time but the answers ARE in the text. Read carefully = good score.

Where do most people lose points?
1. Hören — listening is the hardest because you can't control the speed.
2. Sprechen Teil 2+3 — thinking on the spot is stressful.

So: Secure your easy points FIRST, then fight for the harder ones."
