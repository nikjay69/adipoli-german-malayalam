## [36:00] Vocab Block 5 — The German Alphabet & Spelling
[VISUAL: Animated alphabet on screen; Kuttan points to letters]

**EN:** Something that will save you countless times: the German alphabet.

**EN:** The German alphabet looks like English, but the sounds are different.

**DE:** The tricky ones. Pay attention here.

[VOCAB CARD: buchstabieren — to spell — അക്ഷരങ്ങൾ പറയുക]

**DE:** buchstabieren

**EN:** to spell — Your best friend at offices and the consulate.

**EN:** J in German is NOT "Jay." It is "Yot" — like the letter Y.

**EN:** So if your name has a J, you say Yot. Rahul's friend Jyothi? That's Yot-tee.

**EN:** V in German is NOT "Vee." It is "Fow."

**EN:** So Vikram becomes Fik-ram.

**EN:** W in German is NOT "Double-U." It is "Veh" — like English V.

**EN:** So William becomes Vill-yam.

**EN:** Z in German is NOT "Zee." It is "Tsett."

**EN:** Zahir becomes Tss-air.

*Kuttan (മലയാളം):* German letters separate aanu — ippozhu fix cheyyo!

**DE:** The complete alphabet: A — Ah, B — Beh, C — Tse, D — Deh, E — Eh, F — Ef, G — Geh, H — Hah, I — Ee, J — Yot, K — Kah, L — El, M — Em, N — Enn, O — Oh, P — Peh, Q — Koo, R — Er, S — Es, T — Tay, U — Ooh, V — Fow, W — Veh, X — Iks, Y — Üpsilon, Z — Tsett.

**EN:** Special letters: Ä — Ah-Umlaut, Ö — Oh-Umlaut, Ü — Uh-Umlaut, ß — Eszett (ss sound).
