## [04:00] Schreiben Teil 1 — Formulare ausfüllen
[VISUAL: Kuttan at a desk, a registration form visible in front of him. He taps the form with a pen.]

This is where the exam gets interesting, machane. Schreiben Teil 1 is the EASIEST section of the entire exam. Why? Because you're not really writing — you're copying. Yes, copying! But you need to copy correctly, with the right format. Many students underestimate this part and lose easy marks. Don't be that person. Treat this section with respect, and those five points are yours.

**DE:** Schreiben Teil 1 ist der einfachste Teil.
**EN:** Writing Part 1 is the easiest part.

Let me explain exactly what happens. You'll receive a short text about a person — maybe a registration for a language course, a library membership, or a hotel check-in. Your job is to fill in a German form using the information from that text. Five fields, five answers. Simple? Yes. But with traps for those who don't pay attention. The text might say "Am 7. November 2001" but the form asks for "Geburtsdatum." If you write "7. November 2001" instead of "07.11.2001," you might lose a point for format. These small details matter.

**DE:** Bitte füllen Sie das Formular aus.
**EN:** Please fill out the form.

*Kuttan (മലയാളം):* ഇവിടെ തെറ്റാകരുത്, ഓരോ ഫീൽഡും ശ്രദ്ധിക്കണം!

The golden rule here is simple: read the LABEL, then find the INFO, then copy it EXACTLY. Don't try to translate or rephrase — just copy what you see. Let me show you the most common form fields you'll encounter.

So if the country is "Kerala" or "Indien," the nationality is "indisch." If the country is "Deutschland," the nationality is "deutsch." Remember this rule.

## [09:30] Schreiben Teil 2 — Kurze Nachrichten
[VISUAL: Kuttan at a desk, a blank page and a pen. He looks focused.]

Now we enter the more interesting territory, machane. Schreiben Teil 2 is where you actually WRITE, but don't worry — it's only about 30 words. The exam will give you a situation, and you need to write an email or message that covers THREE content points, called Inhaltspunkte. Each point must be addressed clearly. Miss one, and you lose marks. But cover all three, and you're on your way to full marks! This is like cooking a simple dish with three ingredients — miss one, and the recipe fails.

**DE:** Schreiben Sie eine kurze Nachricht.
**EN:** Write a short message.

**DE:** Drei Inhaltspunkte — drei Sätze!
**EN:** Three content points — three sentences!

*Kuttan (മലയാളം):* ഓരോ പോയിന്റിനും ഒരു വാക്യം — ഇതാ സൂത്രം!

Let me show you the structure for an informal email to a friend. You start with "Lieber Tom," — that's for males. Or "Liebe Anna," — that's for females. Notice the difference in the ending: "Lieber" ends with "er" (like "Mann"), and "Liebe" ends with "e" (like "Frau"). Then you address each of your three points in separate sentences. One point equals one sentence. Finally, close with "Viele Grüße," followed by your name. Simple, clean, done!

For a formal email to a teacher or boss, the greeting is different: "Sehr geehrte Frau Müller," — this never changes for formal situations. Always use "Sehr geehrte" with a capital S and G, and always include the person's title if you know it. Then address your three points formally, and close with "Mit freundlichen Grüßen,"

*Kuttan (മലയാളം):* ഔപചാരികമായാൽ "Sehr geehrte" — മറക്കാതെ!

---

[VOCAB CARD: sich entschuldigen — to apologize — മാപ്പ് പറയുക]
[VOCAB CARD: die Einladung — the invitation — ക്ഷണം]
[VOCAB CARD: die Nachricht — the message — സന്ദേശം]
[VOCAB CARD: der Inhaltspunkt — the content point — ഉള്ളടക്ക പോയിന്റ്]
[VOCAB CARD: der Grund — the reason — കാരണം]
[VOCAB CARD: die Hausaufgaben — the homework — വീട്ടുപറമ്പ്]

---

Here is a real exam situation, machane. Pay close attention to how I structure my answer!

**DE:** Situation: Sie können morgen nicht zum Deutschkurs kommen. Schreiben Sie eine Nachricht an Ihre Lehrerin, Frau Müller.
**EN:** Situation: You cannot come to German class tomorrow. Write a message to your teacher, Frau Müller.

**DE:** Inhaltspunkte:
**EN:** Content points:

1. Sich entschuldigen — Apologize
2. Grund nennen — Give a reason (sick)
3. Nach Hausaufgaben fragen — Ask for homework

**DE:** Meine Nachricht:
Liebe Frau Müller,
es tut mir leid, ich kann morgen leider nicht zum Kurs kommen. Ich bin krank. Können Sie mir bitte die Hausaufgaben schicken?
Viele Grüße,
[Your Name]

**EN:** Dear Mrs. Müller,
I'm sorry, unfortunately I cannot come to class tomorrow. I am sick. Can you please send me the homework?
Best regards,
[Your Name]

*Kuttan (മലയാളം):* ആഹാ! എല്ലാ 3 പോയിന്റും കൃത്യമായി എഴുതി — ഇതാ അഡിപോളി!

Point one: "es tut mir leid" — that's how we apologize in German. Point two: "Ich bin krank" — the reason. Point three: "Können Sie mir bitte die Hausaufgaben schicken?" — asking for homework. Three points, three sentences, perfect structure! Also notice I used "Liebe" because Frau Müller is a woman, and "Viele Grüße" because this is a semi-formal situation — a teacher, but someone you know well.

Let me show you another example, this time an informal one to a friend.

**DE:** Situation: Ihr Freund Max hat Geburtstag. Schreiben Sie eine Nachricht an Max.
**EN:** Situation: Your friend Max has a birthday. Write a message to Max.

**DE:** Inhaltspunkte:
**EN:** Content points:

1. Gratulieren — Congratulate
2. Einladung anbieten — Offer an invitation (to dinner)
3. Nachfrage — Ask when he's free

**DE:** Meine Nachricht:
Lieber Max,
herzlichen Glückwunsch zum Geburtstag! Ich möchte dich zum Abendessen einladen. Wann hast du Zeit?
Viele Grüße,
[Your Name]

**EN:** Dear Max,
congratulations on your birthday! I would like to invite you to dinner. When do you have time?
Best regards,
[Your Name]

See the difference? "Lieber Max" because he's male, and the tone is casual. "Herzlichen Glückwunsch" is the standard birthday greeting. "Ich möchte dich einladen" offers the invitation. And "Wann hast du Zeit?" asks about availability. Again, three points, three sentences, perfect!

Now we move to the SPEAKING section, machane. This is where your voice becomes the star! Sprechen Teil 1 is your grand entry. The examiners will say: "Bitte stellen Sie sich vor." And you need to introduce yourself covering seven points. Think of it as telling the examiner who you are in exactly seven sentences. This is your first impression — make it count!

**DE:** Bitte stellen Sie sich vor.
**EN:** Please introduce yourself.

*Kuttan (മലയാളം):* ഇവിടെ നിന്ന് തന്നെ ആരംഭിക്കുന്നു — സെൽഫ് ഇൻട്രൊഡക്ഷൻ!

Let me tell you something important, machane. Before the speaking exam even starts, while you're waiting outside the room, take a deep breath. Visualize yourself introducing yourself confidently. [VOCAB CARD: sich vorstellen — to introduce oneself — സ്വയം പരിചയപ്പെടുത്തുക]

Here are the seven points you must cover in Sprechen Teil 1 — memorize these like your morning tea routine:

**DE:** Ich heiße... — Name
**EN:** My name is... — Name

**DE:** Ich bin... Jahre alt. — Age
**EN:** I am... years old. — Age

**DE:** Ich komme aus... — Country
**EN:** I come from... — Country

**DE:** Ich wohne in... — City
**EN:** I live in... — City

**DE:** Ich spreche... — Languages
**EN:** I speak... — Languages

**DE:** Ich bin Student / Ich arbeite als... — Profession
**EN:** I am a student / I work as... — Profession

**DE:** Meine Hobbys sind... — Hobbies
**EN:** My hobbies are... — Hobbies

*Kuttan (മലയാളം):* ഈ 7 കാര്യങ്ങൾ ഓരോന്നായി പറഞ്ഞാൽ മതി — സിംപിൾ ആൻഡ് ക്ലിയർ!

Let me give you a full example, machane. Imagine the examiner asks: "Stellen Sie sich vor, bitte." Take a breath, smile, and begin:

**DE:** Ich heiße Kuttan. Ich bin 25 Jahre alt. Ich komme aus Indien, aus Kerala. Ich wohne in Thrissur. Ich spreche Malayalam, Englisch und ein bisschen Deutsch. Ich bin Student. Meine Hobbys sind Cricket und Kochen.
**EN:** My name is Kuttan. I am 25 years old. I come from India, from Kerala. I live in Thrissur. I speak Malayalam, English, and a little German. I am a student. My hobbies are cricket and cooking.

Notice something important? I said "bin" for age, not "habe". In German, we ARE our age, we don't HAVE it. "Ich bin 25 Jahre alt" — not "Ich habe 25 Jahre." This is a very common mistake for Malayalam speakers! In Malayalam, we say "Ende anju aayu" which translates literally to "I have twenty-five years," but in German, we ARE our age. Remember this: age uses "sein" (to be), not "haben" (to have).

Also notice I said "ein bisschen Deutsch" — a little German. You don't have to say "fließend" — fluent. "Ein bisschen" is honest and perfectly acceptable at A1 level! The examiners appreciate honesty and accuracy over exaggerated claims of fluency.

Here's another example, this time for a woman:

**DE:** Ich heiße Anjali. Ich bin 28 Jahre alt. Ich komme aus Indien, aus Tamil Nadu. Ich wohne in München. Ich spreche Tamil, Englisch und ein bisschen Deutsch. Ich arbeite als Ärztin. Meine Hobbys sind Lesen und Musik.
**EN:** My name is Anjali. I am 28 years old. I come from India, from Tamil Nadu. I live in Munich. I speak Tamil, English, and a little German. I work as a doctor. My hobbies are reading and music.

See how I used "ärztin" with a small "ä"? That's the feminine form. If a man says "Ich arbeite als Arzt" — doctor (male). A woman says "Ich arbeite als Ärztin" — doctor (female). German changes many profession words depending on whether the speaker is male or female. Always use the correct form!

*Kuttan (മലയാളം):* ജോലിയുടെ പേര് സ്ത്രീ/പുരുഷ ഭേദം കാണിക്കുന്നു — ശ്രദ്ധിക്കുക!

## [24:00] Sprechen Teil 2 — Fragen stellen und beantworten
[VISUAL: Kuttan sitting across from an invisible partner. He gestures to show conversation.]

Now for the interaction part, machane. Sprechen Teil 2 is a card game — but a German speaking card game! You and your partner take turns. You draw a Wortkarte with a topic, and you must ask a question related to that topic. Then your partner answers, and they ask YOU a question. It's a back-and-forth dialogue. Think of it like a friendly chat about everyday topics — nothing scary, just natural conversation.

**DE:** Stellen Sie eine Frage und beantworten Sie eine Frage.
**EN:** Ask a question and answer a question.

*Kuttan (മലയാളം):* ഇവിടെ ഡൈലോഗ് ആണ് വേണ്ടത് — കൗണ്ടർ സർഫേസും!

---

[VOCAB CARD: die Frage stellen — to ask a question — ചോദ്യം ചോദിക്കുക]
[VOCAB CARD: beantworten — to answer — ഉത്തരം പറയുക]
[VOCAB CARD: das Thema — the topic — വിഷയം]
[VOCAB CARD: wiederholen — to repeat — ആവർത്തിക്കുക]
[VOCAB CARD: die Antwort — the answer — ഉത്തരം]
[VOCAB CARD: verstehen — to understand — മനസ്സിലാക്കുക]

---

Here are common topics for Sprechen Teil 2 — these come up often, so prepare for each one:

- Essen — Food
- Reisen — Travel
- Familie — Family
- Einkaufen — Shopping
- Freizeit — Free time
- Arbeit — Work
- Schule — School
- Urlaub — Vacation

For each topic, you'll ask either a W-question like "Was...?", "Wo...?", "Wann...?", "Warum...?" or a Ja/Nein question like "Haben Sie...?" or "Gehen Sie gern...?" But here's the golden rule, machane: NEVER just say "Ja" or "Nein". Always add a detail! This shows the examiner you can hold a conversation, not just answer with one word. Think of it like making a full sentence instead of a one-word reply.

**DE:** Haben Sie Geschwister?
**EN:** Do you have siblings?

**DE:** Ja, ich habe zwei Schwestern.
**EN:** Yes, I have two sisters.

See? Not just "Ja". Full sentence, extra detail. That earns more marks. The examiner wants to hear that you can communicate, not just pass a test.

Let me show you question templates for each type:

**W-questions:**
- Was machen Sie gern in Ihrer Freizeit?
- Wo wohnen Sie?
- Wann lernen Sie Deutsch?
- Warum lernen Sie Deutsch?

**Ja/Nein questions:**
- Essen Sie gern Curry?
- Gehen Sie gern ins Kino?
- Arbeiten Sie gern mit Menschen?
- Haben Sie Geschwister?

*Kuttan (മലയാളം):* ചോദ്യം ചോദിച്ചാൽ ഉത്തരം പൂർണമായി പറയണം!
